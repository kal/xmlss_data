monument_desc An arbour or root-house located in the grounds of Wick House, Brislington. The arbour appears to have been set within a band of trees immediately to the east of Wick Cottage, on the crest of the valley overlooking Brislington Brook. It was in existence by the mid-1820s and was recorded by a drawing by T. L. Rowbotham of c.1826-1827. The arbour was a circular open-sided structure, approximately 3 metres in diameter, with a conical thatched roof resting on arches supported by columns with vegetation trained over them (Stoddard 1981, 24). The nature of its construction is therefore unclear. The structure survived into the early twentieth century but was subsequently demolished.

monument_desc A pair of entrance lodges to the Bristol Zoological Gardens on the south side of Clifton Down were in existence by 1849 when they were surveyed by Ashmead for a plan of Clifton. The lodges were probably built in 1835-1836 or soon after the gardens opened in 1836 as they are shown on Richard Forrest's plan for the layout of the gardens probably drawn in 1835. Originally two lodges either side of the entrance to the gardens, since 1995 the lodges have been linked by a shop extension to the side and rear. The lodges are single storey limestone ashlar structures with a cruciform plan built in the neoclassical style. They have slate hipped and gabled roof with overhanging eaves.
The structures have a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/34/747).

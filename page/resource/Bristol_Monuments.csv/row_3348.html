monument_desc Statue of Alfred Fagon, located in the grassed area at the junction of Ashley Road and Grosvenor Road. Fagon (1937-1986) was a Jamaican-born playwright, poet and actor and was one of the major Black British playwrights of the 1970s and 1980s. Fagon's notebooks and scripts were deposited with the V & A Theatre Museum in 2000 (accession number RF2000/690). The statue was commissioned by the Friends of Fagon Committee and is a bronze bust sculpted by David G. Mutasa. The work stands on a plinth, made of polished granite slabs, which is approximately 1.2 metres high and 0.5 metres square. The north-east face of the plinth bears the legend:
"ALFRED FAGON/1937-1986/POET PLAYWRIGHT ACTOR/"THROUGH GRIEF TO HAPPINESS/ERECTED/ON THE FIRST ANNIVERSARY/OF HIS DEATH,/29 AUGUST 1987,/BY THE FRIENDS OF FAGON"
The work was unveiled on 29 August 1987.
[Public Monument and Sculpture Association reference number BR52].

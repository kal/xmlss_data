monument_desc Prince Street Bridge, located at the south end of Prince Street and carrying the highway across the Floating Harbour to Wapping. The bridge was constructed in the later eighteenth century and was a wooden structure with a slightly arched deck which was supported on ribs and had handrails and posts along each side. It was originally a toll bridge with a pair of toll houses on either side of the southern approach. These buildings were square in plan with shallow pyramidal roofs with overhanging eaves and central, decorative chimneys. There was an entrance with a round-headed arch towards road and two windows within round-headed frames to the south. There were pedestrian gates either side of the central vehicle gate (BRSMG M2926).
The bridge was replaced in the nineteenth century.

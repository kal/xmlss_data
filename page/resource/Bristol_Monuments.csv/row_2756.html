monument_desc No.21 High Street.. The property was bequeathed to the Bristol Guardians of the Poor by John Knight, of St. Andrew, Holborn, Esquire, by will dated 19 April 1706, when it was described as "The George" (but not a licensed house), in the occupation of William Bush, Linen Draper. At the beginning of the nineteenth century it was occupied by Messrs. Barry & Son, Booksellers.
The building was inspected in 1914 by John Pritchard (Pritchard 1920, 134-135). No description of the front. Behind the then-modern shop was a low ceilinged room of irregular shape, about 27 feet 6 inches. by 16 feet 6 inches., "a relic of Jacobean days, if not earlier", with panelled walls and a "typical" mantel-piece. On the frieze were the initials T.A.D., but no date. The room led to a "lofty apartment", 25 feet by 16 feet, with a carved wood mantel-piece "of a century later". At the extreme rear was a small store room with a trace of seventeenth century moulded plasterwork.

monument_desc Hospital for Infectious Diseases, located on the east side of Holmes Hill Road. The hospital was in existence by the time of the Ordnance Survey second edition 1:2500-scale County series plan (Gloucestershire Sheet LXXII.14) published in 1904. The hospital appears to have been created by St. George's parish by converting two existing attached buildings in the late 1880s. The form of the building is not at present clear but it measured 10 metres square.
In 1897, when the county of Bristol was enlarged, the hospital was transferred to the authority of Bristol. A report by the Medical Officer of Health, dated 1898, reports that "the cottages in rear have been pulled down, the premises repaired and a bath room fitted. At present retained for use as a refuge under Sec. 15 of the Infectious Diseases Prevention Act, 1890, or for emergency use" (quoted in Erskine 2001). The hospital was latterly used as an out-patient centre and later was used as a "Sanitary Yard". The building was still extant at the end of the First World War.
The site of the hospital is now in the gardens to the rear of Nos.28-30a Holmes Hill Road.

monument_desc Society of Friends' burial ground, Brislington. The burial ground was sited on the south side of Bath Road, to the east of the junction with Stockwood Road. It was founded c.1691, the land being held on a one thousand year lease, and was originally surrounded by a wall. It was sold to Dr. Francis Fox in 1836 with the condition that "it shall be kept in good order and repair as a Private Burial Ground, and that Dr. Fox shall enter into a covenant to allow Burial there to Members of the Society of Friends should further occasion arise" (quoted in Stock 1997, 2). The boundary wall of the burial ground was crenellated. In the early 1970s, the area was paved over with the gravestones remaining in situ.
The inhumations apparently remain undisturbed within the burial ground, although neither grave markers nor the boundary wall survive (ibid.). A watching brief in 1998 during development of the adjacent car showroom (SMR 20373) noted what may have been part of the boundary wall of the cemetery. This was of limestone rubble construction and was exposed in a trial pit at a depth of 1.1 metres below the ground surface (Pilkington 1998).

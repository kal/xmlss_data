monument_desc Albion Dry Dock, Gasferry Road. The dock was in existence by the early nineteenth century and is shown as a graving dock on the Ordnance Survey 1st edition map of 1883. It was part of Hilhouse's Shipyard but the yard was bought by Charles Hill & Sons in the latter part of the nineteenth century and was renamed the Albion Dockyard. The dock was extended in the twentieth century.
The dock is approximately 165 metres long and 7.5 metres deep and appears to have cut into the rockhead at least at its southern end. A cast iron caisson closes the northern end of the dock. The walls are constructed of blocks of sandstone as is the floor of the northern part of the dock. The walls on the east and west sides are constructed in a series of five ledges or galleries which vary in size but are roughly 0.5 metres wide by 1.5 metres high.
The dock was the subject of a photographic survey in 1998 [BUAD 3344] in advance of the infilling of its southern end. It was noted that a cannon of probable late eighteenth or early nineteenth century date had been set into the ground at the east side of the dock (NGR ST 5775 7239 approx) to act as a mooring post.

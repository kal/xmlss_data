monument_desc The Gatehouse of St. Augustine's Abbey, located on the south side of College Green and to the west of the abbey church (now Bristol Cathedral). The gatehouse was built between 1140 and 1170, and formed the main entrance to the abbey precinct.
The gatehouse is a three-storey structure of oolitic limestone ashlar with a basement. The semi-circular arched gateway at ground-floor is of Norman date and is supported on twisted columns. It has complex incised geometric decoration of beaded chevrons on the stonework of the arches and vaulting (including contemporary carved bosses). The form of the upper part of the gatehouse as built in the twelfth century is not currently known. The major part of the gatehouse structure was reconstructed in the fifteenth century and is in a Perpendicular Gothic style, with an oriel window on the main, north-facing, elevation. There are niches on either side of the elevation at first- and second-floor level containing statues. Those at first floor represent abbots while the statues at second-floor are possibly of kings. All of the statues appear to date to the fourteenth- or fifteenth-century. The present roof was constructed in the late fifteenth- to early sixteenth-century (Arnold et al. 2003; Anon 2004, 90). Internally there is a winder stair at the south-east corner of the building. The blind arcade at ground floor was painted with a red and yellow colour scheme during the late-middle ages, as was the statuary on the exterior of the building. There are also masons' marks on the stone of the arcade (L. Bainbridge pers. comm.).
The upper part of the structure was restored and altered in the early 1880s under the supervision of the architect John Loughborough Pearson, who designed Truro Cathedral and was also architect to Lincoln Cathedral. This work involved repairs to the stonework of the building, including repositioning and reuse of some carved medieval stonework.
A photogrammetric survey of the exterior of the building was made by Warwick Rodwell in 2003-2004 (BUAD 4151).
The gateway has a Grade I listing (Listed Building number 901-1/15/73).

monument_desc The Old Post Office, No.48 Corn Street, at the corner of Corn Street and Exchange Avenue. The building was designed by Samuel Glascodine probably with the intention of complementing the Exchange [BUAD 337M] and forming a balancing pair with No.56 Corn Street [BUAD 336M]. It was constructed c.1746. The building is of three storeys plus attic and is constructed of limestone ashlar with lateral ashlar stacks and a hipped roof. The symmetrical front has a three-window range and has a pedimented centre set forward, plinth, rusticated quoins, cornice and parapet. The ground-floor windows are semicircular arches with glazing dating to the twentieth century and central glazed double doors. There are pediments to first-floor windows and a wide dormer. The left-hand return is a 11-window range: right-hand 3-window range as the front, with OLD POST OFFICE inscribed in the plat band; to the left 4:1:3 windows, with a raised entrance section set back containing a tall doorway with Gibbs surround, a pedimented first-floor window and a semicircular-arched second-floor window beneath a cartouche with festoon. Each side are windows as the front. The interior is open plan with a central stairwell, largely remodelled late twentieth century; the side doorway, in Exchange Avenue, leads to an open-well stair. At the beginning of the twentieth century the rear part of the Old Post office was rebuilt to designs by Sir George Oatley, extending the building south to take in the site of the northern two shops of a row of four probably designed by R. S. Pope c.1848 (BUAD 1394M). The building was part rebuilt as a facsimile in 1993. It is currently in use as offices.
The building has a Grade I listing (Listed Building number 901-1/16/569).

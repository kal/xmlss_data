monument_desc Frome Gate, a gate on the second town wall circuit by Frome Bridge. Frome Gate was a double gate, probably with one gate structure at either end of the bridge. William Worcestre describes it as "two gates with an iron chain, built upon an arch and two piers" and measured the distance between the gates as approximately 12.8 metres (Neale 2000, 70-71). In the sixteenth century the Bristol Corporation ran a knitting school for poor children in the room over the gate. It formed part of the defences of the city during the civil war of 1642-1646 when two cannon, recorded as sakers in 1643 (Roy 1975, 261), were sited there. During the trial of the Parliamentarian governor of Bristol for surrendering the city after it had been stormed it was reported that a new portcullis had been installed in the gate, and that when Royalist troops had reached the western bank of St. Augustine's Reach, "Maides and Women, in the face of the Enemy, made a Barracado and Bulwark against it 15 or sixteen foot thick with earth and sacks of Wooll, to keep them from entring there" (Prynne & Walker 1644, 64).

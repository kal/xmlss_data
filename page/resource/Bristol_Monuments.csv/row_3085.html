monument_desc Cemetery, located on the north side of Church Street, St. Philips. The cemetery was probably private and was not recorded by the 1828 survey of Bristol by Plumley and Ashmead, suggesting that it was established after this date. It was however recorded by Ashmead's survey of 1854 and the plot measured approximately 40 metres (along the Church Street frontage) by 37 metres. The entrance appears to have been off Clarence Place. The cemetery was one of those closed under the 1854 Act and therefore was in use for interment for only around twenty-five years at most. By the time of the Ordnance Survey first edition plan (Bristol Sheet LXXII.13.17) surveyed in 1883 the site had been partly redeveloped, with housing on Church Street, smaller buildings (possibly also housing) along its northern edge and a large building project towards the middle of the cemetery from its east side.
The site was redeveloped again in the second half of the twentieth century and now lies mainly beneath Nos.2-24 Hassell Drive and Hassell Drive itself.

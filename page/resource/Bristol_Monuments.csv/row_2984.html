monument_desc Bath house for Arno's Court (SMR 8467). The building was located on the north side of the Bath Road (now beneath the east side of Junction Road), opposite the main house and was apparently connected to it by a tunnel. It was built for William Reeve, a Bristol brassmaker, and was octagonal in plan with a long thirteen-bay colonnade, orientated east-west, on the south side. The room containing the great bath was decorated with Bristol scenes. The estate was sold after Reeve's bankruptcy. By 1912 the bath house was in use as an antiques showroom. It later came into the possession of the Bristol tramways Company and was allowed to decay. Walter Ison visited and described the building in the late 1940s or early 1950s (SMR 20209; Ison 1952, 185-186), but no repair work was undertaken. Ultimately, in 1965, it was demolished, and the colonnade removed to Portmeirion in Wales.

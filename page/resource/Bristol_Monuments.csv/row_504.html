monument_desc The church of St Mary Redcliffe. The church appears to have been constructed in the later twelfth century and served as a chapel of ease to St. John's Church, Bedminster.
The upper stages of the spire toppled into the nave during a storm in the fifteenth century, probably in the winter of 1446, and it remained truncated for several centuries.
In 1468 William Canynges created a perpetual chantry in the chapel of St. George and installed Thomas Hallkesock as the chaplain (Berkeley Muniments A2/17/38).
A heavy tenor bell of approximately 39 cwt was cast by Purdue in 1622 (it was removed in 1903).
The Canynges Society was founded in 1848 to raise funds for restoration works to the church. The spire was rebuilt in the 1870s with funding from a public subscription organised by the Canynges Society, and the reconstruction was completed in 1872. The Society was then dissolved but it was reconstituted in 1972.
In 1910 with Messrs. Harrison and Harrison, of Durham and London, rebuilt the organ. The new organ occupied the same position on each side of the chancel as the previous one but because of restricted space, the console was detached and sited just west of the chancel arch on the north side of the nave.
The church has a Grade I listing (Listed Building number 901-1/42/1687) while the churchyard wall and the head of the Redcliff pipe is listed Grade II (Listed Building number 901-1/42/1682).

monument_desc Manor House, located on the south side of York Place. The building had been constructed by 1738 when a lease to Jane Phippen, widow of Whitchurch Phippen the younger, recorded it as newly erected by Whitchurch Phippen. Some of the original internal features survive at first and second floor level.
The house was occupied by the anthropologist John Beddoe until 1891. The building then became a school for preparing gentlemen for the colonial civil service examination and was substantially altered in 1891. Most of the present internal woodwork dates to that phase of works.
In the 1890s the house became an establishment preparing young gentlemen for colonial civil service, army and navy entrance. The school closed in 1915, and for the remainder of the Fisrst World War the building provided temporary accomodation for Red Maids' School while the school buildings were used as a Red Cross Hospital.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/9/887).

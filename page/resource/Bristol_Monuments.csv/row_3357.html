monument_desc A fishpond on the lower slopes of Ashley Hill. The pond is recorded on the 1st edition Ordnance Survey plan of 1881 (Bristol Sheet LXXII.9.12) as a fishpond and is similar in form of a duck decoy pond, though smaller in size than is usual for such a pond. Its surface area in 1881 was just over 820 square metres and the two southern limbs extended south towards a channel carrying water away from Ashleyvale Mill, 150 metres to the north-east, perhaps to create a flow into and out of the pond. There was a small island, presumably for waterfowl, in the centre which was reached by a bridge on the north-west side.
The pond has been infilled and now lies at the northern end of St. Werburghs City Farm.

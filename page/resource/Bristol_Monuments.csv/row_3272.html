monument_desc Two storey coach house to the rear of no.16 Vyvyan Terrace, Clifton fronting Vyvyan Road. The structure, consists of roughly coarsed, roughly hewn or unhewn stone, bonded with lime mortar. The majority of the stonework is Dolomitic Conglomerate with occasional pennant sandstone, some Bath stone ashlars and Bath stone quoins at first floor level in the south, elevation.
The south elevation has two ground floor openings; an eastern segmented arched wooden doorway opening out on wrought iron hinges and a western folding double door beneath a reinforced steel joist and a centrally located, low, single wooden door at first floor.
On the northern elevation facing the house and garden is a single ground floor opening in pointed gothic arch style, four blind pointed arches, two on each floor, a single wooden door at first floor level above the ground floor entrance and a single six pane sash window to the east.
Machine made bricks forming the gables in the east and west elevations indicate twentieth century alterations to the roof structure.
Internally the ground floor is divided into two rooms; the western room flagged with pennant slabs, the eastern room a mixture of flagstones and cobbles. In the southern half of the west wall were the remains of a single blocked fireplace. Metal fixings in wooden shelving in the western room possibly for hanging bridles and saddles. The partial remains of wooden stalling for a horse were recorded in the east room.

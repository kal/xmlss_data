monument_desc 114, St Michael's Hill, also known as Cogent House and formerly Rupert House. Situated on the east side of St Michael's Hill and south side of Myrtle Road. Built in the late eighteenth century, the building is extant and comprises a three-storey three window range constructed of rendered limestone rubble with limestone dressings and a pantile single-pile roof.
Originally a two storey structure the third floor was added in the late nineteenth century as evidenced by surviving stone copping along the north side of the building above first-floor window level.
Internally, very few pre-twentieth century features survive. The eighteenth century staircase survives, though much altered and the original newel post has been removed. In the hall is a metal fanlight with later coloured glass, an eighteenth century arch with reeded pilasters, a further three-centered arch and a reeded cornice to the rear hall and a late Georgian (now) half-glazed door to the rear.
The building was first depicted on Donne's map of Bristol, printed in 1826. The 1:500 Ordnance Survey plan of 1885 shows that the building had been extended to the rear and a square bay added to the rear of the original building..
After the Second World War the building became a Nurses' Home and was subdivided into flats.
The building is Listed Grade II (Listed Building number 901-1/4/274).

A pair of shops of late eighteenth- or early nineteenth-century date, located on the north side of St. Georges Road, a short distance to the east of St. Georges Hall. The pair were probably built as houses and were of three storeys and attic. Rubble construction, with brick at the top; rendered. Attached on the east side. Parapets. Each a 1-window range with shopfront of twentieth-century date. Cellar flaps below the shop windows.
The buildings were demolished in the later twentieth century.

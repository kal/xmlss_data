monument_desc St. Nicholas' Market or The Shambles, located to the south and east of The Exchange. The markets were created in the 1740s as part of the development of The Exchange, moving The Shambles from their historic position on the eastern side of High Street.
The markets had several discrete elements and their original spatial organisation and form is recorded in plans and drawings dating between c.1782 (BUAD 3805) and c.1813. The Somersetshire Market was located on the site of the Market Chambers building on the west side of Exchange Avenue. The Gloucestershire Market was on the south arcade of The Exchange. The main area of the market between All Saints' Lane, Exchange Avenue and St. Nicholas Street, recorded by O'Neill in 1823, consisted of eight parallel rows of stalls, running roughly north-south and containing 150 stalls. Each row was sheltered by a long hipped roof with slate. G. W. Braikenridge (Braikenridge Notes, C100) records that gates were erected at the ends of the standings. Removal of the old market 'houses' commenced on 4 January 1813.
The present structure dates to the nineteenth century open plan aisled extension to an earlier market, and faces St. Nicholas Street. Single storey; 7-window range. Trapezium-shaped with curved corners. The St. Nicholas Street front has a tall central entrance with iron gates, windows each side, and 2 lunettes at each end. Matching 7-window right return and 4-window left return. Inside, square piers support a 20th.century steel-trussed roof; wooden stalls.
St. Nicholas Market has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/16/591) while the Market Chambers has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/16/666).

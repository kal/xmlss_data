monument_desc Winash, Brislington. In 1740, John Braikenridge is recorded as purchasing an estate that comprised the house now called Winash from the Hall family. On his death in 1764 the house and estate passed to his wife and eventually to their grandson, George Weare Braikenridge, who resided there for seven years until 1823 when he purchased and moved to Broomwell House. He is recorded as the owner in 1828 and although the house was let at various times, for most of its history it was inhabited by the Braikenridge family. The Brislington Tithe award of 1846, gives George Braikenridge as the owner with Thomas Kenn as the occupier. It remained in the Braikenridge family until 1907.
The house comprised three storeys.
In 1918 Thomas Ford was the owner and ran a market garden from the premises, but in 1920 Victor Marryat is recorded as the owner and following his death in 1927, his widow continued to live there. The house stood empty for many years and was demolished in the early 1960s. Dixon Road now covers the site of the house and garden..

monument_desc No.90 Redcliff Street, located on the western side of Redcliff Street. The building was a terraced house of medieval date.
The north wall of was recorded during the excavation of No.91 Redcliff Street by R. H. Jones in 1983 (BUAD 447). This section of wall aligned east-west with plain walling at the east end, beyond which was an arcade of three arches (1000A). At least the westernmost two were visible on both sides of the wall. The arches consisted of Pennant Sandstone voussoirs only - there was no decorative stonework. On the northern face, between the springings for the second and third arches, was a carved head, interpreted as a corbel. Excavation down the north side of the arcade was carried out, showing that the pier between the first two arches continued for more than a metre below the modern ground level.

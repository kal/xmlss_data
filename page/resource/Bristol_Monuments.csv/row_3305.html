monument_desc Brush and Bellows Factory, located on Peter Street near St Peter's Church. Depicted on Ordnance Survey 1:500 plan 1882. On Ordnance Survey map referred to as Brush and Bellows Manufactory. E. Brison & Co, wholesale brush and bellows makers, moved to 24 and 25 Peter Street in 1878 and remained there until 1910. The company was previously located on Redcliff Street. According to the 1820 Mathews Directory (the earliest Directory available to the author), John Hunt, Brusthmaker, was located at 56 Redclif Street until 1835. In 1835, the business was under Elizabeth Hunt's name, and a year later, moved to 60 Redclif Street. Robert Hunt took over the business in 1852. From 1854 to 1857, the company was owned by Hunt, Walter and Brison. Hunt left in 1858, and Walter and Brison owned the company until 1862. The business was referred to under the name of E. Brison and Co from 1863 until 1910. There is no mention of the brush and bellows makers in the Directories after 1910.

The brush and bellows manufactory shared its premises on Peter Street with William Randall, clothier and outfitter, until 1892. Four years later, Garlick and Sons, hatters and hosiers, was established at 24 Peter Street in 1896. The hatters remained on location until the 1940's. Edward Stuart milliner was located at 25 Peter Street from 1910 and is last mentioned in the kelly's 1940 Directory. From 1916 a billiard saloon owned by Edward Albert Lewis; a woolen merchants called Langdon, Phillips and Co, and E Broderick & Co. clothing manufacturers were also found at the same address. All these companies were clossed by the late 1920's.

The buildings of 24 and 25 Peter Street were destroyed by World War II bombing.

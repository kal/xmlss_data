monument_desc A house at the western end of Blackfriars. The building is of probable seventeenth-century date, and was constructed as a merchant's summerhouse or garden house (R. H. Leech pers. comm.).
An attached, gabled, house of three and a half storeys plus a cellar. It was of a stone rubble construction with a rendered exterior; There was a 1-window range to north-east, 2-window range to south-east and large doors to the street; 6-light transomed first-floor window; cross-window to second-floor; oculus to attic. Lateral stack in centre of south-east wall, windows to either side (blocked by the 1950s).
Street's album (BRSMG Ex libris No.9104/13 A & C) includes two photographs of a staircase, probably in the small gabled projection on the north-west side of the main house. It is a seventeenth-century open-well version with square newels with ball finials and pendants, and turned balusters.
The building was surveyed in 1989 (BUAD 361). Subsequent archaeological excavation in 1989 (BUAD 444) recorded evidence of since demolished parts of the building on its north side as well as the associated garden. The house was substantially altered after recording and excavation was complete.

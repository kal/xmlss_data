monument_desc St. George's Park. The creation of a public park in St. George was suggested in 1890, and by 1894 the Urban District Council had acquired Fire Engine Farm as a site. Later a parcel of land was bought from Whitehall Colliery. There was an informal opening on 18 July 1894 after which designs for the layout of the park were invited. The winning design was that submitted by T. Lawrence Lewis. This had as its main feature a lake around which paths were set, with straight walks focusing on a central bandstand. Originally this was planted with Chestnut trees but these failed to establish themselves and within a few years were replaced with London Planes. A wrought iron fence 5 feet 6 inches high surrounded the park. In 1897 the Bristol Corporation took over the park and completed the laying out, which was apparently progressing slowly because of financial restrictions. The bowling green was completed in 1907-08. A cannon was positioned immediately inside the main entrance. The cannon was probably removed during the Second World War and the bandstand in 1958 (Lambert 2000, 54-60).

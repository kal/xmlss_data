monument_desc A house located on the north side of Alderskey Lane, attached to the east side of No.79 Broad Quay (BUAD 1395M). The site was created by the infilling of Aldsworth's Dock in the mid-seventeenth century and a building is recorded on the site by Millerd's 1673 map of Bristol. The house was constructed in the later seventeenth- or early eighteenth century date and was of two storeys, and had two bays and a gabled pantile roof. The main entrance was on the west side of the elevation. It appears from cartographic evidence that the house was constructed with a detached kitchen block to the north. In total the plot measured 14 metres from north to south and was 6 metres wide.
The building was still in use as a dwelling in January 1887 according to the Goad fire insurance plan and was apparently demolished soon after the closure of Alderskey Lane in 1900 (Pritchard 1904, 128-129). The site of the rear part was excavated in 1979 (BUAD 442) but any evidence for the building appears to have been removed by the basements of the CWS building.

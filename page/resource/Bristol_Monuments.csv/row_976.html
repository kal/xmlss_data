monument_desc No.18 Broad Street. Adjacent to the old Guildhall, on the upper side. Attached. 3 storeys and attic; front and side gables; fully jettied to front. Right-hand entrance; windows centre and left, the latter set slightly forward; cellar access below central windows; bracket at right end. First-floor full-width windows with transom, left end set forward as an oriel, 10-light/2-light; brackets below sill level of remainder, with about 24 lights, 2 or 4 to end; panelling beneath windows for whole width. Pair of oriels to second-floor, 8 and 2 lights each, 2 brackets below each; 2-light mullioned window to right return. Front attic window of probably 2 lights; side window of 2 lights, the one sub-divided. Front gable with 2 purlins each side. Side half-gable forward of front wall of Guildhall.
Occupied by T. Fry, carver and print seller, 1806. Had once been occupied by one Parsley, tooth-puller to much of Bristol and area. In July 1831 Braikenridge noted that the front had just been removed to be replaced by a modern one.
Other illustrations of the right return against the Guildhall include M.2335 and M.2336.

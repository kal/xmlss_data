monument_desc Colthurst and Harding's Oil and Colour works, Portwall Lane. The works lay between Portwall Lane and Pile Street. The Oil ad Colour works was in existence by 1874 and was leased by Colthurst and Harding from Whitson's Charity which owned the property. Two elements of the works were leased for a term of 99 years determinable on the life of George Thorne Ricketts, for which annual rents of £0/7/0 and £1/0/0 were paid. Of the other two elements, one was held on a 21 year absolute lease and "The reversion in a small part of the Freehold is sold to the Bristol Harbour Railway Company," for which an annual rent of £40/0/0 was paid. The other element, a "Piece or parcel of land whereon formerly stood part of the Temple Gate Hotel, fronting Temple Street and Portwall Lane," was held on a 12 year absolute lease from Great Western Railway Company and Bristol and Exeter Railway Company (Anon., 1877 20). The building was still extant during the First World War but were subsequently demolished. The site now lies partly beneath Redcliffe Way and partly beneath the grassed area on the south side of Portwall Lane.

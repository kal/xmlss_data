monument_desc Guinness Cells, located at Prince's Wharf, Wapping. In the 1930s the Guiness stout supplied to the south-west of England was brewed in Dublin and shipped to Bristol, Glasgow, Liverpool and Manchester. In Bristol Broad Quay was the original site for unloading but in the 1930s this operation was moved to Prince's Wharf. The method of distribution was changed in the 1950s and the stout was shipped in 504 gallon aluminium casks. Two cells, or cold stores, were built at Prince's Wharf for bulk storage of the casks in 1954. Two further cells were later added, and a fifth was constructed against the Guinness shed in c.1960.
The cells are two-storey brick structures, the store element comprising the bulk of the building and having steel double doors in the north-facing elevation.
Comparison with Guinness installations in other major western ports has suggested that the Bristol cells are likely to be unique, representing a local storage solution. The cells may have been designed by the Port of Bristol, although the Guinness company was certainly involved in the design (Leech 2005, 16).

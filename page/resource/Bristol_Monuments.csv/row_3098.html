monument_desc Cote, located on the southern side of Cote House Lane. The building was constructed in the early eighteenth century and is a stone rubble house of two storeys. The building was reached by a carriage drive from the Downs past an entrance lodge. A designed formal garden (SMR 300) was created to the west of the house in the early nineteenth century by Mrs Jean Anderson and John Wedgewood.
The main house has a Grade II* listing (901-1/28/1982) while the lodge (901-1/31/1987) and the Orangery (901-1/28/1984) have Grade II listings. The gate piers of the Orangery have a separate Grade II listing (901-1/28/1983).

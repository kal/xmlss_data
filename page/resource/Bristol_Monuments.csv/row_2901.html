monument_desc The Dove House, No.9, Dowry Square. The structure is a three-storey, rendered house with a double-pile plan and a pitched pantile roof. It was apparently constructed in the mid-1740s since a lease of April 1745 concerning No.10 Dowry Square describes the house as "new built" (Ison 1952, 159).
The main, south-west facing, elevation is of five bays and the main doorway occupies the centre bay at ground floor.
The original panelling and stair in the entrance hall survives. The stair has an irregular arrangement of balusters, with roughly every third baluster being a turned baluster. All of the balusters have small unturned blocks close to the base.
The cellars have flagstone floors and on the eastern wall of the east cellar parts of an original stone chimneypiece survive together with an adjacent copper.
The building has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/14/1426). Photographs of the interior of the building taken on 7 May 2003 are held in the Sites and Monuments Record Collection of Ground-based Photographs.

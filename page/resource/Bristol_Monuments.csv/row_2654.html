monument_desc The Royal Hotel, located on the south side of College Green. The hotel was built between 1863 and 1868 to a design by W. H. Hawtin. Limestone ashlar with ridge stacks and a slate hipped roof. Double-depth plan. Italianate style. 4 storeys and attic; 11-window range. A heavily decorated symmetrical front, 3:1:3:1:3, has projecting wings; a bracketed moulded cornice to the balustrade. Rustication to the ground floor. A Venetian doorway to the main entrance. Ground- and first-floor semicircular-arched windows; 2-storey canted bays to the wings; segmental pediments to the wings and centre, plain pediments to the outer middle windows. Tripartite middle windows. Second-floor windows have raised surrounds and rounded corners, third-floor windows with rounded corners, and semicircular-arched windows to the attic. Hipped dormers. The left return is a matching symmetrical 3-window range.
The building was restored and the interior largely remodelled in 1991; includes a Palm Court with glazed roof.

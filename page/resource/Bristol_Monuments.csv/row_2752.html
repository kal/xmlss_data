monument_desc Stone Bridge, or St.Giles's Bridge, which bridged the River Frome at the Quayhead. The first stone for the bridge was laid on 16 April 1753 (Evans, 1824 274). The bridge, of rusticated stonework, consisted of a single span arch with parapets and was only approximately 6 feet wide. Rowbotham's 1825 drawing from the south-east shows an additional, angled, partial arch at the south-west corner.
The Frome culvert was extended up to this point in the mid nineteenth century, but was extended further downstream in 1891-1892. The end of the culvert was built against the bridge, and the parapets removed to create the pavement for the road. The bridge is known to survive beneath the end of Rupert Street.

monument_desc The Alhambra music hall, located on the northern side of Broadmead. The building was first used as a concert hall and between 1874 and 1888 became the Alhambra Music Hall. It became the New Star between 1889 and 1895 and then The Tivoli Music Hall between 1896 and 1900 (Winstone, 1983 pls.129 & 131). The Tivoli was the first venue in Bristol to show moving pictures in 1896 when a series of short films were booked for a fortnight run from June 8th Anderson, 1983, 8). The building became a cinema owned by Frank Ogden Smith known as the Welligton Palace from 1912 until 1916 then the Broadmead for a short time in 1916, although the cinema closed soon after (Anderson, 1983, 20). C19 facade, heavily decorated. Ground-floor of 3 arches, all different widths but each a pointed segmental arch. First-floor of 7 windows with pointed arches, arranged 3+1+3. Similar arrangement for second-floor, but the heads with shouldered lintels. A pointed window to the middle of the third-floor, which was the base of the tower, with a dormer in the steeply-sloping roof to either side. A further storey in the tower, with, possibly, a clock face against the street, then a spire. The building was demolished in 1952 to make way for the redevelopment of Broadmead.

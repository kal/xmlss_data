monument_desc The Talbot Hotel, located on the southern side of the juncton of Bath Street and Victoria Street. Built in the early 1870s, top two floors lost in the Blitz, but restored 1992. Red brick with yellow brick and limestone dressings. Double-depth plan. Originally had a heavy cornice that included the top storey.
2 storeys; 12-window range. Top storeys missing, the lower ones are defined by strings with angled bricks and have yellow brick stripes; ground-floor arcade of arches within 2-centred arches defined by moulded bricks; similar larger doorways to the Bath Street corner and third bay from the right; first-floor windows in alternate groups of 2 and 3 with semicircular arches. 3 bay frontage of Victorian building to Bath Street, then two storeys of former No.3 and three of No.4.
Some original features including staircase and fireplaces; a panelled lift cage.
Part of a group of commercial buildings developed after Victoria Street was created.

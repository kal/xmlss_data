monument_desc No.24 Pithay. A single-bay house of two storeys plus attic which was located on the eastern side of Pithay. The ground floor was occupied by a shopfront with the entrance at the centre. A full-width pentice roof tiled in pantiles separated the ground from the first floor. At second floor was a bay window and above another full-width pentice marked the division between the second floor and attic. There was a single casement light at attic level. The building had an M-shaped pantile roof.
The building survived to the end of the nineteenth century and was a dwelling in 1887. It was demolished in c.1900 and the site redeveloped.

monument_desc The war memorial for the First World War, Arno's Vale Cemetery. The memorial was constructed c.1920 and is a central flat-roofed loggia of Bath stone ashlar with five semicircular headed arches with moulded keystones and a plat band. On the internal face of the rear wall of the loggia, there is an arcade of five blind arches with copper panels bearing the names of the fallen. The central arch has an inscription carved into the stone. There are flanking walls on the northern and southern sides of the memorial. The legend "THE GLORIOUS DEAD/A. D. 1914 - 1918" is carved into east face of the southern wall while the northern carries the legend "PROCLAIM THROUGHOUT THIS REALM/YE WHO PASS THIS MONUMENT/THAT WE WHO DIED SERVING HER/REST HERE CONTENT."
The war memorial has a Grade II Listing (Listed Building number 901-1/47/2133).

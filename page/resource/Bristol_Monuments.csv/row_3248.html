monument_desc North Street Wesleyan Day School, also known as North Street Junior School, located on the northern side of Backfields. The building was designed by the the local architects Foster and Wood in May 1856 (BRO 22938(2)). The school was constructed over the following couple of years. The Master's house, attached to the south-west end of the building, formed an integral part of the design.
The school is a two-storey Pennant sandstone structure with limestone details in a Gothic Revival style. It has a single-depth plan with a hall in the core south-west to north-east two-storey element, with gabled north-west to south-east wings at either end. The fenestration consists of limestone ashlar mullioned and transomed Perpendicular Gothic tracery windows. The Master's House is a lower two-storey element of the strucure with with a hipped roof and low chimneystack at the south-western end of the ridge. There is a porch with a French Empire-style pyramidal roof on the south-east facing elevation.
An extension, again designed by Foster and Wood, was added to the girls' school, on the north-western side of the building, in 1871 (BRO 22938(16)).
A memorial to former pupils killed during the First World War (1914-1918) was fixed at the landing of the main stairwell after the war. The primary school closed in 1959 and planning permission was granted in the following year to convert the building into a Coroner's Court and mortuary. The works were apparently carried out in 1961, with the mortuary being located on the ground floor and the Coroner's Court occupying the first floor. The interior was modernised for the Coroner's Court and the building remained in that use until 2003 when it closed.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/6/2019).

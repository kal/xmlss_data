monument_desc The Society of Friend's Burial Ground, Quaker's Friars, also known as the Priors' burial ground. The plot on which the burial ground was sited was bought by the meeting in February 1669 (Old style) but it is not clear when interment began. An Order in Council of 18 February 1854 made under the Burial Act of 1852 imposed the restriction that "only one body [was] to be buried in each grave" in the burial ground.
The meeting house and burial ground were sold in 1956 and the burials reportedly exhumed (being reburied in Avon View cemetery) (Stock 1996, 6). The grave markers were removed to Bedminster Meeting House.
The site of the burial ground was used for car parking and was re-landscaped in 1998 along with the rest of the area surrounding Quakers Friars but, beyond the insertion of shallow service runs, the stratification was not disturbed. Further archaeological investigation on the south and east sides of the burial ground in 2002 (BUAD 3849) and 2006 recorded in situ post-medieval inhumations.

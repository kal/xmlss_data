monument_desc Everard Building, a printing works, located on the east side of Broad Street. The building was constructed in 1900-1901 to a design by Henry Williams. The facade was by W. J. Neatby, Chief Designer for Doulton and Co.
Polychromatic 'Carrara' marble-ware faience. Pre-Raphaelite style. 3 storeys; 4-window range.
A wide semicircular-arched doorway with flanking elliptical-arched windows. A pair of semicircular-arched first-floor windows, EDWARD EVERARD in Art Nouveau lettering, spandrel panels featuring Gutenburg (L) and Morris (R). A second-floor arcade of 4 semicircular arches. In the gable is a semicircular-arched panel. Interior completely rebuilt behind the facade. Forrmerly part of a larger printing works extending to John Street.
Beneath is a fragment of medieval cellar (1022A), possibly 14th.century, ribbed, and part of a 5-bay vault. Now combined with the cellars of Nos.35 & 36, as part of a public house. The works was demolished behind the façade c.1970
An important early use of glazed coloured external ware to propagate Arts and Crafts ideas of the reconciliation of art and industry, in the largest decorative facade of its kind in Britain.
The building has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/11/527).

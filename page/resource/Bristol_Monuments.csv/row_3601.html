monument_desc Odeon Theatre, a cinema located on the western side of Union Street. The building was designed by T. Cecil Howitt and opened on 16 July 1938, having cost £57,600 to construct. It had 1945 seats, 1051 in the stalls and another 894 in the balcony. The cinema had a corner entrance with a circular tower above which contained five recessed windows. The entrance foyer was circular and there was an octagonal double-height foyer which was apparently unique amongst Odeon cinemas. A promenade at first-floor level led to the balcony (Eyles 2002, 186-189). The cinema was damaged by bombing on 3 December 1940 and closed briefly, re-opening on 17 December 1940. In 1967 the building was modernised and it was converted to three screens in May 1974, with 844 seats in the former balcony and 108 and 103 in the former stalls. The cinema was closed again in October 1983 for redevelopment of the interior to a design by the architect Donald Armstrong of Downton & Hurst. A new shop was created in the ground floor and the entrance moved to the side. The cinema reopened on 13 June 1985 with three screens seating 400, 225 and 215 respectively (Eyles 2002, 249-250). The building is currently (23/04/2002) still in use as a cinema.

monument_desc Ashton Swing Bridge. A rail and road bridge constructed as part of the Portishead to Wapping Wharf railway in a joint initiative by the Bristol Corporation and the Great Western Railway. The bridge was opened on 4th October 1906 and was hydraulically-powered, using 182 gallons for each swing, drawn from the hydraulic power system of the Floating Harbour. The bridge was last swung in 1936 and is now fixed. The road deck and operator's cabin were removed in 1965 when the Cumberland Basin flyover was built (Buchanan & Buchanan 1980, 42: Lord & Southam 1983, 47-48; 64-65; 77-78).
The bridge has a Grade II listing (Listed Building No. 901-1/41/10087)

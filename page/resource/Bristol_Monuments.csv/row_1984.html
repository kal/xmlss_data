monument_desc Ashton Gate Football Ground. Bristol City played their first game here in 1894 on land leased from the Ashton Court estate, although the first ground was constructed by 1904. The earliest depiction of the sports ground is on the 1:2500 Ordnance Survey plan published in 1903. This plan shows a pavillion building on the northeast side of the ground and an incomplete rectangular area surrounded by banks. The west corner of the sports pitch is not depicted presumeably because the ground was not completed until after the survey. The plan also indicates that the area was in use as a cricket and footbal ground. By the time of the Ordnance Survey plan published in 1918 the ground had been repositioned to be centred approximately 100m west of the original ground. On this plan the site of the original pitch was in use as a bowling green and the football ground appears to have southwest and northeast stands with open terraces possibly made from earth banks to the northwest and southeast. The stands have been considerably altered and rebuilt at various times during the twentieth century. Records held by the football club suggest that the northeast stand was rebuilt in 1970, the southeast in 1928, the southwest in 1958 and the northwest stand in 1994.

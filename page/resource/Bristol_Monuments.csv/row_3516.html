monument_desc Whitwood Mill, Stapleton, also known as Snuff Mills Park and Stapleton Mill, located on the northern bank of the river Frome, some 300 metres to the east of Frome Bridge. The mill was in existence by the early-seventeenth century and is recorded by a map of Kingswood of 1610. By the 1770s the mill was owned by Thomas Bridgman and was known as Bridgman's Mill. It remained in the hands of the family until c1823 when it was taken over by Charles Hopkins. Hopkins had been declared bankrupt by 1843. In 1846 the mill became the property of Thomas Jones junior. Jones died in 1848 and the mill was subsequently leased to Josiah Bell, who ran it until the early twentieth century. The Ordnance Survey first edition 1:2500 plan (Gloucester Sheet LXXII.06) published in 1882 names the mill as Stapleton Mill and indicates that it was a flour mill at that date but by 1902 the mill was disused.
The mill was a single storey structure with an undershot wheel, boiler and chimney. In 1899 the machinery comprised "a 12 Horse-Power Water Wheel, three pairs of stones, Oat, Malt and Bean crusher and Dressing Mill" (quoted in Ward 1969, 24). The dwelling house was extant by the time of the tithe survey in 1839, and lay immediately to the west of the mill.
Elements of the mill survive. The mill has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/30/1971).

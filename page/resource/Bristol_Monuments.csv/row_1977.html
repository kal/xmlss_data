monument_desc A building known as the Tithe Barn, located on the southern side of High Street, Shirehampton, which is reputed to have been built c.1470. The building appears to have been constructed as a barn and has a ten bay roof, of collar beam type, with curved wind braces. It is reputed to be associated with a medieval priory but there is currently no historical evidence to support its connection with any monastic site.
The building was heavily altered in the nineteenth and twentieth centuries. A two-storey cottage was added to the western end of the building and the rear (south) wall of the barn has been substantially rebuilt, with the insertion of modern windows. The fabric of the north wall, facing High Street, shows evidence of a large blocked entrance.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/18/1737).

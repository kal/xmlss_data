monument_desc Wesleyan Methodist Chapel, located on the northern side of Lower Ashley Road. The chapel was founded in 1837. A group of Methodist merchants acquired part of the former Baptist Mills brass works in January 1837 and the chapel opened on 22 November of that year. The building was a two-storey building in a classical style with transepts and an apsaidal eastern end. The Ordnance Survey first edition 1:500-scale Town plan (Bristol Sheet LXXII.13.3) surveyed in 1881 indicates that the chapel could accommodate 2000 worshippers.
The associated burial ground surrounded the chapel building, and was enclosed by a low stone wall surmounted by railings, but there was also a separate part to the north-east of the building. The register for the burial ground lists 1062 interments there between 1837 and 1899 (BRO 35123/W/P/1-2).
The Bristol Corporation the Compulsory Purchase of property within the study area in the early 1960s. The Wesley Chapel was subject to Compulsory Purchase by the Bristol Corporation in 1970 and had been demolished by 1973.

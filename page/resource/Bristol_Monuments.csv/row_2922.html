monument_desc The church of St. Jude the Apostle, located between Braggs Lane (to the south) and Lamb Street in Old Market. The church occupies the site of Poyntzpool. It was constructed in 1849 to fill a perceived need to expand the ministry to the growing working-class population of St. Philip's Marsh, and much of the funding was provided by the Special Fund created by Bishop Monk (Ralph & Cobb, 1991 28). The church was designed by S. B. Gabriel and is of squared Pennant Sandstone rubble with limestone dressings and a tiled roof. Chancel and south-east chapel, aisled nave and south-west tower. Decorated Gothic Revival style. 2-bay chancel with a late nineteenth century south vestry and an early twentieth century south-east chapel. 4-bay north aisle. Similar south aisle. 3-stage tower with diagonal buttresses and a square-sided south-east stair tower to the second stage.
The organ from St. Matthias-on-the-Weir in Broadweir (BUAD 912M), built by the Bristol-based organ builders W. G. Vowles, was moved to the church, presumably in the 1940s after St. Matthias closed. It was refurbished by the firm of Rushworth & Dreaper in 1970, but was subsequently removed and installed in St. George's Anglican Church on Orleana Square in Gawler, a town north of the Australian city of Adelaide.
The church was unoccupied for around three years around the end of the twentieth century and the interior suffered substantial damage from vandalism in that period. The church was converted to flats in 2003.
The church has a Grade II listing (Listed Building Number 901-1/12/1626).

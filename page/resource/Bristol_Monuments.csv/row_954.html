monument_desc The Ship public house, located on the north side of Griffin Lane. Gable to the Lane. 2 storeys and attic; 1-window range; timber-framed front. Attached to the west. Square 2-storey porch projecting from left-hand side, open to south and east at ground-floor, sash window to front above, lean-to roof. Main entrance through porch. Bay window. 3-light windows to first-floor and attic. First-floor right-hand set forward slightly. Lateral stack a short distance in on east return. There may have been another lateral stack on the opposite wall. On the west side of the main block, about halfway back, was a small projection to eaves level. To the rear was a small extension of slightly increased height, with stacks between the two parts. The building was demolished to enable the construction of Perry Road in the late 1860s.

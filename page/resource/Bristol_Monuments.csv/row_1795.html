monument_desc St Blaise's Chapel. The foundation of the chapel of St. Blaise (patron saint of wool combers) on Henbury Hill. The hill was dug up in 1707, when "ancient Roman coins...(SMR 778), and in a vault 10 yards long and 6 yards broad...many human bodies were discovered." A chapel of St. Blaise on Blaise Castle Hill is marked on early maps (Saxton 1577; Speed 1610). It is also mentioned in local histories and traditions, though no medieval records of its existence have yet come to light. Possibly dedicated earlier to St Werburgha (SMR 817). J Bartlett's excavations in 1918 revealed the foundations of the chapel of St. Blasius about 15 feet east of Blaise Castle. Little more than the levelled and mortared surface remains, with a few stones in their original alignment, of a C14/15 buttressed building measuring 28 feet by 14 feet. Several skeletons were found in rock cut graves, all with their heads to the west and indicated a well-filled burial (SMR 778). Excavations in 1957 revealed Roman and medieval material.

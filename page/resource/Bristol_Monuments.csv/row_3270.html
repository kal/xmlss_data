monument_desc A lighthouse located on north bank of the river Avon, located 150 metres to the north of the north corner of Avonmouth Dock. The lighthouse was probably constructed in the mid-nineteenth century and consisted of a hexagonal central tower with connected blocks on its north and south sides) set within a semi-circular enclosure.
The lighthouse tower stood approximately five storeys high and the top had a machicolation approximately 1.5 metres high with a walkway around the lantern. The lantern had a circular sloping roof with a ball finial at its apex. Single-storey blocks with pitched roofs connected the tower to identical two-storey structures to the north and south. The two-storey blocks appear to have had an identical, mirror-image, form. The west-facing elevations had bay windows at the centre and a smaller sash window above. There was a moulded stone hood mould over each. The side, outward-facing, elevations had wide projecting three-flue chimney stacks at either end. These projected above the eaves and had three tall individual brick flues with clay chimneypots on top of each stack. Between the stacks, apparently mid-way between ground and first floor level there were small casement windows with a hood mould, possibly indicating the position of internal stairs. The roofs were pitched and there were low parapets at either end. The inward-facing elevations had a large window at ground floor with a hood mould above at its western end. The enclosure was surrounded by a tall curving wall.
The Ordnance Survey second edition 1:2500 plan (Gloucestershire Sheet LXXI.01) published in 1903 records that the lighthouse was had an occulting white light (giving out longer phases of darkness than illumination) with a red sector (a vertical red strip of light in the centre of the beam which was only visible within a certain arc and could therefore be used to set a course). The lighthouse survived into the early nineteenth century but was demolished between mid-1902 and 1908 to allow the construction of Royal Edward Dock. The site is now at the edge of the channel leading from Avonmouth Dock to Royal Edward Dock. All traces of the lighthouse tower will have been removed by the construction of a small dock c.1916 but the at least part of the buildings to either side may well survive.

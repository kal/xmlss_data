monument_desc At ST54967787 is a univallate earthwork on Kings Weston Hill (Tratman 1946, 179). The rampart is up to 2 feet high, and the outer ditch 3 feet wide, and 1 foot deep. The northern bank, marked by the hedge of Evergreen Wood, is 220 yards long and is best preserved at the east end near a small tumulus (SMR 784). The ditch apparently avoids this mound by crossing the crown of the hill obliquely. {1}The north bank of this earthwork does not run along the hedge line of Evergreen Wood, but along the edge of the very steep scarp about 12 metres further north. The ditch here is inside the bank and not outside as elsewhere. Within the wood at the west end there are signs of a retaining wall in the outer face of the bank which is 3 metres wide and 1.5 metres high above a ditch more than 2 metres wide. Traces of an outer defence along the north and south sides have been noted by Dr. Everton. There does not seem to be any entrance to the earthwork. In 1966 the earthwork was cut by a gas main trench.

monument_desc Piggery, located some 180 metres to the north of Manor Road, Stapleton. The piggery was constructed at the end of the nineteenth century and is first recorded by the Ordnance Survey second edition 1:2500 plan (Gloucestershire Sheet LXXII.06) published in 1903. It is a single-storey building, measuring approximately 5.5 metres from east to west and 8 metres north to south. The piggery is constructed of coursed Pennant sandstone rubble with ashlar quoins and details with a pitched roof. It was extended in the later twentieth century by the addition of pre-fabricated concrete structures on its east side and converted to changing rooms. The interior is reported to have been remodelled.

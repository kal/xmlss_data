monument_desc A house at the east end of Broad Weir. The house formed the westernmost of a group of four and was apparently built as one of a pair, the other being the house to the east [162M]. The house was of two storeys plus attic and was rendered with jettied fronts. The frontage had a single bay with shop windows to ground-floor. The building had a jettied north elevation and pantile roofs with clustered chimneys. The second-floor window was of 8 lights The building was demolished in January 1901.
The site now lies mainly under the pavement and flower bed on the south side of Broad Weir but was in the highway between the late nineteenth century and the 1960s. Services are likely therefore to have disturbed the site.

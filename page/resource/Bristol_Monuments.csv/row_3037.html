monument_desc Clifton College. The college was founded in 1860-1862. A company was created to manage the school and finance was raised by issuing shares in it (Christie, 1935 18). The core buildings of the school were constructed between 1860 and 1863 and were designed by Charles Francis Hansom and included the hall, boarding house and dwelling houses for the masters. The Guthrie Memorial chapel, again designed by Hansom, was added in 1866 and the library and additional classrooms were constructed in 1869. The Wilson Tower, by Hansom and Bond, was built in 1889, followed by a science lecture theatre around 1898. A memorial commemorating the South African War by the sculptor Alfred Drury was added in 1904 and a First World War memorial in the form of a gatehouse, designed by Charles Holden, in 1922. A statue of Earl Haig by W. McMillan was unveiled by Field-Marshal Sir William Birdwood, commander of the Anzac forces during the First World War and a Clifton College old boy, on Saturday 15th July 1932.
Several of the college buildings are listed Grade II. The Guthrie Memorial Chapel (Listed Building No. 901-1/2/830) and the memorial to the South African War (Listed Building No. 901-1/2/831) are Grade II*.

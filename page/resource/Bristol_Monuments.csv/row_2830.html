monument_desc No.74 Stokes Croft, located on the eastern side of Stokes Croft. The building was constructed in 1868 for Rev. R. W. Gotch of the Stokes Croft Chapel (BUAD 1451M) to a design by E. W. Godwin (Godwin's original drawings for the buildings are held by the Kelmscott Gallery, Chicago). The building was one of a pair with No.76 Stokes Croft (BUAD 1468M). It is a house of two-storeys and attic, constructed of brick with a tiled roof and comprises a shop with residential accommodation above (Soros 1999, 151-153). As designed, the main, north-west facing, elevation had a large Gothic arch forming the ground floor and this contained the shopfront. A string course divided ground and first floor. The fenestration at first-floor consisted of two sashes within arched openings. The parapet had a brick dentil course with stone copings. Above there was a central dormer with a pitched tile roof and a finial at the apex of the gable, which projected out of the parapet. The building has a pitched roof with a tall chimneystack, serving both No.74 and No.76 Stokes Croft, on its north side.
The building is extant although the arch has been removed and the shopfront replaced.

monument_desc Nos 7-10 Bedford Row, four attached three-storey houses constructed in the mid-
1820s with considerable twentieth century extensions and alterations in association with their use as nurses accomodation for the Bristol Royal Infirmary. The row of houses incorporated a wedge-shaped portion at the Terrell Street end which appeared to have comprised a separate unit listed as no.11 in some street directories.
At some point in the twentieth century the cellars for the properties were knocked through to create a single room spanning the entire length of the terrace.
The terrace is Listed Grade II, although consent for their demolition was granted in 2005. The terrace was recorded in 2010 prior to demolition (BHER 24870).

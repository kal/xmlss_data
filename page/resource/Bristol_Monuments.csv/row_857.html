monument_desc Vestry Hall, located on the north west side of Pennywell Road. The building was constructed in 1880 as a segragated sports and activities gymnasium owned by Bristol Corporation Baths Department (Stephenson & Willmott 2005, 113). David Lloyd-George addressed a meting in the hall during the Second Boer War.
Ralph Pringle leased the building and converted it into a 580-seat cinema. This opened on 19 October 1909 (Winstone 1972, 8) and Vestry Hall was Ralph Pringle's second cinema in Bristol after the Bedminster Town Hall Cinema (Anderson 1983, 16). A licence for cinematograph entertainment, music and dancing had been obtained by 1920. In 1921 the lease was taken over by Mr A. B. Atkinson who installed an RCA sound system. The hall remained in use as a cinema until 1954. The building was subsequently converted to industrial use.
The building is extant. A survey of the building was carried out by David Etheridge of Avon Archaeological Unit in 2007 (BHER 4428) (Etheridge, 2008).

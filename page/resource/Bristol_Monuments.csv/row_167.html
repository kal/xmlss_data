monument_desc The sugar refinery of Isaac Hort in Redcliff Backs. A tenement on Redcliff Backs was converted into a sugar refinery apparently by Isaac Hort in the latter part of the seventeenth century and was taken over by Stephen Clutterbuck in 1715. After Clutterbuck's death the refinery passed to Nicholas Bloom and remained in the hands of the Bloom family into the second half of the eighteenth century. The building became the slaughterhouse of the butcher Robert Bartlett in 1774 but was leased to Messrs. Morgan, Blake & Co. in 1776 who resumed sugar refining there. The building survived into the nineteenth century but at least half was destroyed for the creation of Ferry Street in the mid nineteenth-century.
Elements of the south wall of the building survive in the listed wall in Ferry Street.

monument_desc Lady Huntingdon's Chapel, also known as Salem Chapel, located on the north-west side of St. Augustine's Place. The frontage was a continuation of a facade that included No.7 St. Augustine's Place and was of three storeys with a three-window range. String courses divided the storeys and the upper part of the elevation had a cornice and parapet. The southern end of the elevation was angled to begin to turn the corner into Pipe Lane. At ground-floor there was a segmental arch double-width entrance with steps. The fenestration at first-floor consisted of three Gothic arched windows, the central window being a four-centred arch with four lights and intersecting tracery. The two outer windows were narrower two-light windows. At second-floor there were three six-over-six pane sashes.
From c1726 building was occupied by a theatre which was the base for Charles Williams's Company. Thomas Lewis and his company also exhibited plays there around the end of the 1720s. The buildings remained in use as a place of entertainment until their purchase by the Countess of Huntingdon's Connexion.in c.1775 by the Countess of Huntingon's Connexion to act as a chapel. The congregation moved to a chapel at the bottom corner of Lodge Street in 1830.

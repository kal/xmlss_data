monument_desc All Saints' Almshouse, All Saints' Street. The almshouse was located on the east side of All Saints' Street with a burial ground located behind (BUAD 1190M). The almshouse had originally been sited in High Street and moved to a location adjacent to St. John's Almshouses, St. John's Steep in 1740-1741.
The almshouse at All Saints' Street was built c1814 (Lobel & Carus-Wilson 1975, 26) and the almshouse and burial ground are shown on Plumley and Ashmead's map of 1828 and Ashmead's map of 1850. However, the form of the building is not currently known. The building provided accommodation for "eight poor women selected and appointed by the vestry from the parish poor." Each had an apartment to themselves and the parish was reported in the late 1820s to keep the building in good repair (Manchee 1831, 308).
The almshouse had been closed by the time of the Ordnance Survey first edition 1:500-scale Town plan of the 1880s and the site taken over as an iron foundry. It is not clear whether this involved the complete demolition of the almshouse building, or whether parts were incorporated into the foundry. The burial ground, however, seems to have remained undeveloped for many years and to have been used as a yard for the foundry.

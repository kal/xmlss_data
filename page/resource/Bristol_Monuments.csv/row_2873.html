monument_desc Prebendal house, located on the south side of College Square. The building was constructed in 1753 by Rev. John Castelman. The building was of two storeys, basement and attic and had a footprint measuring 13 metres from east to west and 10 metres north-south. The main, north-facing, elevation had five bays, each with a single arched sash window in a moulded opening at ground and first floor. The sills were supported by brackets. The main entrance door was in the centre bay. The division between ground- and first-floor level was marked by a plat band and there were ashlar quoins at either end of the elevation. The plat bands marking the division between basement and ground floor and ground and first floor continued around the west side of the building. The form and fenestration of the west-facing elevation were similar to that of the main elevation, with similar sash windows at ground and first floor level. Above the cornice and parapet there were three attic dormers and the roof was apparently a mansard. When recorded by a watercolour in the mid-nineteenth century (BUAD 3790) the building was rendered and painted black or dark grey. There was a large "T-shaped" garden (800 metres square), with a formal layout of paths and a number of mature trees, to the rear of the building and this was reached by steps leading down from the back of the house.
The house appears to have been demolished c.1890 along with the rest of the buildings on the south side of College Square to enable the construction of Anchor Road. The site of the building now lies mainly beneath Anchor Road.

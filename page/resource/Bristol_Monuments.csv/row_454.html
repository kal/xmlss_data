monument_desc The Elton Mansion, No.7 Small Street. The street frontage was rendered, with a short plinth, the whole elevation one with that of No.8. The property was purchased by Sir Abraham Elton from Sir Thomas Cann in 1700, the house having been occupied previously by Richard Streamer. On the ground-floor, a central arched vehicle entrance with double doors; a large window to each side. Three tall first-floor windows; two second-floor windows, above the outer ones of the floor below; similar mouldings to the window openings on both storeys, with decorative keystones.
An ornate stone chimney-piece (BUAD 1006A), in a first-floor room, used as a board-room, was removed to the company's new building at Telephone Avenue, to be installed in the board-room there. Another example, carrying the arms of the city and the Merchant Venturers, was likewise relocated. The wood panelling and fine plaster ceiling and frieze of the principal room were acquired by Sir E. H. Elton, Bart., of Clevedon Court.
The building was last occupied by the Bristol Waterworks Company. It was removed for the Post Office extension in 1908.

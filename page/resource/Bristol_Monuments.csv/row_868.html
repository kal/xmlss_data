monument_desc Tomb of Hugh Brown, located against the south-east boundary wall of St. John's Churchyard (BUAD 377M). The tomb is of seventeenth-century date and has effigies of Hugh Brown, alderman, and his wife, within a vaulted structure of ashlar stone. There is an open semicircular arch on its north-west side and panels on the underside of the semicircular vault. The vault was recorded in a watercolour drawing by T. L. Rowbotham in 1825 (BRSMG M2430). George Weare Braikenridge, writing in the 1820s, notes that the monument had once had doors, but they had rotted off (Braikenridge Notes, E84).

monument_desc Mylne's Culvert. The culvert was constructed as part of the water management system of the Floating Harbour at the beginning of the nineteenth century to carry the flow of the river Frome away from the lock gates at the Quay Head and into the New Cut. It runs from Stone Bridge, at the northern end of Colston Avenue, down the eastern side of that thoroughfare, below Broad and Narrow Quays, under Prince Street Bridge, and down the line of Wapping Road to the New Cut. Rowbotham's 1828 view (M.2825) shows the entrance to the culvert, built by the Bristol Dock Company for the purpose of carrying the flow of the River Frome directly to the New Cut. Immediately upstream of Stone Bridge and the floodgates. Protected by bars, which had to be cleared from time to time to ensure free flow of the water, and for which purpose a stage or boat was provided at the location. The culvert is still in use.

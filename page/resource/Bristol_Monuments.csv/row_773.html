monument_desc Tropic Club, No.84 Stokes Croft, located on the east side of Stokes Croft and lying some 20 metres back from the street. The building is a three-storey, three-bay rendered house with limestone ashlar dressings and probably dates to the early-eighteenth century. It measures 11 metres from north to south and is approximately 5 metres wide. It has a pitched pantile roof and a cellar. The building was apparently in existence by 1742 and originally stood at the rear of a large walled garden measuring approximately 20 metres from east to west by 11 metres wide. The Ordnance Survey first edition 1:500 plan (Bristol Sheet LXXI.16.10) surveyed in 1883 shows the design of the formal garden.
In the early nineteenth century the building appears to have undergone significant alteration. Plumley and Ashmead's 1828 plan indicates that the structure had bow windows on the west-facing elevation by that date. By 1903 the garden had been developed for the bank which now stands on Stokes Croft. Internally the central entrance hall and the front room to the north of this are fully panelled. The hall also has a semicircular hall arch and open-well stairs. The building has been significantly altered and extended and few original internal features now appear to survive. The roof timbers are also modern.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/6/2022).

monument_desc The Chapel of the Holy Spirit (sometimes called St. Sprite's), in St. Mary Redcliffe Churchyard. The chapel stood close to the south-west corner of St. Mary Redcliffe church and was in existence by 1363. The building measured 17 metres from east to west and was 8 metres wide.
The chapel appears initially to have had a two-cell plan with the entrance, protected by a porch, on the north side of the building. Documentary evidence suggests that the during the medieval period the chapel included an annexe for lepers, tended by the religious from the hospital of St. John the Baptist. A plan of 1717 by Lyons indicates suggests that a room, possibly this annexe, ran the full length of the wall between the porch and the western corner had subsequently been added to the north side of the building.
The chapel was suppressed in 1537 and a grammar school, Queen Elizabeth's Free Grammar and Writing School, was established by letters patent and appears to have occupied the building. The school was to have a master and one under-master and was managed by a board of Governors established by the same letters patent (Manchee 1831, 45). The interior was drawn by James Stewart in 1748 (Bodleian MS.Gough, Somersetshire 2 & 8). Stewart's drawing records two (blocked) pointed arches in north wall; another at east end of south wall, with an ogee-headed opening towards the west, with, in between, a statue (possibly that of Queen Elizabeth now in the church) in an ogee-headed niche. In the east end wall was an eight-light mullion and transom window with, at the north end of the same wall, a fireplace. It had a three-part ceiling with angled sides and flat centre. The visible walls were panelled to height of arch springing.
In the early 1760s the churchwardens seem to have decided that the "beauty and magnificence" of the church were "greatly impaired and diminished by the situation of the said schoolhouse" and the decision was taken to remove it. The Governors resolved to move the school into the Lady chapel at the east end of the church on 31 December 1762 (Manchee 1831, 49). The Lady chapel was fitted out as a schoolroom and the Chapel of the Holy Spirit was demolished in 1763 (Dawson 1981, 23).

monument_desc A Smithy on the south side of Kennel Lodge Road was constructed in the early nineteenth century and the first cartographic depiction of the structure is on an estate plan of 1826 (BRO AC/PL/105) and an accompanying set of record cards lists the plot as (413) "Smiths Shop & Yard".
The building was a single storey rubble construction with gable ends. Archaeological building recording in 2009 (Bryant and Ducker, 2009) identified that the smithy had been built against the roadside of an earlier boundary wall constructed of Brandon Hill grit bonded with a brownish mortar. The gable end walls of the smithy abutted this boundary wall.
The smithy contained a gabled internal cross wall that also abutted the earlier boundary wall.
The walls of the smithy were constructed with Carboniferous Limestone with some Brandon Hil Grit and, Pennant sandstone.

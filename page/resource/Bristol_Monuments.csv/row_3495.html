monument_desc Grove House, also known as The Grove, located on the east side of Grove Road, Brislington. Grove House and garden occupied an area that was bounded by Bristol Hill, Grove Park and Montrose Park. The house is extant but the grounds have been built on and are now covered by housing on the southern side of Montrose Park, on the northern side of Bristol Hill and Grove Park Avenue.
The house was purchased by Richard Ricketts in 1799 from William Fry after Fry became bankrupt. On his death in 1818, the estate passed to his nephew Henry Ricketts, head of the Flint Glass Works at Temple Gate, and he was recorded as being the owner by the Brislington Tithe Survey of 1846. He died there in 1859, and the house remained in the Ricketts family until 1865 when it was sold to Mr. Cripps who died in 1899.
The estate was broken up and the grounds were subsequently developed for housing. The house became a children's home until it was purchased in 1909 by Brislington Congregational Church who had built their church nearby at the corner of Grove Park and Montrose Park. The Ordnance Survey County Series plan published in 1916 indicates that the grounds had been completely built over and the coach house had been converted into a laundry. The house itself became a private school until 1929. In 1978, the United Reform Church sold the property and there were plans to convert the property into flats.
The carriage entrance to the house was in Wick Lane, with smaller gates on Bristol Hill for tradespeople. The walls, gates and railings are constructed of ashlar and pennant rubble with wrought-iron railings and date to the early-nineteenth century. They have a Grade II listed (Listed Building no. 901-1/48/462).

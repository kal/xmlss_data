monument_desc Nos. 5-8 High Street. Four jettied, gabled houses located on the east side of High Street.
Four jettied houses, each with a front gable, all rather narrow, the central two matching in detail above first-floor level. Shop fronts at ground-floor; pairs of sashes at first-floor; single sashes at second-floor; no windows to roof spaces. Much of the interior had been altered, but it was still possible to see that each gable did not necessarily represent an individual property. The solar or principal room extended over Nos.6 & 7at first-floor level, with a small apartment adjoining in No.5. It "was also clear" that No.4 was a part of the original premises.
Only fragments of oak panelling survived in the solar, but there was a finely carved overmantel of sixteenth century date, bearing the name Wyllem Young, also the initials "WY" and "IY" (BUAD 989A). A small medieval relic found on the ground-floor, about six feet in, was a bracket post of the entrance porch, pierced by a narrow light with pointed trefoil head, the post 9ft.3in. high to the top of the spring.
The cellars were of massive construction. A flight of eight steps led down to that below No.6 (BUAD .990A), which was 19 feet long and 11 feet wide, beyond which was an arched passage that led to a pair of parallel cellars (each 37 feet long by 15 feet wide) that communicated at the far end. These must have run under the adjacent properties. From the northern section fourteen steps led up again, with a small chamber (BUAD 991A) leading off the sixth step.
The buildings were demolished in order to erect new premises for Messrs. Jones but were recorded by John Pritchard during demolition, commencing in January 1914.

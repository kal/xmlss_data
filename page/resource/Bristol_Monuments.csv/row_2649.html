monument_desc People's Palace cinema, located on the north side of Baldwin Street. The building opened as a music hall and was in existence by the time of the Ordnance Survey second edition 1:2500 plan (Gloucestershire Sheet LXXII.11) published in 1903. It became a full-time cinema in February 1912 and was taken over by the Biocolour circuit in 1920 (Eyles 1996, 201). The cinema became part of the Gaumont chain in March 1927 and was closed on 30 June 1927 for a reconstruction behind the faacde designed by the architect Frank Verity. It reopened on 14 February 1928 as the New Palace with 1574 seats. The cinema became the Gaumont c.1952 and was modernised c.1960, reducing the seating to 1245 in the process. The cinema closed on 15 March 1980 and is currently (22/04/2002) in use as nightclub.

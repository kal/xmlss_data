monument_desc The Bristol Royal Infirmary, located on the south side of Marlborough Street, between Lower Maudlin Street and Whitson Street. The infirmary was opened in 1737 in a former brewery building to which two extensions were added to form a rectangular court enclosed on three sides (Stevenson 2000, 137). This building was recorded by Jean Rocque's 1742 map of Bristol on the corner of Maudlin Lane and Earl Street, but not reaching to Marlborough Street.
A replacement building was constructed in 1784-86. Main block of an 11-window range, the central 5 windows set forward under a pediment. Wings of 2-window range, with 1-window range links. All 3 storeys with attics, but basement storey(s) at west end of main elevation to Marlborought Street, and to sides and rear. Sash windows. Much altered and extended.

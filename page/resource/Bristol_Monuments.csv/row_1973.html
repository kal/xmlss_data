monument_desc A Roman building at Bedminster Down. Roman pottery, tile fragments and some plaster were found in the area in 1921-22 on the edge of a quarry, 150 yards south of Bridgwater Road. The site was then archaeologically excavated by the University of Bristol Spelaeological Society in 1925 (Prowse, 1923-25 89). The excavation recorded the foundations of walls, plaster, pottery, iron objects, samian pottery, sandstone roof tiles and coins (Tratman 1922, 298). The site extended as far as the Bridgwater Road on the north.
The site was subsequently built over and destroyed.

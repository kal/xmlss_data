monument_desc Brislington Hill House. Aaron Ithell is recorded as purchasing land in 1660 from Rowland Lacy, lord of the manor, part of which subsequently formed the estate of Brislington Hill House. A succession of owners are recorded in the C18, but in 1798 John Hurle, the prominent Quaker and Bristol merchant, purchased the estate and built a new house on the site of an older building. In 1828, his widow Elizabeth, is recorded as living in the property (Ireland manuscripts; BRO St.LB/HM/1a & 1b). After her death the property passed to her neice Susannah who had married Joseph Cooke of "West Town House" in 1805. Their son, Joseph, took the name Cooke-Hurle and lived at Brislington Hill House. The Brislington tithe Award of 1846 records John Hurle as the owner and occupier. The Cooke-Hurle family continued to reside at Brislington Hill House until 1921 when the property was sold to George Gay and converted into flats.
The house was badly damaged during World War II and had to be demolished. The "Gilton House" flats stand on the site of the house.

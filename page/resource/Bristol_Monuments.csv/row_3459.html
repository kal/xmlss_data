monument_desc Stoke House, now Trinity Theological College, located on the northern side of Stoke Hill. The house was constructed as the summerhouse or second residence of the Bristol merchant Robert Cann in the second half of the seventeenth century. The building was constructed as a single depth plan with rooms either side of a central staircase (R. Leech pers. comm.) and there was a stable block to the south-west of the main house (NGR ST 56413 75475) which has features of seventeenth-century date within it (J. Bryant pers. comm.).
The wing to the east was added and the road on the southern side of the plot was moved to its present course in the early eighteenth century. An engraving by Kip records the house at this time. The formal garden was on the west side of the main house while the icehouse lay to the north (at NGR ST 56415 75600 approx..), being now within the gardens of a modern house. There is an orangery of nineteenth-century date 20 metres to the north west of the main house (NGR ST 56356 75539). The building was partly refronted in 1872 by Foster and Wood.
The house has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/31/2012).

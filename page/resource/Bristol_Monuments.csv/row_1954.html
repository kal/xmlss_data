monument_desc A pond with a central island, which was filled in when the old people's home currently occupying the site was built. The date of the feature is not clear; it is recorded by a sketch map amongst the Smyth papers (BRO AC/PL18b) but not by the Bedminster tithe map of 1841 although later maps do show it. The date of creation and function of the pond are unknown and while its form may possibly suggest a medieval date there is currently no firm evidence to support this.
The land was sold to the City by the Smyth family in 1929 (BRO 4627/181a-c) and in the 1960s it was a nature conservation site.

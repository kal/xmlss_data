monument_desc No.17, St. Augustine's Parade. An attached late eighteenth-century house, now an office and shop, restored in 1991. Render with party wall brick stack and slate mansard roof. Double-depth plan. 3 storeys and attic; 2-window range. A C20 shop front, with a banded left-hand pilaster strip to a cornice and parapet; keyed architraves to 6/6-pane sashes.
The interior (though not the ground-floor) was briefly inspected during renovation work, c.1991 by John Bryant. The first-floor contained no significant features. There may have once been a staircase to the second-floor in the rear (i.e. south-west corner). The second-floor comprised two rooms and a closet. The fireplaces were tiled 20th.century examples. Front windows of 18th.century (?) sashes with shutters. Doors with L-hinges and fielded panels. Some panelling opposite the staircase to the top floor. The staircase was open-well with quarter landings, in eighteenth- or nineteenth-century style. The bottom was not cut, suggesting that it never reached the first-floor. The third, or top, floor was covered with a mansard roof front and rear, but a ridge roof in the centre over the stairwell. There was an internal gutter running along the west side, on the floor.
St. Augustine's Parade is a street developed in the medieval period. The party walls with adjacent properties may be of some age.

monument_desc Bank Farm. Lying on the north side of Severn Road, the site of the farm is shown by Taylor's map of the area in 1773 as an open field. Bank Farm had been built in the western part of the field by the time of the 1841 Henbury tithe map, and the apportionment indicates that the farm was owned by Edward Sampson and occupied by William Gregory. Orchards lay to the north of the farm (BRO EP/A/32/22). The Ordnance Survey map of 1915 (Glos sheet LXVII.10) recorded the farm as a single range with associated outbuildings, the remainder being orchard. The farm was demolished in 1960 and fuel storage tanks built. These have since been removed.
The site was visited by R. G. Jackson during the course of a desk-based assessment (SMR 20081) of areas of Bristol City Council owned land north of Avonmouth in December 1996. The site was found to be derelict and the ground had been disturbed by heavy vehicles.

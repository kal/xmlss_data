monument_desc St. Peter's Churchyard, located on the south side of St. Peter's Church (BUAD 187M), but with a strip running up beside the west end of the church, as far as the street. The churchyard was bounded to the south by St. Peter's Hospital (BUAD 9M) and St. Peter's Almshouse (BUAD 10M). The churchyard was in use from at least the thirteenth century. The churchyard was closed to burials by an Order in Council of 18 February 1854 made under the Burial Act of 1852.
The main area of the churchyard was planted as herb garden in the late twentieth century. The burials are believed to remained undisturbed within the site.

monument_desc A probable Roman building lies along the line of the main road, 1800-1900 feet from King's Weston villa (SMR 744) and 400' from the junction with Kingsweston Lane. Pottery (mostly late, and a piece of mended samian, Dragendorf 31), walling, roofing, ashes and a possible flagged floor were discovered. Part of the site probably underlies the grounds of the adjoining school. (Boon 1974) Some 100' along the road, towards the villa, is a possible lime kiln (a pit was found with much lime and pottery).

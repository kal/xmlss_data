monument_desc Redland House, located on the north side of Redland Hill. The house appears to have been in existence since the mid eighteenth century (Bryant 2000) and was first recorded cartographically by a map of the area produced by Sturges in 1817. In 1825 Redland House was owned by John Vivian and occupied by Arthur Foulks (BRO P/HTW/V2(a)) but by the time of the Westbury tithe survey it was owned by Sarah Nayler. Redland House was divided into two properties between the 1840s and 1855, the eastern half retaining the name Redland House and the western becoming Redland Bank. Both Redland Bank and Redland House had formal gardens according to the Ordnance Survey first edition plan surveyed in 1881, although that belonging to Redland Bank was the larger.
Construction of a terrace of housing at the foot of Redland Hill involved the removal of around 60% of Redland House. Redland Collegiate School moved to the building in 1915 from its original site in Clarendon Road but during the Second World War Redland House was requisitioned and the Collegiate School moved to Winterbourne. After the war the building became home to the Redland Training College.
Redland House were listed Grade III but it was removed from the statutory list in 1961 for the construction of new educational buildings.

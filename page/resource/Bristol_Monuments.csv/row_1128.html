monument_desc A tenement of twelfth-century date which was recorded during the archaeological excavation at Dundas Wharf in 1982 (BUAD 344). The tenement was built simultaneously with the tenement to the north (BUAD 1291M) and was built of Pennant sandstone. It apparently had a yard to the rear. The northern and southern boundaries of its plot remained essentially unchanged until the twentieth century but the building was extended westward as land was reclaimed from the river and at least three more rooms had been added by the fifteenth century via enclosure of the yard and new construction. These extensions were entirely independent of the tenement to the north and the plan of the building and its rate of expansion were different (Good 1990/91, 38).

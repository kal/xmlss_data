monument_desc No.16 High Street. In use in 1821 as paper warehouse with ground-floor shop. South corner of High and Mary-le-Port Streets. Attached to south and east. Roof not recorded, but a building of 4 storeys otherwise, with a 1-window range to High Street. Jetties to both first-floor elevations, supported by pair of figure brackets at corner and a scroll bracket halfway along Mary-le-Port Street. Main elevation to High Street, where a left-hand entrance and shop window. Rear entrance at far eastern end of north return wall, which was of 4 storeys. At first-floor, central canted bay with flanking windows, all sashes; full-width moulded string above. Second-floor canted bay an extension on that below, 8-light front and 2-light sides; another string. 8-light third-floor window; eaves above on brackets. At various places the return propped against the opposite property across street.
It appears that this building probably had a gable to High Street. It is shown by O'Neill in 1820 (M2797) as having a transverse gable at the eastern end of the northern return along Mary-le-Port Street. After this, there was a short eastern return wall of 1-window width, with a sash on each of the uppermost 3 floors, and a tiny window high in the ground-floor.

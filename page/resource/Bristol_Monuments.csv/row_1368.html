monument_desc A two-storey, four window range house constructed of Pennant rubble walls with limestone dressings. Built in c.1834 as a new Turnpike House when the original Turnpike road was diverted to the present route of the Bath Road (see 1991M). The building remained in use as a toll gate until the abolition of the Turnpike roads in 1867 after which the building became a public house and is recorded as such on the Ordnance Survey plan of 1884.

The building is extant and continues in use as a public house. Many original features survive including a toll booth recess, now blocked, to one side of the entrance.

The building is Listed Grade II (Listed Building number 901-1/47/2038).

monument_desc Tobacco factory of W. D. & H. O. Wills, located on the north side of East Street, Bedminster. The factory was designed by Sir Frank Wills and opened on the 27 March 1886 (Alford 1973, 135-136). It was designed to allow production to flow through the site from the leaf warehouse to the despatch sheds. Some machinery was brought from the existing factories but most was new. Bedminster No 1 factory was equipped with Belliss and Morcam high-speed steam engines providing power and exhausting into process and steam heating systems (Buchanan & Buchanan 1980, 32). The use of process steam made these engines very economical. A new block was opened at the centre of the site in 1895, constructed at a cost of £7000.
The buildings on Lombard Street survive as well as those at the west end East Street, although both have been converted to shops on the ground floor. The other factory buildings were demolished in the mid-1980s.

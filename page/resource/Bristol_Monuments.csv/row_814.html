monument_desc Llewellin and James Brass Foundry, located on the south side of Castle Green. The works was the largest brass foundry in Bristol, employing 100 men, according to Mr. Lewellin, and produced "brass work of all common kinds, as well as for steam engine mountings, hydraulic engineering, bell founding, and copper work, such as making coppers for breweries, sugar boiling, &c" (Children's Employment Commission 1866, 61-62).
Currently little is known about internal organisation of the factory, but part was excavated in 1989 (BUAD 459) and the remains of a furnace, well, walls and floors were recorded. Archaeological stratification associated with other parts of the brass works are likely to survive beneath Castle Park.

monument_desc Redcliff Caves. A system of caverns cut into the red sandstone (Mercia Mudstone) at the western end of the hill known as Redcliffe.The caves were probably cut for sand to supply the glassworks on Redcliff Wharf during the post-medieval period and extend for some 100 metres into the cliff. Brick columns support the roof of the caves to stabilise the foundations of houses on Redcliff Parade above.
The caves have been investigated on a number of occasions, excavations taking place during the first three years of the Second World War (BUAD 3404) and in the 1960s (BUAD 493). The caves were surveyed in the late 1980s by the Field Archaeology Section of the City of Bristol Museum and Art Gallery (BUAD 481) and have reportedly been further explored in the 1990s by Axbridge Caving Club, leading to the discovery of further chambers. Features have been found inside the caves including wells and graffiti.
The results of the investigations have not been analysed, indeed in most cases the location of the finds and site archives is unknown.

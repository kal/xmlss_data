monument_desc No.71, Old Market Street, located on the southern side of Old Market Street. The building is a rendered single bay house of two storeys and attic and appears to have had a detached kitchen. It was constructed in the early-seventeenth century. The building has a double-depth plan and a basement with access steps down from the footway.
The main, north-west facing, elevation has a left-hand entrance with a rectangular overlight, and a full-width lean-to canopy on two moulded, curved brackets with Tudor roses on the sides, over paired plate-glass sashes; canted first- and second-storey bays have sashes, below a gable with scalloped barge-boards pierced with quatrefoils, side finials and a central pendant. The right-hand return has a six-panel door and side- and overlights beneath paired lintels each with 3-stepped voussoirs. An area at the front of the building is enclosed by wrought-iron railings with a 2-leaf gate in the middle and is paved with Pennant sandstone flags. At the rear the gable to an earlier, adjoining property is cut off short above the current building. The building has a butt-purlin gabled roof, apparently original, but this is pantiled only on the east side.
Internally there is a central winder stair with stick balusters and column newels; rear niche cupboard with curved shelves; front second-floor bolection-moulded fireplace and a rear cast-iron range with pointed-arched panels. On the first-floor landing there is a seventeenth-century panelled cupboard door with butterfly hinges, and in the southern room is a spice cupboard, as well as evidence for a former three-quarter height fireplace. There is a separate stair to the attic storey.
In the mid-nineteenth century the building was owned by a Mrs Godwin, the mother of the architect E. W. Godwin, who lived on the rental income from the building (L. Lambourne Edward William Godwin (1833-1886): aesthetic polymath. In Soros 1999, 20). In 1875 the building was recorded in the street directory as being occupied by William Smith & Sons, decorators.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/12/1655).

monument_desc Westmoreland Farm, located on the northern side of Crow Lane, at its junction with Henbury Road. The farmhouse was constructed in the eighteenth century and formed part of the estate of the local Sampson family which was built up from the late seventeenth century. The farm was let to William Taylor at the time of the Westbury-on-Trym tithe survey of 1841 (BRO EP/A/32/41). The stables were built in the nineteenth century.
The farm was subject to compulsory purchase in the late 1940s and the farmhouse and ancillary buildings were rented as a council house to the McEwen Smith family. The family purchased the farm buildings under the right-to-buy in the 1980s.
The farmhouse and the stables have Grade II listings (Listed Building numbers 901-1/21/1338 and 901-1/21/1339 respectively).

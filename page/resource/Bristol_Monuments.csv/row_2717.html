monument_desc The Guildhall, located on the west side of Broad Street.
A frontage of 69 feet. Holmes' drawing of unknown date (1813?), M2436, shows the old front just as scaffolding was erecting to new front the building. Ground-floor in left and right sections, divided by a vertical line. On the left of the left-hand, a two-centred arch doorway with lamp bracket above, then 2 arched openings that are largely obscured by the planking of the fence. On the right, an arched doorway to left, another shorter one at the right, and in the centre a plaque or window. String course above the left-hand ground-floor. A decorative frieze above the right-hand area, with, immediately above, a large traceried window reaching two storeys in height, almost to the top of the wall. In the centre of the left half of the upper wall, twin two-centred arch windows, sharing a central jamb; at the left end, arms in a square frame; at the right end, a similar but larger feature below a niche containing the figure of Charles II (M2440). At second-floor, twin arched windows in a shared square head. One parapet common to both halves of the facade. The wall was equal in height to the late C18 or early C19 buildings immediately to the north.
Rowbotham shows the building after the refronting (M2437). The whole front in stucco or ashlar, with a moulded cornice-parapet. The ground-floor a symmetrical arrangement of openings with two-centred arches and deep mouldings: 3 doorways, the central possibly false, and 2 lower openings, perhaps only niches. Above a string course, 2 large traceried windows with two-centred heads, the northernmost perhaps the original surviving from the earlier facade. Each window of 8 lights in 2 groups of 4, rising to 2 arches within the uppermost tracery. Arms at each end of the elevation, with, between the windows, a statue within an ogee-headed niche; above, a quatrefoil.
The interior of the court illustrated by O'Neill and Johnson (M2438-M2439). Arch-braced roof of at least 7 bays, at right angles to the street, with at its end, one of the large traceried windows, presumably the northernmost. Trusses springing off of corbel heads. In the side wall to the right of the window, an arcade of 2 arches.
The General Gaol Delivery, Court of Nisi Prius, Quarter Sessions, the Sheriffs Courts, Court of Conscience & Request, and Elections for members were held at the Guildhall (Braikenridge Notes, A.74x).
Replaced by the 1843 Guildhall, which occupies a longer frontage.

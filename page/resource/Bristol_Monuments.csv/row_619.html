monument_desc Granary, located on Welsh Back. The building is a seven-storey granary and was constructed between 1869 and 1871 for Wait and James to a design by the Bristol architectural practice of Ponton and Gough. The building is in the "Bristol Byzantine" style with patterned brick elevations, using local Cattybrook bricks (Crick 1975, 40-41; Gomme et al 1979, 375-377; Lord & Southam 1983, 90-94). According to The Builder (3 June 1871) the lifts were incorporated into the corners of the building, the openings in internal niches, and the grain sacks were placed "into carts upon moveable skids, sliding out of the round holes under the first floor string course" (quoted in Crick 1975, 41).
The building was surveyed by the Royal Commission on Historical Monuments (England) in 1988 (BUAD 902; Anon. 1989 32).
The building has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/16/341).

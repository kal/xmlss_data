monument_desc The Roman settlement of Abona, located in the area of present day Sea Mills. Peter Ellis (1987) reviewed the available evidence for the settlement as part of the published report on the archaeological excavations at Abon House in the mid 1960s and argues that the initial activity on the site was military. He rejects the case made by G. C. Boon (1949) for a Claudian origin for the settlement, and suggests that the military presence may have been established as early as c.55AD. A fort was presumably built alongside the road between Sea Mills and Bath, although no military site has yet been excavated. This may have been a stores or supply depot serving a naval detachment operating in the Bristol Channel area. The fort is argued to have remained until 85AD at the latest and the ceramic evidence suggests a break in occupation from then until the founding of a civilian settlement at the beginning of the 2nd century AD.

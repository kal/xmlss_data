monument_desc Bristol Sailors Home, located on the northern side of The Grove, to the rear of No.29 Queen Square. The Bristol Sailors Home was constructed by the Sailors Home Committee from 1851 (Pilkington, 2000) and was a conversion of the stable/warehouse, counting house and kitchen of No.29 Queen Square (BUAD 1463M) together with the western of a row of four houses constructed c1778 on The Grove. The works included reconstruction of the elevation to The Grove (BRO 40311/B/1 fol.17), the creation of an entrance hall towards the east side of the building at ground-floor and the insertion of a chimney stack against the west wall (Leech, 2002). The shell walls of the c1778 house formed the eastern rooms of the structure.
The building was recorded in a photograph of the 1870s, and as completed was a three-storey, four-bay structure with four hipped pantile roofs, three retained from the stable/warehouse to the west of the main entrance door and the other the roof of the c1778 house to the east. The main, south-facing elevation was of rendered rubble stone. The ground-floor level was of rusticated ashlar with moulded voussoirs above the window openings. The main entrance door is located towards the eastern side of the building, between the third and fourth ground-floor window and its opening is surrounded by a moulding. The upper floors of the elevation were plain except for a pilaster which extended to the base of the parapet and divided the western bay from the rest. The fenestration appears to have comprised large sashes in all of the windows although the window opening at the east end of the second floor was blocked. The legend "SAILOR'S HOME" was painted between the first- and second-floor windows.
The original internal organisation of the building is not currently clear. At the front of the building, to the west of the entrance hall there was a single large room at each floor. The ceiling of the ground-floor room was supported on cast iron columns and may have been an association area. The upper floors are likely to have been dormitory space. At first floor in the east range (the former counting house) a metal safe for the inmates valuables was constructed against the party wall. During the twentieth century a number of alterations were made to the interior, including the insertion of partitions to create individual bedrooms in the large first and second-floor rooms and suspended ceilings on the ground floor. These alterations were removed in 2002. The site remained in use as a sailors home until the 1990s. It was converted to offices in 2003.
The building was surveyed by Jayne Pilkington in 2000 (BUAD 3627; Pilkington 2000) and by Dr. Roger Leech in 2002 (BUAD 3913; Leech 2002; 2003). There are photographs of the interior of the building before its conversion to offices within the Sites and Monuments Collection of Ground-based Photographs.
The building has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/42/204).

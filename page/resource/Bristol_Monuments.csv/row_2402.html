monument_desc Washingpool Farm. A farm was depicted on this site (ST 53414 81560) in 1830 (Ordnance Survey 1:63360). The range of buildings was recorded in 1915 (Ordnance Survey County Series 1:2500-scale plan, Gloucestershire Sheet LXVII.10). It was a farm with a number of outbuildings, probably of post-medieval date. Much of the post-medieval site now lies beneath the A403 and was destroyed by construction of a reservoir associated with the Sevalco Works. Walls and brick paved surfaces survive west of the A 403.
A small "island" shown by the maps to the south of the site was reached from the farm by a small footbridge, and may represent an earlier possible moated site (ST 53423 81511) (1). The area enclosed by the ditch measures 20 metres north-south by 27 metres east-west. The site of the farm is largely overgrown with trees, although not built over, and the "moat" is untouched. Substantial archaeological deposits and structures probably survive.
The site was visited by R. G. Jackson during the course of a desk-based assessment (SMR 20081) of areas of Bristol City Council owned land north of Avonmouth in December 1996. The ditch surrounding the site of the possible moated site was dry and was up to 1 metre deep in some places. The site was still overgrown by trees.

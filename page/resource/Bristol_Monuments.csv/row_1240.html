monument_desc Arno's Castle Triumphal Arch, located between Bath Road and Junction Road. The triumphal arch was built c.1760 for the local brass founder William Reeve, possibly designed by James Bridges, and originally formed an entrance arch to Arno's Castle (HER 1899M). It was intended that the arch would house figures taken from Newgate. The arch forms a free-standing wall with tall central round archway flanked by plain arched doors. The central range is a third higher than the sides and broken forward slightly. The rear elevation is much simpler and there is a carving of the City's coat of arms above main arch (Ison, 1952 186-187). The arch was moved to the entrance to Arno's Court Bath House in 1912 and has apparently been moved c.10 metres closer to the main Bath Road since, presumably when the Bath House was removed to Portmeirion.
The arch has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building Number 901-1/56/428). It was originally designated as a Scheduled Ancient Monument but was later descheduled.

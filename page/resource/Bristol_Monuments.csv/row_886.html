monument_desc Lawford's Gate, a town gate standing at the eastern end of Old Market Street. A gate probably stood in this position from at least the twelfth century, and "Laffordesyate" is mentioned in the 1373 perambulation of the County of Bristol. The drawings of the gate show what appears to be a structure which is late-medieval in date, suggesting that it was rebuilt, or at least refronted, at that date.
A single wide entrance with a depressed moulded drop arch. Facing east (?), a mullion and transom window with two pointed lights in a square head, beneath a drip moulding, with, to either side, a niche containing the statue of a king, each niche decorated with crocketing. Above the first-floor was a string or cornice. In the gable there was a window or opening, below a pentice roof. It appears from a drawing of 1745 by James Stewart (the only known depiction) that the gate was set back slightly from the line of the town wall to either side.
The gate was removed in 1768 but the two statues have survived, and are now in the collections of Bristol Museum and Art Gallery.

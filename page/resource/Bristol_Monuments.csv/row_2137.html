monument_desc The King's Orchard, part of the precinct of Bristol Castle. The orchard was located to the south of the Castle Ditch, roughly in the area bounded by modern Passage Street, Tower Hill and the river Avon. It was recorded by the 1373 perambulation of the County of Bristol (Harding 1930, 162-163). The perambulation mentions an orchard ditch on the west side of "Seint Phelip is lane" (now Tower Hill). The site appears still to have been open land at the time of Millerd's 1673 map, although this does show a mound of unknown function close to the western end of the Castle Ditch.

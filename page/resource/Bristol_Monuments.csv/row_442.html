monument_desc House located at the eastern end of Temple Churchyard. The building was a two-storey stone rubble building of probable seventeenth-century date (possibly No.6 Ash Lodge by 1906). There was a single three-light mullioned window to each storey and the ground floor was sunken. The building was roofed with clay pantiles.
The building may be identifiable on Millerd's 1673 map of Bristol and seems later to have been subdivided, with the southern end possibly being demolished. By 1906 the property was in the ownership of Bristol Municipal Charities (Costello & Burleigh 1997, 47). It had been demolished by 1959.
The foundations of the building are likely to survive beneath the footway and pavement of Church Street at the east end of Temple Gardens, and the boundary wall of the churchyard here may have reused part of its west wall. This wall had been repointed and appeared also to have been partly rebuilt. This possible remnant element was demolished at the end of the 1990s when the southern wall of Temple Churchyard was reconstructed.

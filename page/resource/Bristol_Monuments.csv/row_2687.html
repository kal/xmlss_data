monument_desc A possible dovecote of medieval date, located some 40 metres to the east of Partition Street. The structure was recorded during archaeological excavation in 2000 (BUAD 3822). The dating evidence recovered was not sufficient to establish a close date for the structure but it was constructed in the medieval period. It was a circular building of Brandon Hill Grit and measured approximately 8 metres in diameter. It had stone buttress 1 metre wide on the southern side. Beneath the centre of the floor there was a stone drain which discharged into an area with a flagstone floor on the southern side of the structure, between the buttresses. The building was demolished and the stone quarried for reuse. Again the evidence could give a date for demolition but the structure was overlain by demolition rubble containing eighteenth-century pottery.

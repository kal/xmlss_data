monument_desc Nos.9-11 Chantry Road, pair of attached houses formerly a single farm house constructed in c.1670. The first cartographic depiction of the building is on de Wilstar's survey of the Manor of Clifton, 1746. Plumley and Ashmead's plan of Bristol, 1828 shows the building by which time it was named Nursery Villa and was associated with the adjoining Durdham Down Nursery (SMR 22123) managed by John Miller. An advertisement dated 1827 for John Miller, Nurseryman, Seedsman and Florist, shows the square outer bays of the property (listing description). In the late 1830s Nursery Villa and Durdham Down Nursery was taken over by James Garaway who later divided the house into the present two properties formerly Nos.1 and 2 Nursery Villas now Nos.9 and 11 Chantry Road. In the late nineteenth century or very early twentieth century the nursery was relocated and the area developed for housing. Chantry Road was created at this time and was formed from the entrance drive to the nursery.
The building is extant and has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/34/10036).

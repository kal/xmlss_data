monument_desc Manilla Hall, located on the east side of Clifton Down Road. The building was erected in 1763 by Sir William Draper and named in honour of his capture of Manila in October 1762.
The house was a nine-bay, three-storey structure with a tetrastyle portico with ionic columns which extended to second storey level but had no pediment. The ground floor was rusticated with flat relieving arches above the windows and entrances arches with open pediments above at either end of the house, each adding half a bay to its width.
Draper erected a cenotaph to the 79th Regiment (SMR 20126) and an obelisk commemorating William Pitt the elder (SMR 20127) in front of the house (Dreaper 1998, 1).
Draper died in 1787 at Bath and the house was inherited by his surviving daughter. It was later bought by William Gordon, who lived at the property adjacent on the north side, and passed into the hands of the Miles family of Bristol bankers in the nineteenth-century. Sir Joseph Weston bought the property in 1892 and parcelled some of its grounds for building plots, while Manilla House itself was sold to the Dames de la Mere de Dieu and used as a Roman Catholic school. The obelisk and cenotaph were removed to their present position when the order wanted to dispose of them. The house was demolished in the early twentieth century Dreaper 1998, 23-24).

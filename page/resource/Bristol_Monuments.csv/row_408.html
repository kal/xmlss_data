monument_desc The Assize Courts Hotel, No.15 Small Street, located on the north-east side of Small Street. The building was constructed in the seventeenth century and was the house of Humphrey Brown in 1630.

Sir Thomas Langton owned the property in the later seventeenth century and at his death, in 1673, it passed to his widow, Lady Elizabeth, who died in 1703. In 1704 the property belonged to Robert Langton and Ann his wife, Robert being the youngest son of Sir Thomas, who sold it to Richard Champneys in that year. A deed of 1761 refers to the whole of the property as belonging to Richard Champneys, together with "the new buildings now or recently in course of erection". These stood where the present Hotel stands. By the 1820s the premises were occupied by John Matthew Gutch, printer of Felix Farley's Bristol Journal. Hirst described the building in 1927 as follows: "it contains on the ground floor a large kitchen and other offices, one of which is a strong room; on the first floor is the state room or dining room in which stood the chimney piece; there is a strong room and another small room adjoining. This state room is of fine proportions, 27 feet by 20 feet and 13 feet high. On the second floor are a number of bedrooms now disused and in poor repair. underneath the kitchens and extending right out under the new buildings to Small Street is a large cellar referred to in the old deeds as 'the great vault'. An inscribed tablet, apparently removed from some disused burial ground, is built into the wall of the kitchen." The building contained the 'Five Senses' chimney-piece (1007A).

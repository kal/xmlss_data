monument_desc Croft Dale, Hepburn Road, a two storey attached house of mid nineteenth century date to the rear of 100-102 Stokes Croft.
The building is rendered with limestone quoins and detailing including an arched doorway in the front/south elevation with incised name 'Croft Dale' on limestone and four pain fan light..
The building is first depicted on Ashmead's 1855 survey at which time it is shown as a semi detached pair of dwellings adjoining the Carriageworks (1466M) to the north and a north-south aligned building to the southwest. A similar arrangement is shown on later nineteenth century plans.
By the time of the 1950 Ordnance Survey the structure is depicted as a single dwelling attached only to the industrial building to the north.
When the site was visited in July 2008 the building was extant with the open area to the south laid to lawn. The door and fanlight over appeared to be original, although all windows have been replaced. Features associated with the former attached linear building depicted on the 1855 plan, including door openings and brick stringer course, were visible in the west garden rubble stone boundary wall.

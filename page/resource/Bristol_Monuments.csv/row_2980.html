monument_desc The Moravian Burial Ground at the rear of the Moravian Church in Upper Maudlin Street. The cemetery was located on the south side of the Moravian Church and was enclosed by buildings belonging to the brethren. On the north side was the chapel and on the west, stabling. On Lower Maudlin Street were the Old Sisters houses, Minister's house and the school, while on Blackfriars was the Old Brethren's house. A path ran around the edge of the burial ground and another ran across it from the church to the Old Brethren's house. The burial ground was commended as a model of good practice in G. T. Clark's report to the General Board of Health (Clark 1850, 214). The graves were indicated by stone markers arranged in rows. There were a number of deep brick burial shafts and one vault running roughly across the centre of the plot, which were revealed during a watching brief in 1993.
The burial ground was partly cleared in the early 1970s, although the number of burials removed seems to have been small. The majority of the burial ground was finally cleared and destroyed during groundworks for an extension to the Dental Hospital in August 1993. A watching brief maintained during the work recorded approximately 220 interments dating between the eighteenth and the late nineteenth or early twentieth centuries (BUAD 471). Further work was undertaken on the western side of the cemetery in 1999 (BUAD 3586; Pilkington 2000).

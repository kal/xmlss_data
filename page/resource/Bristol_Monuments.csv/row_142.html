monument_desc The Old Library, King Street. Originally the library was housed in an older building, but this had fallen into disrepair by the 1730s. Consequently the Bristol Corporation took the decision to erect a new building which was built between 1738 and 1740, possibly to a design by James Paty. The total cost is recorded in the Audit Books as £1,3081 8s. 1d. James Paty provided the ornamental carving.
The library was housed in the two larger rooms at first floor level. The building remained the home for the City Library until 1907 when it moved to the present building in College Green. The interior has since been remodelled and is currently occupied by a restaurant. The coat of arms in the pediment and the panels above the first floor windows have been removed, and the carved wooden fireplace from the first floor room in the west range was taken to College Green as part of the library's move. Internally major features survive, including the oak staircase. The building remains in the freehold ownership of Bristol City Council but is under lease.
The Old Library has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building Number 901-1/16/604). The wall, railings and gateway were originally listed separately but are now included in the main listing for the building.

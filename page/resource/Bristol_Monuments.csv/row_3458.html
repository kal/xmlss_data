monument_desc Icehouse, Kings Weston House. The building was constructed after 1772 and replaced an earlier icehouse (Niall Phillips Associates, 1985) and is situated some metres to the north-east of the main house. It is of brick with a entrance tunnel 3 metres long by 2.1 metres high and 1.2 metres wide which had inner and outer doors. "The E[ast] side door post and top frame member are in the ice-house pit itself, they are oak and the butt hinge recesses can be seen" (Beamon & Road, 1990 188). This entrance tunnel leads into a domed circular chamber with an internal diameter of 5.5 metres. The icehouse has three walls, an outer stone wall 0.53 metres thick, separated from a brick wall 0.23 metres thick by a 4 inch gap (0.1 metres). Inside this wall is another brick wall 0.1 metres thick. Opposite the entrance the chamber has a rectangular hole 0.46 metres wide by 0.32 metres high which has been interpreted as a ventilation feature (Beamon & Roaf, 1990 188). The icehouse was surveyed by G. G. Locke in 1975 (SMR 20943).

monument_desc No.21 Wine Street. The building was located on the north side of the street, the third to the east of Pithay. It was a four-storey, two-bay jettied house, although it appears to have originally been two properties, built as a single development, which were later made into one. The ground floor was a shopfront with entrances at either end. The first floor fenestration was formed by two bays containing sashes, while both the second and third floors contained three-light sashes. The building had a double hipped roof and there was a chimney stack on the west side.
The building, occupied in the later nineteenth-century by E. John's Hat Shop, was demolished in the 1880s for road widening.

monument_desc Aladdin Gas Holder, located on the north side of Temple Back. The gas holder was part of a gasworks constructed at Temple Back by John Gostling for the Bristol Gas Light Company between August 1816 and 1818 (BRO 28777/G/L/4/1). It was sub-rectagular in shape, a plan of July 1818 indicating its maximum internal measurements were 59 feet (18 metres) from south-west to north-east by 34 feet (10.4 metres) wide. The walls were of Pennant sandstone bonded in a grey-yellow mortar with charcoal and ash inclusions and were approximately 1 metre thick with a batter towards the base. A test in March 1817 identified a leakage problem and the base of the gas holder was puddled. The required capacity also seems to have been increased to 36,500 in 1818 (BRO 28777/G/L/4/3), making it the largest gas holder in the country at the time. The gasworks closed in 1821 when the Bristol Gas Light Company moved its operations to a new site in Avon Street. The site was leased for two years, then sold in 1824 to Tothill & Co., cement manufacturers. Cartographic evidence suggests that the gas holder was incorporated into another building but survived into the 1850s before being demolished.
The foundations of the western half of the gas holder was recorded during an archaeological watching brief on the site in 2004 (BUAD 4133; Longman 2005).

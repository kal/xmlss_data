monument_desc The E. S. and A. Robinson building, located at the corner of Victoria Street and Redcliff Street. The was constructed in 1876 to a design by W. B. Gingell (Gomme et al. 1979, 336-337). It was of five storeys plus attic on the Victoria Street elevation and had a semi-basement. The ground floor was in a Byzantine style which was echoed in the upper floors and there was a distinctive domed tower with a lantern above the junction of Redcliff Street and Victoria Street.
The western half of the building was damaged by bombing during the Second World War (1939-1945) (Warne 1943, 13) and it was demolished to make way for the office block which currently occupies the site, No.1 Redcliff Street, in the 1960s. The decorative frieze from around the tower has been preserved, and is in the City Museum's collections.

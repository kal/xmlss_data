monument_desc Downend Park Farm. A seventeenth-century (possibly c.1630) farmhouse of two storeys, possibly originally with large dormer gables. According to a survey by Linda Hall (SMR 20152) the central front door opens on to a lobby, behind which is a dairy. One side of the lobby is the kitchen and on the other a parlour. There is a 2 1/2 storey porch which contains the stair, which rises to the attic.
Inside the building is a kitchen dresser of late seventeenth-century date, while panelling and fireplace surrounds in the parlour date to the mid eighteenth-century. An outbuilding to the east of the house has remnants of tie-beam roof trusses and may also date to the early seventeenth-century. The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building No.1410). It was restored c.1989.

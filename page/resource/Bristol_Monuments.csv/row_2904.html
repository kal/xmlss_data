monument_desc Church of St. George, Brandon Hill, located on the hillside between Great George Street and Charlotte Street. The site was purchased for £2400 using the church rate. The church was built in the early nineteenth century, to a design by Sir Robert Smirke, as a chapel at ease to St. Augustine-the-Less. It cost of £10042, met by the Parliamentary Commissioners, and was consecrated in 1823. It could accommodate 1416 worshippers, with 496 of the sittings rented and 920 free (Ralph & Cobb 1991, 27).
The church is constructed of ashlar and Pennant sandstone rubble, with limestone dressings. It has a flat roof with hipped edges.
The original organ was built by John Smith senior of the Bristol organ builders John Smith and Sons. It was replaced with a new organ by John Smith junior in 1854.
An Order in Council of 18 February 1854 made under the Burial Act of 1852 imposed the restriction that burials were "to be discontinued within five yards of the church, or any housewall [sic] in the churchyard." In addition a restriction that only one body could be buried in a grave was imposed.
The church was declared redundant in 1984 but not deconsecrated. It was subsequently converted to act as a music venue.
The church has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/10/34).

monument_desc Gaunt's Ham Park, located on the north-western side of Lincoln Street. The need for a park in the area had been identified by the 1880s and a site known as Gaunt's Ham, lying behind terraced houses on Lincoln Street and adjacent to the Wain Brook, was approved for laying out by the Sanitary Committee on 18 August 1887. The land was owned by the Bristol Corporation and the Committee paid an annual rent of £40. On 21 August 1890 the purchase of a gateway at Highgrove, Totterdown (for £12 10s 0d) for the Elizabeth Street entrance to the park was authorised, and the park opened in the same year. Installation of a second gate for the Tenby Street entrance was agreed on 5 March 1891. The park layout appears to have been designed by the Bristol Corporation's Surveyor, George Ashmead and tenders for the undertaking the works were then invited. That by August Kruss of £653 was accepted on 12 May 1891. The Bristol Member of Parliament and former chairman of The Anchor Society Sir Joseph Dodge Weston donated a drinking fountain for the park which was erected by the Elizabeth Street entrance. This was manufactured by Macfarland and Co. and carried an inscription reading "Presented by Sir Joseph D. Weston M.P. February 1892".
The park originally covered an area of around 5300 square metres and had an adjacent recreation ground at its south-west end. Further planting of trees and shrubs by was agreed on 3 March 1892 at a cost of £72/12/0.
The park was extended along its south-east side when the site of the Lincoln Street terraced houses was incorporated into the park after their demolition. During the Second World War an air raid shelter (SMR 21889), an underground tunnel shelter approximately 9 metres square, was constructed at the south-west end of the site.
The layout of paths has not been substantially altered since the early twentieth century and the original gate piers at the Elizabeth Street and Tenby Street entrances are extant, although the gates have been removed from the Elizabeth Street piers. The drinking fountain has also been removed.

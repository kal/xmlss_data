monument_desc Redcliff Poor House, or William Canynges Almshouse, located on the west side of Redcliff Hill. Jean Rocque's 1742 plan of Bristol indicates that the almshouse stood parallel to, but was set back a little from, the highway on Redcliff Hill. The almshouse is reputed to have been founded in the early 1440s by William Canynges (Manchee 1831, 56). After the reformation the almshouse appears to have been managed by the vestry of St. Mary Redcliffe and supported using parish funds.
The building was demolished and rebuilt slightly to the west in 1804 by the Bristol docks company as part of the construction of the New Cut "under the powers of their act, and at their own expense" (Manchee 1831, 57-58).

monument_desc Kensington House, Bath Road, Brislington. The house was built in 1822 by John Brown. In the late 1840s, Brown sold the house to Richard Jenkins Poole-King, a merchant from Bristol, who lived there until his death in 1874. In 1935 it was acquired by St. Christopher's Church who used it as a club house. During World War II the house was used by the Territorial Army. It was subsequently used as business premises and was demolished in 1973. The present PDSA building occupies the site. The coach house and stables are extant.

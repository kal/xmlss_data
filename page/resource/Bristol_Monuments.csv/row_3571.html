monument_desc Brislington Regional War Room, located at the southern end of the Flowers Hill trading Estate, Flowers Hill, Brislington. The Civil Defence Act 1948, enacted in the context of the developing Cold War, divided the UK into twelve mainland regional and four London sub-regional areas, each to have its own war room. These were often sited in government estates (Cocroft & Thomas 2003, 197) and were built between the spring of 1953 and 1956 to a standard design (McCamley 2002, 152-155). Flowers Hill was the war room for the south-west region, Region No.7.
The building is a two-storey structure, apparently constructed of brick (though presumably with an internal structure of reinforced concrete) and measuring 23 metres square. The exterior of the building is largely covered by foliage trained over it, obscuring detail of its construction but it has three large ventilation towers at the east end of the roof, probably serving the toilets, standby generator and air conditioning plant. The lower level is below ground.
The original internal layout survives. Internally the building has a "ring" corridor on each level circling around a central map room. Access to each of the rooms is off this corridor and each major area of civil administration (eg. Medical Officer of Health, Port of Bristol Authority etc.) was allocated a room.
The policy under which the war rooms were built was reviewed soon after the construction programme was completed. In 1962 The Flowers Hill war room was replaced by two new sub-regional command bunkers in Devon and Gloucestershire and was thereafter relegated to a minor civil defence role. The war room was abandoned in 1981 after the Home Office increased the rent required from Avon County Council from £400 to £4000 per annum (McCamley 2002, 207).
The war room is extant and the original internal layout survives. The fittings and furniture associated with several functions are apparently also still in situ (McCamley 2002, 157-158). The building was surveyed by Nick Catford in the late-1990s (SMR 21436) and photographs from the survey are held by the National Monuments Record under the file number AA98/18033-18034. There are also photographs of the exterior in the building in the SMR Collection of Ground-based Photographs.

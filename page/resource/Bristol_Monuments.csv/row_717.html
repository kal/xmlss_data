monument_desc Rupert House, on the south side of Upper Church Lane, facing Lower Church Lane. This was a stone building of seventeenth-century date which had been terraced into the hillside. It was named, according to one source after Prince Rupert (Winstone 1987). It was apparently renovated in the mid eighteenth-century and again in the nineteenth-century. Perry Road, built in the 1870s is reported to have destroyed the view of the city from the house. The building and the Llan House was turned into a single property in the 1880s when the photographer , William Harvey Barton, resident at Llan House on the north side of Rupert House expanded his business. The buildings seem to have been demolished in the mid-1960s.

monument_desc Brislington National School, located on the east side of School Road, Brislington. The school was built was built in 1859. The plans for the school (SMR 20539) are deposited with the Somerset Record Office (SRO DD/EDS C/1404 S.B.D. Plans 5228) and are signed by N. Williams, Moses Webley, Thomas James and John Gray. The building was in a Victorian Gothic style of and was constructed of stone with a grey slate roof. The sides of the windows were edged in plain yellow brick, while over the top there were alternate red and yellow bricks. The doors were of wood. On the roof there was a weather-vane and a bell which was enclosed in ornate framework. The masters' and a mistresses' houses were sited at opposite ends of the building.
In the front of the building, and fronting on to School Road, there was a yard with a water pump. There was also an upper sloping playground. The school was built on the site of an older school and the additional land required for the new school was donated by James Ireland in November 1858. The school suffered bomb damage in 1941 and was demolished.

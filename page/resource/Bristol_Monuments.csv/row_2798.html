monument_desc A pair of houses. Attached. One front gable each. 3 storeys and attics; jettied; 1-window ranges. Haulingway entrance at left-hand of left property, cross-window to right. Right-hand entrance to right-hand property, sash to left, cross-window at left-hand end. Total of 8 brackets to the first-floor jetty across the pair, most with posts beneath, but 1 bracket removed by haulingway entry. One bay at all upper storeys of each property, 8-light fronts to first-floor left and attics, 10-light to second-floors. Sashes to first-floor right. Gabled mini-roofs to attic bays. Brackets to all eaves; pendants to gable apexes. Posts to ends and centre at the first-floor, possibly representing removed brackets. In 1821 the left-hand premises were occupied by John Hemming, wharfinger.
By the 1930s the haulingway was 2 storeys in height, though no wider. Only 1 bay survived, at the first-floor of the right-hand house. On the ground-floor of the same building there survived on the right a 16-panel C17 door in a frame decorated with carved jambs, with brackets above; to the left was a 8/8 pane sash. The entire ground-floor was fronted in brick. P. E. W. Street's photograph of the rear shows that the roofs were connected by a cross-roof. The first and second floors were jettied. A pair of sashes had replaced the left-hand (i.e.eastern) first-floor window; upper storeys had retained their earlier fenestration, viz. 10-light to each at second-floor, 6-light to the right-hand attic, 3-light to the left-hand. Street notes that the buildings were destroyed by bombing on 24 November 1940.

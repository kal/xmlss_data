monument_desc Nos.14-16 Christmas Street A group of four houses located on the south and south-east side of Christmas Street, opposite the end of Narrow Lewins Mead. The 3 westerly examples survived until c.1970. All appear to have been refronted. The rears, to the Frome, were recorded by O'Neill in 1823 (M2913). Westernmost (later No.16) of 2 storeys with hipped roof, 1-window range, ground-floor not visible, 3-light first-floor window. Next (No.15) of 4 storeys with hipped (?) roof, 2-window range, sashes except right-hand ground-floor, which 2-light or double sash; strings between first/second and second/third floors; 2 top storeys slate-hung; small left-hand ground-floor extension. This and the next (No.14) above river arches of unknown exact function. No.14 of 4 storeys and attic, gable and part-gable to rear; 2-window range; second-floor jettied, strings over first to third floors; top 3 storeys slate-hung; lateral stack(s) on west side; 1 window to attic; all sashes; window boxes to first-floor. The rear of the next building to the east jettied out over the river; 4 storeys and attic, gable visible; 1-window range, mostly sashes, but small second window at far left-hand of ground-floor; strings between all floors, possibly representing small jetties; whole wall slate-hung.

monument_desc Ebenezer Chapel, 683 Fishponds Road. A Nonconformist chapel constructed c.1880. An inscription at the front of the building reportedly read 'Methodist Free Church 1880 Opened 1881'. The chapel is constructed in the Gothic style and consists of Pennant sandstone with ashlar dressings and Roman tiles covering the pitched roof. Internally the building contains a mock vaulted ceiling with ornate iron chandelier mounts. At the time of a survey of the building in 2008 (Corcos, 2008) an internal wooden gallery supported by two ornamental pillars survived. Access to this gallery was provided by two spiral stone staircases flankning the main front entrance.
The chapel was known locally as the 'Clock Chapel' because of the clock on the front elevation below a bellcote.
The chapel first appears in the street directories in 1899 when it is listed as the 'Methodist Free Church'. Other directory records name the chapel; 'United Methodist Church' (1909), 'United Methodist Chapel' (1914) and 'Ebenezer Methodist Chapel' (1934).
Religious worship ceased at the chapel in the 1960s when it was amalgamated with the Trinity Weslyan Church at 800 Fishponds Road. A combined service to mark the closure of the chapel was held in February 1964.
Following the closure of the chapel the building came into the possession of M.W.M. Motors who converted the building to a car-tyre and exhaust centre. (Townsend, 2004).

monument_desc St. Augustine's Hall. A warehouse occupying the whole of the north-west side of St. Augustine's Yard and extending back as far as the rears of Nos.20 to 24a Orchard Street. Large 18th/19th.century warehouse, constructed in three or four phases. Four storeys; seven bays. Rubble contruction, mostly Pennant Sandstone, with brick details. Grey, white and whitish mortars identified. Seven parallel hipped pantile roofs, one above each bay. Cellars to four left-hand bays, each similar and interconnected, with rubble walls, brick barrel vaults and Pennant flagstone floors. In some areas the ground and first floors were knocked together. The earliest part comprised the two left-hand bays, with curved east corner; these bays were set forward of the remaining five; original north-east external wall partly survived. Possibly contemporary was the fourth bay, also with a curved corner, but whose facade was set back from that of the left pair of bays. It also had a former external wall surviving, the south-west. The third bay of the warehouse was formed by roofing over the yard between the earlier buildings and inserting floors. The three right-hand bays appeared to be the latest build, and were of reduced depth. They had concrete floors, whereas the other bays were floored with Pennant flagstones. There were an assortment of doors and windows at all storeys of the seven-bay facade. At the north-east end of bay 2 was a lead rainwater-head, bearing the initials ' R S ' above the date 1774. The various storeys were linked by vehicle ramps. This had been Bristol's first multi-storey car park. Much of the building was supported internally by walls between the bays, some with pierced by arches, but there were also steel stanchions in places. Cast-iron columns supported the roof between the first and second and third and fourth bays. Timber roofs, mostly carried on collared trusses, but the third bay with braced collars. Top floor lit additionally by skylights. Some hoists at the front of the building.
The Bristol SMR (BSMR 6159) notes that a building (possibly this one), consisting of two houses and land on which was a warehouse and brass foundry with entrances to Denmark Street and Orchard Street, advertised for sale in the Bristol Journal, 30 March 1771.

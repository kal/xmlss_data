monument_desc St. Matthias Park. The park is located on the north side of Redcross Street. In the early 1880s a scheme for the creation of a new road, St. Matthias Park, between Redcross Street and the Broad Weir led the Corporation to compulsorily purchase the burial ground. The Open Spaces Act or 1881, which allowed for closed urban burial grounds to be converted to use as public parks, prompted the city council to consider it as a candidate for "an open space or Play-ground for the inhabitants of that poor and populous neighbourhood" (BRO Sanitary Committee Minutes 15 June 1882). Initially, c.1882, a recreation ground was created in the north-west corner of the existing park, the remaining area being purchased from the Streets Improvement Committee in 1884.
The new park was laid out by Messrs. Parker & Son, with the City's surveyor supervising the work, and was formally opened by the Lord Mayor on 24 June 1886.

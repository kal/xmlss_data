monument_desc No.8 King Street, located on the south side of King Street. The house was constructed in 1665 as one of a pair with No.7 King Street (BUAD 11M). The building is a single-bay, jettied house with a gabled pantile roof. The main entrance is on the east side of the building and is reached by a set of four stone steps shaerd with No.8. On the west side of the doorway there is a lagre bay window. A pantiled pentice roof, which runs the full width of Nos.7 and 8, divides the ground from the first floor. The building is likely to have had pentices on the upper floors but these have apparently been removed. The fenestration at both first and second floors consists of six over six pane sash windows. The attic is lit by a smaller six over six pane sash.
The building was refenestrated in the eighteenth- or nineteenth-century. Restoration between 1967 and 1976, when it and No.8 formed the offices of Moxley, Jenner and Partners, architects, revealed the reuse of ship's timbers as structural elements within the building. Some laths were also found to be reused barrel staves.
The building has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/16/599).

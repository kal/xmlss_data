monument_desc Site of nineteenth-century buildings, at Severn Road, Hallen Marsh. The site is recorded in 1769 (B. Donne Map of the country 11 miles around Bristol), although not by a 1773 survey of the area (BRO 29351). The 1841 tithe survey shows three enclosures containing two buildings. Only one building remained on the site by the time of the 1915 Ordnance Survey map, however. A square hedge enclosure was still in existence in 1975.
The site was investigated by Wessex Archaeology during archaeological work associated with the construction of the Second Severn Crossing and found to date at the earliest to the late eighteenth-century (Barnes et al 1993, 22).
The site (Area 1; Site 17) was visited by R. G. Jackson during the course of a desk-based assessment (SMR 20081) of areas of Bristol City Council owned land north of Avonmouth in December 1996. It was found that no evidence of the buildings or enclosures remained. The site has probably been completely destroyed by works associated with the construction of the bridge carrying Severn Road across the M49 motorway (Jackson 1997).

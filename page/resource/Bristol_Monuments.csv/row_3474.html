monument_desc South African War memorial, Clifton College, located 17 metres to the south-west of the Guthrie Memorial Chapel. The memorial was designed by the Bristol architects W. S. Paul and R. C. James and the sculptor Alfred Drury (1856-1944) was invited to make the figure of St. George. The statue was unveiled by Lord Methuen in May 1905 (Merritt 2002, 32-33).
The memorial comprises a bronze figure of St. George in armour with a sword in the right hand and a shield in the left. The figure is set on a square carved limestone plinth in a Gothic Revival-style with angle buttresses which turn into pinnacles, linked by swept labels with crockets and finials. The top is panelled and there are bronze panels on each of the faces of the plinth. The bronze panel on the south-east face of the plinth carries a legend composed by the poet Sir Henry Newbolt (1862-1938), a Clifton College alumnus: "SOUTH AFRICAN WAR / 1899-1902 / CLIFTON / REMEMBER THESE / THY SONS WHO FELL / FIGHTING / FAR OVER SEA / FOR THEY / IN A DARK HOUR / REMEMBERED WELL / THEIR WARFARE / LEARNED OF THEE." The panels on the remaining faces bear the names of the fallen.
The memorial has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/2/831).

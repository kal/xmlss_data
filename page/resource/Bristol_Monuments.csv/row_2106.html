monument_desc Bishopsworth. The village was established in the Saxon period and is recorded in the Domesday survey of 1086 as the land of Bishop Geoffrey of Coutances. The form of the settlement in the medieval period has not been firmly established but a Chapel dedicated to St. Peter (SMR 650) was founded apparently c.1189. This was presumably a chapel-of-ease to St. John at Bedminster and is likely to have been the focus of the settlement. The chapel was dissolved in the mid-sixteenth century when it was recorded by a survey of 1548-49.
The Bedminster tithe survey of 1841 indicates a group of dwellings in the area of the junction of Church Road and Whitchurch Road, with additional development to the north, along Romn Road.

monument_desc Mortimer House on the east side of Clifton Down Road. The house was built in c.1750 and consists of three storeys and a basement. It is a 9 window range constructed of Limestone ashlar with gable chimney stacks.
The house was part of the development, along with the neighbouring Rodney Lodge and Duncan House, of 'Holly Lands' first recorded in a survey of the smaller manor of Clifton dated 1625 (BRO 32835) and depicted on Jacob de Wilstar's survey of the Manor of Clifton, 1746, at which time the land was in the ownership of Francis Freeman (Leech, 2008, 15). By 1750 the land appears to have been sold to Isaac Elton for the purpose of constructing a new dwelling probably in accordance with a covenant with Francis Freeman drawn up to ensure that all the properties built along Clifton Down Road were of similar proportion and style (ibid., 16). Sketchley's Directory of 1775 records Elton, Isaac, Esq., alderman as the occupier. Ashmead's map of 1828 records that the building had been extended to the south by the addition of a small house abutting the main building. This plan also shows a carriage way at the front of the house and a long garden to the rear with terrace walk.
The house was sold at auction by the Elton family in 1876. The Ordnance Survey plan of 1884 depicts the house with outbuildings to the rear. The property was further extended in 1895 with the addition of a 'Refectory' at which time the house appears to have been used as a school (BRO Building Plans, vol.31, fol.45) and extended again in 1900 (BRO Building Plans, vol.38, fol.63) and 1906 (BRO Building Plans, vol.38, fol.63).

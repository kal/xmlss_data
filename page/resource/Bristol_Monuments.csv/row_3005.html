monument_desc A medieval chamber beneath 31, Jacob's Well Road and adjoining footway at the junction of Constitution Hill and Jacob's Well Road. This chamber is associated with the water supply to St Mark's Hospital known as St Mark's conduit or Gaunt's Pipe (934M). This is a large underground rectagular chamber measuring approximately 3.5m in length and 1.4m wide constructed of rubble of possible medieval date with a stone vault above. The feature is accessed from a steel road plate on the Jacob's Well Road frontage.
This chamber possibly relates to the grant of the property containing a spring, refered to as Garden Springs in 1742 on a plan by William Halfpenny (the professional name used by Michael Hoare), to St Mark's Hospital in c.1235-45. Prior to this the associated water supply may have related to the possible Bet tohorah or mikveh (949M SAM number AV 28881) at 33 Jacob's Well Road.
From the mid-thirteenth century the chamber is likely to have formed part of the complicated network of chambers and conduits collecting water from springs above Jacob's Well Road to supply water to St Augustine's Abbey and St. Mark's Hospital via Gaunt's Pipe.
The chamber was surveyed by Mark Horton of the University of Bristol in 1999 (BUAD 3520).

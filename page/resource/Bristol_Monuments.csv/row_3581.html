monument_desc An Iron Age and Romano-British settlement at Henbury School. The settlement was identified through the discovery of six or more inhumations during groundworks on the south side of the school grounds in 1982 (SMR 5470; Russell 1983). Geophysical survey in 2002 (SMR 21560) and more extensive excavation in 2004 (SMR 21855) identified a system of ditches at the south-western corner of the playing fields although the landscaping of the playing fields in the 1980s had truncated the features.
The ditches formed part of a rural settlement which had begun during the Iron Age and continued well into the Roman period. A large enclosure had been subdivided into smaller enclosures on the same orientation during the Roman period. Margary (1957) suggests that the Roman road between Sea Mills and Gloucester (Margary 541) passed through the school grounds a short distance to the east of the site and the fieldwork also identified a section of its line close to Avonmouth Way.

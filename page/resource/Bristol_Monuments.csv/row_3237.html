monument_desc The house known as "The Marshalsea", located on the north side of Wine Street. The site is now beneath Aylward House, on the west side of the junction of Wine Street and Union Street. The construction date of the building is not known but in 1632 it was, together with the house to the west, the home of Robert Blunt, ironmonger. The house seems subsequently to have been divided into two, the western of the two being owned by Joyce Tyson, widow, in 1712. The plot is described as extending back to the town wall (Leech 1997, 183). A drawing by James Stewart dated 18th April 1751 entitled "The HOUSE formerly the MARSHALSY, from the Garden" (BUAD 3674) records the rear, north-facing, elevation of the building, outbuildings to the north and the garden. The building was of four storeys plus attic and appears to have had three bays, although only the western and half of the central bay can be seen in the drawing. The roof seems to have been hipped with a stack on its west side. The fenestration is recorded as comprising 3 by 6 pane sashes and there was a door at the centre of the ground floor. Attached to the west side of the north-facing elevation is a small 2 1/2 storey structure with sash windows and a pantile roof. The entire west wall of the structure appears to comprise a large chimney stack, freestanding for at least a storey above the roof. To the north of this, on the west side of the plot was a single storey outbuilding with a lean-to roof. On the east side of the plot, apparently a short distance from the house is a narrow four-storey building, possibly a square garden house. This has a single sash window in the third and fourth storeys, these storeys being divided by a string course, perhaps a cornice. There appears to be a tall free-standing stack projecting from roughly the centre of the roof and there were decorative urns on the north-east and north-west corners of the parapet. The rear yard was enclosed by a low wall with a gate at the centre. A path ran through the centre of the garden, with a rectangular planted bed on either side.

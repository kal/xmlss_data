monument_desc Gate Lodge, Lodge and gateway. 1798-9. By Humphrey Repton. Pennant ashlar with limestone dressings, roof not visible. Single-depth plan, linked by an arch to a turret. Gothick style. 2 storeys; 1-window range. Left-hand lodge slightly forward of the entrance porch beside the 4-centred carriage archway, a tall octagonal turret on the right, with string courses to the ground and first floors and a crenellated parapet, machicolated above the archway. 3-light ground-floor and 2-light first-floor windows with Tudor-arched heads and labels; Tudor-arched door in porch below a shield and first-floor crossed arrow slit window. INTERIOR not inspected. Part of Repton's landscape design for Blaise Castle House

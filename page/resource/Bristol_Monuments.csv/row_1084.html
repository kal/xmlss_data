monument_desc The open space known as College Green. The land belonged to St. Augustine's Abbey and was used as the cemetery for the abbey according to documents of the thirteenth-century. It was the subject of disputes between the abbey and the neighbouring Hospital of St. Mark in the thirteenth- and fifteenth-centuries which were over access and grazing. The disputes were only settled by the intervention of the Bishop of Worcester. An open-air pulpit (BUAD 510M) was located in the centre of the green and the chapel of St. Jordan (BUAD 1058M) was located towards the abbey.
The green was transferred to the ownership of the Dean and Chapter after the dissolution. The 1880s Ordnance Survey map shows avenues running away from the centre of the green. A replica of the High Cross was erected at the end of the nineteenth-century. Deanery Road was pushed in front of the cathedral in the late nineteenth-century, dividing it from the green. Part of the area on the northern sides were destroyed by the construction of the new Council House through the 1930s and during the Second World War two air-raid shelters were dug across its centre. In the 1950s the green was landscaped: the trees around its edge were removed and the level of the ground apparently lowered. A refurbishment scheme removed Deanery Road and landscaped the area around the Queen Victoria statue in the early 1990s.

monument_desc A sub-rectangular ditched enclosure to the south of Severn Road, Chittening (NGR ST 53299 81787). The site was identified by R. G. Jackson during the course of a desk-based assessment (SMR 20081) of areas of Bristol City Council owned land north of Avonmouth in December 1996. It was first recorded by the tithe survey in 1841 and is shown by the 1881 and 1915 Ordnance Survey maps as being occupied by an orchard. The enclosure itself measures approximately 51 metres by 53 metres by 50 metres by 23 metres. The site was visited and it was found that water-filled ditches surround the enclosure (those to the north-west and south-east having been partially filled in to allow access by a trackway). The enclosed area is considerably higher than the surrounding farmland, and there is evidence there of both banks and a brick structure.

monument_desc The burial ground of All Saints' Almshouse, All Saints' Street. The cemetery was located on the west side of Fairfax Street and behind the almshouse which faced on to All Saints' Street. The almshouse (BUAD 1189M) was originally sited in High Street and moved to a location adjacent to St. John's Almshouses, St. John's Steep in 1740 to 1741. It was moved again to All Saints' Street in 1813 (Lobel & Carus-Wilson 1975, 26). The almshouse and burial ground are shown on Plumley and Ashmead's map of 1828 and Ashmead's map of 1850. The almshouse had been closed by the early 1880s and the Ordnance Survey first edition map indicates that a foundry occupied the site. The burial ground, however, seems to have remained undeveloped for many years and to have been used as a yard for the foundry. The site of the cemetery was subsequently redeveloped as Nos.10-12 Fairfax Street but it is not known whether, or when, the cemetery was cleared before redevelopment of the site.

monument_desc A medieval stone building was identified during archaeological excavation at Deep Street in 1989 (BUAD 444). The building was a ground floor hall which was built on a terrace cut into the south facing hillside of rubble construction and measured approximately 8.5 by 5.5 metres. A fourteenth-century open hearth with a stone surround was found in the centre of the building. The hearth was constructed of over 400 ceramic floor tiles pitched on their sides. A small stone drain was found running west to east through the middle of the building. The excavator interpreted the structure as the lodge of the warden or the guest house of the Franciscan friary in Lewin's Mead.
The building was demolished in the early modern period and a secular domestic building constructed on the rubble. The remains of the south wall of the building were destroyed by construction of the revetment wall bounding the site.

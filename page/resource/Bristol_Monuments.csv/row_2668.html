monument_desc Pair of two storey gate lodges at 140-142 Frenchay Park Road. Constructed in c.1879 as two detached lodge houses flanking the entrance to the Beaufort Nursery that was in existence from 1879 to 1931.
Both buildings are constructed of mortared pennant sandstone blocks and slabs, roughly squared and with a fair face, laid in uneven courses, with the occasional random squared block. Quoins are in bath stone ashlar or similar, as are the sills of the windows. There are four windows front and back, paired two up two down, each face. The windows are single sash wooden frames, each divided from its twin by a moulded bath stone pillar, forming the central springer for a rough pennant double arch, one arch for each window of the pair. Springers for the outside edges of each arch are in moulded bath stone, as are the arch keystones.
Despite use of the Beaufort name, no records indicate any direct connection with the Beaufort estates. Rather, the lands on which the nursery was established were agricultural fields in private hands from at least 1754, while the nursery itself was owned by Parker and Sons throughout its entire period of operation (1879 to 1931).
Between 1916 and 1935 a western extension in the form of an early 20th century house was added to No. 140, with a detached garage to the rear of the property. Minor changes and extensions were made to No. 140 in the early 1980s, but in 1988 and 1989 planning permission was granted for the conversion of the property into a private residential care home, with the addition of a two storey extension to the rear.

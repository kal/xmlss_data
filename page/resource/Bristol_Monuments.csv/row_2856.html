monument_desc No.25, Kingsdown Parade. The house is located on the southern side of Back of Kingsdown Parade. The building appears to have been constructed by 1742. While the phasing of the development of the building has not been established through investigation, it seems originally to have been of two-storeys. In plan it is likely to have had a central entrance hall with room on either side at both ground and first floor levels, perhaps with an archway for a coach at the northern end and a small servant's room with a window facing on to St. Matthew's Road. Internally, cupboards built into the recesses adjacent to the chimney breasts survive at both ground and first floors. On the north wall of the western first-floor room there is a surviving fragment of wall painting probably dating to the eighteenth century, although it is not clear whether it dates to the original or second phase of construction. This painting is a single leaf and dart motif in black, white and grey on a liver-grey ground with a narrow white border. The painting was originally more extensive but the majority has been lost.
The building appears to have been altered during a programme of remodelling around the end of the eighteenth century. These works involved the replacement of the stairs and alteration of the position of several of the doors leading on to the landings as well as the addition of a storey and replacement of the roof. Extensions to the garden (south-east facing) elevation of the house were probably added at a slightly later date.
The building is now a structure of three storeys and attic, with a single-depth plan, which is aligned south-west to north-east along the street. The building has a pitched roof with a hip at the northern end.
The majority of the garden originally belonged to a garden house on Kingsdown Parade (BUAD 1246M). However, by 1828 this house had been demolished and most of its garden incorporated into the curtilage of No.25 Kingsdown Parade. A small plot had, however, also been carved out of the south-west corner to accommodate the present No.23 Kingsdown Parade by that date.

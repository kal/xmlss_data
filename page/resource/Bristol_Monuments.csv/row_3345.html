monument_desc Redland Chapel, Redland Green. The chapel was commissioned in 1740 as a private chapel by John Cossins of Redland Court. Dening (1923 85) suggests John Strahan as the architect, but Ison (1952, 54-55) establishes that William Halfpenny (the professional name used by Michael Hoare) oversaw the construction work and may therefore have provided the design. The chapel was drawn by Halfpenny in 1742 (Eustace, 1982 fig.22). Stone carving on the west front and the interior was by Thomas Paty, as was some of the plasterwork. Cossins nominated William Ray, then vicar of Westbury-on-Trym, as minister. An indenture dated 10th June 1749 records that an "Orchard and a piece of void ground on which a mansion house formerly stood" were used as endowment to maintain the chapel and pay a stipend to the minister.
The chapel has a Grade I listing (Listed Building No.901-1/34/1723). the perimeter wall, gate piers and gates to the churchyard (SMR 20266) are listed separately and are Grade II* (Listed Building No.901-1/34/1724).

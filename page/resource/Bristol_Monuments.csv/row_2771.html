monument_desc Cooper's Hall, located on the north side of King Street. The livery hall known as The Coopers' Hall was built between 1743 and 1744 to a design by William Halfpenny (the professional name used by Michael Hoare). The structure was erected on plot with a frontage of 65 feet 9 inches, which was bought from the Corporation for £900. Ison (1952, 105) notes that this hall replaced an earlier hall in Cock Lane (modern Exchange Avenue) which was demolished in clearing the site for The Exchange.
The building has a single storey with an attic and basement. It has a five window range. In the centre of the King Street (south) frontage is a pediment containing the Coopers' arms.
After the ending of its use as a livery hall, it became in turn an assembly-room, a sale-room, an exhibition hall and a Methodist chapel, finally being used as a warehouse. The interior of the building was rebuilt in 1971 to act as the foyer of the Theatre Royal.
The building has a Grade I listing (Listed Building Number 901-1/16/624).

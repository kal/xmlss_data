monument_desc No.26, St. Augustine's Parade. C17, rebuilt C19 and C20. Timber-frame, render with a cross-gabled pantile roof. Double-depth plan. 3 storeys and attic; 2-window range. Mid C19 shop front has Tudor arches to plate-glass windows and a central doorway, on slender columns, and a cornice; shallow jetties to each floor above, and paired gables. Paired 9/9-pane sashes below jetties, and a 3-light casement to the attic, in exposed frames. The left return has a stone mullion window to the attic with lattice leaded lights. A large hanging 3-sided clock with elaborate wrought-iron brackets and decoration. Little evidence of timber-framing; the roof extends back one gable, and is a C20 ridged roof behind. Nos 26-30 are now internally one building.
Cellared. Gutted internally in 1983, at which time a number of extra features were identified. There was evidence that early floors were at different levels to those associated with the 17th.century facade.
Some relieving arches were noted in the exterior walls.

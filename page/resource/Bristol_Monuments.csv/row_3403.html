monument_desc A Buried land surface recorded during evaluation and subsequent excavation at Katherine Farm, Avonmouth (21420 and 21421). The deposit was identified with an upper surface height of 5.07 - 5.28m aOD and a maximum thickness of 0.12m. Two radiocarbon dates were obtained from this layer; the first from a charcoal sample produced a date of 2778 +/- 55 BP (NZA-12725, 1070-810 cal BC) and a second from a cow tooth with a date of 2957 +/- 55 BP (NZA-12726, 1380-1010 cal BC) suggesting a Bronze Age date of deposition. Artefacts from the layer included 56 pottery fragments, imported stone fragments (mainly sandstone), five worked flints and animal bone. It was suggested that this evidence represented seasonal activity on the saltmarsh possibly hunting, grazing or salt-making, although conclusive interpretation was not possible from the available evidence.

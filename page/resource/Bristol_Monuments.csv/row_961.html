monument_desc St. Peter's Pump conduit house, located on the north-western end of Peter Street, at the junction with Dolphin Street. The structure formed part of the No.5 Dolphin Street. It was constructed c.1766 as part of the redevelopment of Bridge Street, Dolphin Street and Union Street to the designs of Thomas Paty. The conduit house replaced the medieval St. Peter's Pump (BUAD 39M) after the Trustees of Bristol Bridge ordered the removal of the original well head at the west end of Peter Street in 1766, and stood 10 metres north-north-west of the original site.
The conduit head was a limestone ashlar structure which was open to Dolphin Street and Peter Street, via a single semicircular-headed arch in each elevation. The elevations comprised rusticated ashlar piers, quoins and voussoirs, with a plat band above.
The pump was taken out of use by the Health Authority in 1887.

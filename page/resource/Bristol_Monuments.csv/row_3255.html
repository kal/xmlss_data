monument_desc Shirehampton Remount Depot, Barrow Hill, Shirehampton. The depot acted as a temporary stables for horses and mules arriving at Avonmouth Docks for military service. It was part of the establishment of the Army's Remount Department and was opened in September 1914 as part of an expansion of the Department (Singleton 1993, 188). It was initially staffed by 1380 civilian staff under the control of Army officers.
Animals were bought from Ireland, the traditional source of horses for the British Army, but the majority were shipped across the Atlantic in specially converted ships from the United States of America and Canada (Singleton 1993, 187). The first consignment arrived on 24 October. Most animals arrived from Avonmouth Docks, but around 35000 were also brought by rail (Stone & Wells 1920, 97; 238). The depot could accommodate 5000 animals and held them in quarantine for between a fortnight and twenty-one days. It was divided into ten squadrons and had a veterinary hospital capable of treating 500 animals which included an isolation ward for up to 100. Some animals were shipped out through the Port of Bristol but the majority seem to have been moved inland, many to other depots around Somerset, by rail.
In 1915 the depot became a fully military establishment. It was visited by King George V and Queen Mary on 7 September 1915 and was inspected by Viscount French, Commander of Home Forces, on 23 February 1916 (Stone & Wells 1920, 345).
The last major shipment of animals was on 25 November 1918 and Shirehampton Remount Depot was closed in October 1919, having handled 347045 animals (Neale 1976, 118) including 13811 which were returned from France after the Armistice (Stone & Wells, 1920 97). After the closure of the depot the Bristol Corporation acquired several of the huts as temporary housing (Stone & Wells 1920, 98).
The precise location and extent of the depot has not yet been established.

monument_desc BBC Emergency Radio Studio constructed inside the tunnel of the Clifton Rocks Railway. The BBC sought an emergency studio at the beginning of the Second World War and one of the tunnels of the Bristol to Avonmouth Railway was initially considered. This did not become available and the Corporation leased the tunnel of the former Clifton Rocks funicular railway. The tunnel was made water and gas-tight, and extractor fans and an ozoneator were installed. Four chambers were constructed along the tunnel. The highest, around the mid-point of the tunnel, housed transmitters. Immediately below this was the studio. The recording room was below this and the lowest chamber was a the control room. Smaller rooms housed a canteen, diesel generators and water tanks (Penny 2001, 9-10).
Use of the tunnel as a studio appears to have ended in 1946 but the BBC continued to lease it for use as a local radio repeater station. In the mid-1950s structural failures were noted and in January 1958 it was recommended that a series of concrete buttresses be constructed to support the face of the tunnel. The BBC ended its lease in 1960 and the tunnel has remained vacant since then.

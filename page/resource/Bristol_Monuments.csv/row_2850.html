monument_desc Spencer's Almshouse. On south side of Lewins Mead, between there and the River Frome. A long narrow range at right-angles to the street and on the eastern side of a courtyard, separated from the Mead by another building, under which was an access passage. There was a like building at the river end, also. The main range was single-storey, attached, with a large pent roof against the east side, with a catslide corbelled out beyond the fronts of each of the 5 units. Pantiled roof. Stacks at the rear of the range. Each unit with an entrance to the right of a small window or windows. Below each window, a stone platform or seat.
The range fronting Lewins Mead, which may not have been part of the almshouse, was of 2 storeys and an attic. A main roof parallel to the street, with a raised gable at the western end of its south side. The north elevation is not recorded. The south elevation a 2-window range. Left-hand passage to the street, with a small window to its right. Right-hand half aginst the north-south almshouse range. 2 first-floor windows. 1 window in the gable; a dormer window to the right. Stack at right gable end. Pantiled roof.
At the southern end of the courtyard, a 2-storey, 2-window range, the roof again at right angles to the almshouse range. At the right-hand end, a passage to the river, with square opening to the yard, but a two-centred arch at the far end. Immediately left of the passage, another arched entrance, with a four-centred head and 'stable' doors. Above, a small tiled pentice roof (protecting a bell?). 2 small first-floor windows. A stack at each end of the pantiled roof.

monument_desc Eagle House, Brislington. Part of an estate called Templeman's that was originally purchased in 1660 by a Mr. Munday from Rowland Lacy, the Lord of the Manor. The land was purchased by John Tipton and he is recorded as the owner in 1751. The estate remained in the Tipton family as Mary Tipton is recorded as living at Eagle House in 1828. In 1830 the estate was purchased by Sir John Hare but was broken up soon after and William Plummer is recorded as purchasing the house, and John Hurle and Richard Poole-King, both local landowners, purchasing the land lying to the north of the property. The Tithe Award, 1846, records that William Plummer continued to be the owner and occupier of the property.

The house was built in 1769 and took its name from the stone eagle that surmounted the centre of the façade of the house.

A ha-ha crossed by a wooden bridge gave access to the field which bordered Talbot Lane, the railway lines and Bristol Road. The house was demolished in 1934.

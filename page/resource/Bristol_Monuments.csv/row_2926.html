monument_desc Robert Adams's house on Castle Mill Street. Immediately downhill of Newgate Prison. 2 storeys and an attic to Castle Mill Street, but possibly more storeys cut into the hill below. Gabled front; jettied; 2-window range. Left-hand entrance; central entrance to shop, window right. On the left, a 6-panel door in a frame with a depressed four-centred arch, the spandrels with the initials 'R A'. Bracket at far left-hand. At first-floor a wide central oriel with 2 sash windows, small left-hand (3-light?) window. 6-pane attic window. Carved bargeboards, carrying the date 1547 (or 1543) and the initials 'R A'; pendant at apex; eaves supported on brackets. Pantile roof. Lateral stacks. There was a C17 style full-height chimney-piece in one of the ground-floor rooms, the upper part decorated with the city arms between two panels each with rosettes or compositae at their centre. Rowbotham's 1825 drawing shows some openings in the west party wall with the gaol, but it is not clear to which building they belong, as there was clearly formerly another structure, probably a house, between there and the Prison. O'Neill shows a pair of C16 or C17 chimney-pieces on the outside of the second-floor wall.

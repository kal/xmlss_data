monument_desc A pair of houses. Fronting the 'Mulberry Tree' tavern. Attached. On the west side of Broad Street, opposite John Street. 3 storeys and attics; gabled fronts; fully jettied. Entrance to right of right-hand ground-floor; bay windows to each ground-floor, cellar access below right-hand example. Large bracket at right end. Bays to both first-floors, each with Ipswich window; glazing beside both bays, giving almost full-width fenestration across combined frontage. Brackets to second-floor. On the second-floor, to left a bay with Ipswich window, glazing to either side; to right, an oriel with 6-light window without transom. Brackets to eaves of left gable. Small window to each attic. Decorated bargeboards. Panelling to various parts of both buildings.
In front of the 'Mulberry Tree Tavern' and taken down in 1819. The tavern lay at the back of the houses, up a passage that ran under the far right-hand end. Turpin the bookbinder occupied one of the rooms in the buildings, his sign being on the second-floor of the left-hand house. Parry's boot and shoe warehouse had a sign between the first-floor bays. G.Franklin's name is under the left ground-floor bay. All the aforementioned listed at No.23 by the street directories.

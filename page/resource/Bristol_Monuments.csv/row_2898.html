monument_desc Trin Mill, located at the confluence of the river Avon and the Malago on the south bank of the Avon opposite The Marsh (modern Queen Square). The mill was granted to St. Augustine's Abbey between 1154 and 1170 by Henry II and had apparently been constructed by 1170-1183 (Walker, 1998 6; 12). The 1373 perambulation of the boundary of the county of Bristol recorded the mill as "Trenlemulle". James Millerd's map of 1673 shows the site at the end of Guinea Street and indicated that it was a watermill at the end of two leats which ran north from a large mill pond in the area of Bathurst Basin. Trin Mills survived into at least the mid-eighteenth century and was recorded by Jean Roque's map of 1742. It was presumably removed to make way for the lock of Bathurst Basin (BUAD 621M) at the beginning of the nineteenth century and the mill pond converted into the basin itself.

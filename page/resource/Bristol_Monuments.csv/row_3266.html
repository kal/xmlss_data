monument_desc West House Farm. The site of the farm (ST 53389 80905) is shown as an enclosure on Taylor's map of 1773 (BRO 29351) and was recorded by the 1822 enclosure map (GRO Q/RI 154), the 1841 Henbury tithe map, as well as the 1881 and 1915 Ordnance Survey maps. The tithe map records that the farm was owned by Celia Dickenson and occupied by Henry Hall. On the north side of the enclosure containing the farmhouse were three small enclosure identified as barn and orchard (BRO EP/A/32/22).
Most of the site is beneath a warehouse and silage clamps and yard of an adjoining farm. Two buildings of the farm remain, a stone barn with a tiled roof to the west of the original farm complex and a stone building of unknown function to the east. Historic maps show a ditched enclosure to the south-east of the farm (ST 53448 80874) which may be a medieval moated enclosure and possibly the original site of the farm. This feature is extant.
The site (Area 1; Site 5) was visited by R. G. Jackson, during the course of a desk-based assessment (SMR 20081) of areas of Bristol City Council owned land north of Avonmouth, in December 1996. The enclosure containing the farmhouse was found to be overgrown by trees and undergrowth.

monument_desc A house recorded in the rate books as belonging to John Garway in 1679. The house was first depicted on Millerd's revised map of c.1710. A drawing by James Stewart made in 1752 (Bodleian, MS.Gough, Somersetshire 2, fol.14) suggests that the house had three storeys with a projecting two storey porch (Leech, 2000, 59).
The property is recorded on several plans of the area including Rocque's 1742 plan and estate plans of the later eighteenth century. By the time of the 1884 Ordnance Survey plan the house had become the Preventive Home for Girls. The 1951 1:1250 Ordnance Survey plan records the site as "Ruin" suggesting that the property was largely destroy by bombing in the war and the site had been cleared entirely by the time of the OS survey in 1956.

monument_desc Chittening Platform, located to the south of Smoke Lane, Chittening. The station was created in 1917 for the use of munitions workers at the adjacent shell-filling factory (SMR 21403) but the eastbound and westbound platforms do not appear to have been contructed until 13 November 1918 and these continued in use until 11 October 1923. The station was reused in the Second World War when new shelters for passengers were provided. It continued in use after the war and reopened to passenger use on 31 May 1948. The station was closed on 23 November 1964 and the platforms were subsequently demolished (Mitchell & Smith 2004).

monument_desc The Marsh Gate. The town gate at the east end of the town wall known as the Marsh Wall. The gate led from Welsh Back into the Marsh and stood originally on the west bank of the river Avon. The ground to the east of the gate was subsequently reclaimed. The gate was recorded by William Worcestre as measuring "2 yards in width of the gate-walls" (Neale 2000, 214-215) and he also notes that it had not been built above, probably meaning that there was no upper accommodation in contrast to the earlier gates. The gate was still apparently standing in 1673 but had been demolished by 1742 (probably c.1738 when the removal of the nearby Back Street Gate was ordered by the Corporation).

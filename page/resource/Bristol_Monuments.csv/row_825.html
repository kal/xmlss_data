monument_desc No.53 Redcliff Street, located on the east side of Redcliff Street. The building was constructed in the seventeenth century, apparently as one of a pair with No.52 Redcliff Street (BUAD 268M).
The land parcel, defined by Redcliff Street and the lawditch to the east, was leased to Thomas Oates in 1661 (BRO DC/E/40/37/52064) and to Rowalnd Collins by 1672 (BRO DC/E/40/37/52070). The building was recorded in a watercolour drawing by T. L. Rowbotham (BUAD 967; BRSMG M2042) in 1826 and this view shows the structure as a single-bay jettied, gabled house of three storeys and attic. The building was still apparently extant in the mid-1850s, but it had been demolished by the time of the Ordnance Survey first edition plan (Bristol Sheet LXXV.4.5) surveyed in 1882 and the site redeveloped. The site of the building now lies partly beneath the highway in Redcliff Street, but the rear, eastern, part of the footprint is on the site of modern No.54 Redcliff Street.

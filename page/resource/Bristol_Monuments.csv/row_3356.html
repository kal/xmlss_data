monument_desc The Wyck, otherwise known as The Wick, Wick Farm or Week Farm. The placename "The Wyck" was first recorded in the Pipe Rolls in 1195 and re-occurs in subsequent medieval property records (Smith 1964, 133). In 1794 "all that messuage tenement or dwelling house called or known by the name of Week House otherwise Wick House with the barns, stables offices, yards, gardens, orchards and paddock ground there belonging", then rented by William Yeoman, passed into the ownership of Sarah Beausoy of Stokes Croft in Bristol (BRO 28048 P92). The farm was inherited by her daughters Sarah Hood and Susannah Southall at her death in 1799 (BRO 28048 P92) and by the time of the Henbury Tithe Survey in 1840 was owned by Ann Hood, presumably the daughter of Sarah Hood, and Thomas Hayne. It was then rented by William Lambert (BRO EP/A/32/22). The Wick is recorded by the first edition Ordnance Survey 1:2500 plan (Gloucestershire sheet LXVII.16 published in 1881). In the early twentieth century the Avonmouth and Filton Railway, which opened on 9th May 1910, bisected the fields to the north of the farmhouse.
The farm buildings were still extant at the time of the Ordnance Survey Provisional Edition map of 1938. The farm and farmhouse site has since been developed for housing and the majority of the buildings associated with the farmhouse lie beneath Nos.276-288 Passage Road.

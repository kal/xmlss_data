monument_desc Bristol Lunatic Asylum, located on the north side of Blackberry Hill. The asylum, designed by Henry Crisp, was constructed in 1861 a short distance to the north-west of the Bristol Union workhouse. An isolation wing (now Orchard Day Hospital) was constructed c1898 to designs by H. Crisp and G. H.Oatley. The main asylum building was extended, the hospital chapel and the Lower Lodge constructed between 1885 and 1916 when the second edition Ordnance Survey 1:2500 plan was published. In 1948 the buildings became Stapleton Hospital (later Glenside Hospital) and there have been alterations to the building while it has been in National Health Service ownership (Leech 2001).
The main hospital building (901-1/30/1260), the Upper (901-1/30/1259) and Lower (901-1/30/1262) Lodges, the hospital chapel (901-1/30/1261) and the Isolation Block (901-1/30/1263) all have a Grade II listing.

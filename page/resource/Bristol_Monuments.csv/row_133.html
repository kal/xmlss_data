It is a rendered timber frame building with brick stacks to the left and a pantile roof. There are gabled front and rear elevations to the building and a double depth plan with central stairs between the front and back rooms. It has an eighteenth-century ground floor and outer doorways with sashes. It is joined internally to No.20. The building was refenestrated in the eighteenth-century and restored in 1991.

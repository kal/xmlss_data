monument_desc The News Theatre, located on the north side of Peter Street. The cinema was built on the site of the former Queen's Hall to a design by Curno Cooke of W. H. Watkins' firm of architects. It was commissioned by Joseph Cohen of Jacey Cinemas Ltd. and built by Frank Wilkins at a cost of £451.1s and took just four months to build (Ewins 1984, 19; 28; Anderson 1983, 55). The News Theatre opened on 26th December 1933 as the first specially designed provincial news theatre (Ewins 1984, 19). The building was very modern in design with a black and green alabaster glass frontage with stainles steel bands and a black tower above the main entrance. The Bristol Evening Post reported on 22nd December 1933 that "This news theatre is unique in the city, notably in the treatment of its main façade, the whole of which is faced with black and green alabaster glass, having stainless steel bands. The tower which rises above the main entrance is faced in black, and the flanking walls are treated with horizontal bands of green and black glass on a black granite base. Further striking features of this elevation are the neon lighting, which has been arranged to harmonise with the architectural design, and the flashing 'world' sign which depicts a rotating sphere with the continents depicted in red neon. The tones of green, silver, and black have been continued in the decorative scheme of the entrance vestibule, which is in every way strinkingly modern. The decoration has been carried out in tints of peach colour, pale green, and silver to the walls and ceilings, with walnut doors, pale green architraves, and black skirting," (Ewins 1984, 19-20). There were approximately 400 seats in the cinema and 50 more than the former Queen's Picture House due to the enlargement of the site (Ewins 1984, 19).
The cinema remained open until 1956 and was demolished in 1959. A photograph of the auditorium taken during the demolition of the building shows that the original late art deco ornamentation around the stage was then still extant (Winstone 1972, pl.231).
The site of The News Theatre now lies within Castle Park.

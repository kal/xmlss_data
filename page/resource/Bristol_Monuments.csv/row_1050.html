monument_desc The Black Horse, located on the south side of West Street, now on the south-west side of the corner of West Street with Waterloo Street. The Black Horse is recorded by Jean Rocque's 1742 map of Bristol.
The building was attached. 2 storeys and attics; double-gabled front; 2-window range. Ground-floor raised above what is probably a high cellar but could be a sunken ground-floor. Twin sashes to the raised storey. Full-width pentice roof over. First-floor segmental-headed sash windows. Cross-windows to attic, with some timber-framing visible to the front. Lateral stack on west wall, with twin pots. The building was rebuilt on the same site to form the present building.

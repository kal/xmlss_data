monument_desc No.40 Old Market Street, located on the northern side of Old Market Street.. The building was constructed in 1688 by the Bristol clay tobacco pipemaker Llewellyn Evans. It is a single-bay jettied, house of two storeys and attic. The building has a gabled pantile roof. At ground-floor the main, south-facing, elevation comprises a shopfront with a central entrance. The second floor fenestration consists of a pair of sash windows at the centre of the elevation. There is a smaller casement window at the centre of the gable. Internally the original plan consisted of front and rear rooms, with a staircase to one side of the rear room.
In 1875 the building was recorded in the street directory as being occupied by John Sprod & Son, outfitters.
The interior has been substantially reconstructed.

monument_desc Merchant Navy Memorial, located on the east side of Welsh Back. The Merchant Navy Association (Bristol Branch) decided in 1996 to create a "War Memorial Garden in Bristol Docks in memory of the Merchant Seamen of Bristol who died for their country". A memorial committee was set up and an outline scheme drawn up for a central memorial surrounded by a small garden enclosed by dwarf walls. A site in the Centre was initially investigated but one at Welsh Back was agreed in the late summer of 1999. Funds were raised by a public campaign and the artist, David Backhouse (who also sculpted the Cloaked Horseman at Narrow Lewins Mead), selected in May 2000 after a competition.
The memorial is a polished Portuguese granite monolith set on a plinth and has a bronze collar. It was constructed by Hanson Bath and Portland Stone of Keynsham and the collar was cast by G. F. James of Southampton. It is surrounded by seating. The memorial carries two inscriptions. That on the monument is:
"This memorial is a tribute to/all merchant seafarers who have sailed/to and from Bristol/throughout the ages." At the base of the monument is a poem by Capt. J. Earl, a member of the Bristol Branch of the Merchant Navy Association:
"Merchant Seamen/In war and peace they plied their trade/Over the angry seas/Remember them as here you stand/Beneath these placid trees."
The memorial was unveiled by HRH The Princess Royal, Princess Anne, at a public ceremony on 21 May 2001.

monument_desc Oldbury House, located on the west side of St. Michael's Hill. The building a late seventeenth-century gabled house of three-storeys and attic and is of rendered stone rubble. The main, north-east facing, elevation is of six bays. The main entrance doorway is a the centre of the elevation and is surrounded by a rusticated ashlar moulding with an open-base pediment above. There is a pair of sash windows at either end of the elevation and six sashes at first floor. The three sashes at second floor are beneath the gables but those at the northern and southern ends of the elevation are not set exactly between the sashes below. The three gable ends are blind. The building is one room deep in plan and has a projecting stair at the rear. There is a chimney stack at the north and the south end of the roof.
The house was built between 1679 and 1689, apparently for Marmaduke Bowdler and was purchased from him in 1692 by Lady Phillipa Gore and occupied by her and Joseph Knight Esq., according to the ratebooks, until 1706. The building was occupied by Richard Haynes Esq. from 1709 and by Edward Bisdy (or Bisdee) and his widow from 1722 until the mid-1760s. From 1765 until 1773 Charles Horwood lived there (Leech, 2000 48-49).
The house is now in the ownership of the University of Bristol. It has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-/4/275).

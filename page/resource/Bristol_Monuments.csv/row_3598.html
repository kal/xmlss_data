monument_desc Greenbank cemetery. The cemetery was established by the Burial Board for the parish of St. Philip and Jacob and was opened 1871. The entrance gates were on Rose Green Road and Greenbank Road, with a lodge adjacent to the Greenbank Road entrance. A pair of mortuary chapels (SMR 20112), designed in a Gothic style by Henry Masters, was built at the centre of the site. The mortuary chapels and are constructed of squared Pennant sandstone rubble with polychrome limestone ashlar details and have pitched slate roofs.
The cemetery was expanded in 1881 and the Ordnance Survey first edition 1:2500-scale County Series plan indicates that the cemetery then covered an area of 8.2 hectares The cemetery was transferred to the Bristol Corporation under the Bristol Corporation Act of 1895. It was expanded again in c.1900.
There are several war graves within the cemetery and a Cross of Sacrifice, or Great War cross, (SMR 20113) was erected immediately to the south-west of the mortuary chapels after the First World War.
The mortuary chapels have a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/38/1258). The entrance gates and piers to the cemetery on Greenbank Road have a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/38/1257).

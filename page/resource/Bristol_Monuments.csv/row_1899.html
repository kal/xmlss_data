monument_desc A possible mine revealed beneath the garden of Bellevue, Grove Road during ground works at Malvern House. The 'mine' consisted of two interconnected chambers with smaller side chambers approached by an east-west aligned shaft. The original shaft entrance is likely to have been beneath the former Malvern House garden and later modern extension to Malvern House. The mine was only identified when ground reduction of the former garden area revealed an underground chamber 2 metres below the surface measuring 1.5 metres wide and 1m high. The chamber continued down at a slight incline for a further 3.5 metres to a depth approximately 3 metres below the garden to the rear of Bellevue. The chamber had been excavated into soft Dolomitic Conglomerate immediately to the west of an area of Carboniferous Limestone. There are no known cartographic depictions or documentary references to the mine, dating the activity to a period before the first specific reference to the area on a sketch plan of 1801 (BRO 32226, Boxes 20-21, Redland bundle). It was speculated that the mine was excavated in the eighteenth century for Celestine or other rare minerals that can be found in the conglomerate.

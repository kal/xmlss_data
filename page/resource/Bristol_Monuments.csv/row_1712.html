monument_desc A nursery located on the eastern side of Cheltenham Road, on the northern side of its junction with Bath Buildings. The nursery was in existence by the 1880s and is recorded by the Ordnance Survey first edition 1:500-scale Town Plan (Bristol Sheet LXXI.16.5) surveyed in 1881. The street directory for 1883 record it as belonging to Jesse Prestidge, seedsman and florist. The nursery was a walled, essentially rectangular site with the main vehicular entrance on Bath Buildings. The site had a 42-metre frontage on Cheltenham Road and was 37 metres wide, covering an area of approximately 1490 square metres. A large building at the south-west corner of the site appears to have been residential, and there were ten greenhouses towards the centre of the site.
The western part of the site was removed to enable the widening of Cheltenham Road and the remainder of the site was subsequently redeveloped.

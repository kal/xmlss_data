monument_desc St. James Priory. The priory was locatoed in the area defined by modern Whitson Street, St. James Parade and Cannon Street. The monastery was a Benedictine site founded in the twelfth century, reputedly in 1129, by Robert of Gloucester (Robert Fitzroy). The nave of the church was in use for parochial purposes by 1374 and survived the Dissolution by virtue of its role as the church of the parish St. James (BUAD 143M). Part of the western claustral range, including 2 bays of the arcade of the cloister walk, survives incorporated into Church House (BUAD 144M). The brothers' burial ground was located immediately east of the conventual buildings, the parish burial ground to the south.
St. James Priory was dissolved in January 1540, and the buildings appear quickly to have become dilapidated. In 1544 Henry Brayne, a London merchant, bought the priory site and all its property, including the advowson of several Bristol rectories but specifically excluding the bells and the lead on the roofs of the priory buildings, for £667 7s. 6d. In January 1544 (Gairdner & Baird 1903, 37).
St. James Church has a Grade I listing (Listed Building number 901-1/11/347) and the railings and attached walls also have a separate listing. Church House has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/11/348).

monument_desc Holy Nativity Church, located on the north-eastern side of Wells Road. The church was built in 1871-83 to a design by A. Ponton and W. V. Gough and was consructed of Bridgwater brick with limestone ashlar dressings. The church had an apsaidal end and a square tower on the south side of the nave. The tower was completed by Gough's son Arthur in 1931.
During the Second World War the body of the church was largely destroyed by bombing and the damaged building was painted by John Piper (BRSMG K1726).
The tower of the church is listed Grade II (901-1/47/1560).

monument_desc Three houses. On the inside of the bend on old Baldwin Street. Each three storeys and attic, jettied, with gabled fronts. Brackets to first and second floors; attic fronts flush with second-floor fronts. Beyond right-hand property was that occupied by Whiting (Whitting), plumber, etc., in 1823, given as No.53 in the directories. Right-hand entrances to left and centre houses; right-hand house had entrances at centre and right. Oriel at first-floor of left house, 10-light with transom. Projections at same storey of right house, with large muti-paned window. Mixed windows to second-floors, with cross-window to right house. Small attic windows. It was reputed that the right-hand house, No.46, contained the room where Henry II was educated as a child. This building is illustrated separately, M.2224. In 1827 it was owned by Mr.Terrel, flax dresser of the Back, and let out as a lodging house to "the low Irish". On carrying out alterations, the removal of a stack of chimneys revealed some wall painting depicting a 16th.century (?) man with some text (M.2225 & 2225a). Various fragments of panelling were also discovered and recorded (M.2226-2228). A sketch plan of the house is given (M.2229). It is assumed that the finds were made on the first-floor of the house. The house consisted of a full-width front room, with fireplace on the back wall, and, to the right, a doorway leading to the rear room via a lobby. The rear room was of reduced width, but backed onto the rear of the adjacent property to the west, with their fireplaces back-to-back on the party wall.

monument_desc Lord Mayor's Chapel, formerly the church of St. Mark's Hospital (BUAD 509M), located on the north-easterns side of College Green. The church is the only part of the hospital which is known to survive above ground. The building was erected in 1230, the south aisle added in 1270, the tower in 1487, and the chancel and south aisle chapel in 1500. The Poyntz chapel was built in 1523. The roof of nave is early sixteenth-century. After the dissolution (in 1539) the precincts of the hospital and its properties were acquired by the Corporation in May 1541. In the early nineteenth-century oil paintings on stone which formed part of a squint were recorded in part of a building next door to the chapel (Gill & Howard, in Keen 1997, 97-106).
In 1822 the west window of the Chapel was removed [This window is currently located in the garden of No.5 The Ridgeway, Westbury-on-Trym and is listed Grade II*].
A new organ was built for the chapel in 1825 by John Smith senior of the Bristol organ builders John Smith & Sons. The organ was refurbished by W. G. Vowles in 1878. The chapel was the subject of a programme of restoration by J. L. Pearson in 1889, during which the north transept and the west front were rebuilt.
The church has a Grade I listing (Listed Building number 901-1/15/66).

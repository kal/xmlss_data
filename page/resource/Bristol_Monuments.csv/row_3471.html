monument_desc The Synagogue, located on the northern side of Lower Park Row. The synagogue was designed by S. C. Fripp and Hyman Henry Collins of London and was built in 1874. The synagogue is a three-storey structure of coursed, rusticated Pennant sandstone with freestone ashlar detailing and a pitched roof. The interior of the synagogue was designed by Collins and is typical of his work. Most of the fittings are original. The Ark incorporates doors from the earlier synagogue at Temple Street [BUAD 291M]. The Bimah (central reading platform) is of unusual curved shape and the lamp stands are also from Temple Street. The Royal Family prayer boards came from the Montefiore Synagogue, Ramsgate (1831-1833) (Sharmian Kadish pers. comm.). The gallery was extended on the west side in the 1970s.
Collins was one of the earliest Jewish architects to practice in Britain and designed several synagogues in London and elsewhere. His only other surviving buildings are Chatham Memorial Synagogue (1865-1870) and London, St. John's Wood (1880-1882)

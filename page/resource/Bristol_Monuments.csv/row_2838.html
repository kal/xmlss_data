monument_desc No.42 Old Market Street. The building is of early eighteenth-century date and is a three storey, three-bay redered house with limestone dressings. It has a hipped pantile roof. The building has a double-depth plan and has a cellar which is rectangular in plan, with stairs from the rear of the shop and a chute from the pavement. There is a possible former window in the rear wall.
The shop front is of eighteenth-century date, with rusticated pilasters to a deep modillion eaves cornice which returns at the ends. The early nineteenth-century shop front has two eight-pane bow windows either side of a two-leaf door, with fluted timber pilasters to a dentil cornice which bows out above the doorway, narrow first-floor 6 over 6-pane sashes beneath raised pediments, segmental to the centre, and shorter, keyed second-floor 6 over 6-pane sashes, all in flush boxes.
Internally the rear ground-floor fireplace is set in an ashlar niche, beside a cast-iron safe with fluted stone sides, and two-leaf doors above.
In 1875 the building was recorded in the street directory as being occupied by Samuel Butler, druggist.
The building had been truncated to two storeys by 1974, and it was restored c1980. The interior was largely remodelled at that time. Some of the fittings of the chemist's shop were obtained by Bristol Museum.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/12/1645).

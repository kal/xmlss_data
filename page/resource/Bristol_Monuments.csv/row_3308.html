monument_desc The Scala cinema, located on the north-east side of Cromwell Road. The cinema was built by R. Wilkins and Sons, Bristol for Ralph Pringle to a design by W. H. Watkins and opened as Pringles Picture House in 1910 (Ewins 1984, 26). Pringle's Picture House was Bristols second purpose built cinema. The cinema was constructed on the site of three shops and two houses towards the bottom of Cromwell Road. It had a furnished lounge and grand circle reached by a marble staircase and could seat 1400 patrons. Pringle sold the cinema to Cheltenham and Zetlan Halls Ltd in 1914 when it became the Zetland Picture House (Ewins 1984, 26) The cinema was later run by Atkinson's Pictures Ltd and was known as the Scala from the 1930s (Ewins 1984, 26). The building was demolished in 1974 (Anderson 1983, 17).

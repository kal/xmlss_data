monument_desc Hermitage chapel of St. Vincent at Ghyston Cliff (located at the top of the cliff in the vicinity of Clifton Down Camp) The site was visited by William Worcestre in 1480 who made a number of measurements there:
"latitudo aulo heremitagij est .7. virgas
longitudo vie ad Ecclesiam de aula in Rupe predicta [St. Vincent's Rock] fodiata .16. virgas.
longitudo Capelle sancti Vincencij 8. virgas
latitudo Capelle sancti Vincentij .3. virgas
longitudo domus Coquine .6. virge" (Neale 2000, 44-47).
Worcestre's reference to "the hermitage and chapel" suggests the existence of a discrete oratory. Similarly the mention of a kitchen may imply the existence of a kitchen detached from the hermitage. In 1492 an episcopal brief offered an indulgence to those who assisted the warden of the chapel of St. Mary and St. Vincent and contributed towards its preservation.
The precise location of the chapel is unknown, although a drawing of the 1780s by Grimm shows a ruined structure to the south of Clifton Down Camp. It is possible that all trace of the hermitage may have been removed by subsequent quarrying. A glazed encaustic tile, part of a small Gothic window and sherds of medieval pottery are reported to have been found in the Giant's Cave (Hall Warren 1907, 204-205).

monument_desc Ivy Cottage, located some 35 metres to the north of Royal Fort Road. A building stood within Joachim's Close, "an closse of pasture", and was extant, and apparently ruinous, by 1581 when it was mentioned in a lease of the close by the Dean and Chapter to Elizabeth Dee as the remains of a toft.. A survey of the lands of the Dean and Chapter in c1618 also recorded this structure, describing it as "an house 40 foote long built within a stump of an house without [h]is covering 7 yards long" (quoted in Leech, 2000 44). It Is not clear whether this was the building subsequently known as Ivy Cottage. The building was, however, certainly extant by 1683 when it was occupied by John Millard. By 1699 Thomas Hickes, who owned the building as part of his lease of Cromwell House (BUAD 1412M), was letting it to Margaret Gilbert, widow. The Hicks family contibued to hold the However, James Stewart's drawings of the area of 1752 (BUAD 2756 & 2757) indicate that it was by that date either ruinous once more or being rebuilt. At some time between 1828 and 1881 a bay window was added to the eastern wall of the building and it was known as Ivy Cottage by the latter date (Leech, 2000 46). The building survived into the twentieth century but was subsequently demolished. Its site now lies beneath an extension ot the rear of the former Hospital for Sick Children.

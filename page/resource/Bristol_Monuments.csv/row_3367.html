monument_desc An Iron Age occupation site at Hallen Marsh. The site was identified in 1990 during observation of a geotechnical pit by Mr P. Anderson of Soil Mechanics. A 0.3 metre thick layer of Iron Age occupation material, including quantities of pottery, bone, charcoal and stone was noted. In 1992 the site was archaeologically evaluated by Glamorgan-Gwent Archaeological Trust as part of the evaluation work for the English approaches to the Second Severn Crossing (Lawler et al. 1992). This work confirmed an Iron Age date for the site, which was subsequently archaeologically excavated by Wessex Archaeology between December 1992 and March 1993 (SMR 20131).
A palisaded enclosure of Middle to Late Iron Age date was recorded, with evidence for timber, post-built structures and considerable artefactual evidence. The site was noted to have undergone several modifications and it was suggested that "it was constructed during a period when the Avon Levels were relatively dry, probably drier at least than the periods immediately pre- or post-dating the settlement." It was also observed that the area of settlement continued beyond the western edge of the excavated area (Barnes et al. 1993, 8-13).

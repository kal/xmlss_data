monument_desc Broadmead Baptist Church, with its main entrance in Union Street (formerly Lower Union Street), but before construction of that Victorian thoroughfare, entered from off of Broadmead. Founded in the mid-17th.century. A new meeting house (40ft. by 50ft.) was built in 1695.
Altered and extended principally in 1764-5, 1871-2 and 1875, to leave a building of 92ft. by 50ft. (of which only part of the east wall may have survived from the original). Gabled south wall - large gable between smaller ones (to aisles?). The west wall was rebuilt in 1876-7 to the new street. A new organ was built John Smith junior of the Bristol organ builders John Smith and Sons in 1843. It was apparently removed in c.1960 and installed at Downend Baptist Church, Salisbury Road.
The building was demolished and a new church was constructed in 1969. However, but one small part of the original building is thought to survive.

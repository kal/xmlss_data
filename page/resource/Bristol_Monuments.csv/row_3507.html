monument_desc Hill Cottage, 47 Brislington Hill, Brislington. In 1660 the owner was recorded as William Newman, a blacksmith, who owned the property now called Hill Cottage as well as lands around the house on either side of the main road. Parts of the house probably date from this period. By 1751, the estate had had several different owners, including Jeremiah Pearce, a Bristol sea-captain. His son, sold the estate in 1751 to John Newman which also comprised the present Church Hill House. The house eventually became part of the Clayfield-Ireland estate, and in 1837 a school was being run from the premises (Ireland, 1870: BRO St.LB/HM/1a & 1b). In the mid-19th century the house was enlarged. In 1884 David Cox Sherwell was recorded as the tenant, and in 1924, when the Clayfield-Ireland estate was being broken up, he purchased Hill Cottage and the next door property of Keeper's Cottage. In 1984, Avon County Council purchased the property which was resold in 1985 to a private purchaser.
The two-storey cottage is of rendered rubble with a pantile roof with brick end and ridge stacks. It has three window bays with pane sash windows. The cottage is grade II listed (Listed Building no. 901-1/49/447).

monument_desc Conduit house for St. John's Conduit. The conduit house was located at the junction of Broad Street and Tower Lane, against the west end of the south side of St. John the Baptist Church. Half-gable at south-west end, conduit below. Two gables along Tower Lane, frieze beneath, then plain wall below. Ball finials and other decoration to gables.
A new conduit house (BUAD 375M) was created in Nelson Street in 1827, after a series of serious traffic accidents which involved women and children standing at the conduit. The stonework of the conduit house was destroyed at the time of removal, March 1827.

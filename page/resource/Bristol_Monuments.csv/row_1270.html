monument_desc Holy Trinity Church, the parish church of Horfield, located on northern side of Wellington Hill. The church is of medieval date and the tower dates to the fifteenth century. The nave and chancel were rebuilt in the late 1840s to a design by the architect William Butterfield, who also designed the chapel of Horfield Barracks, Highbury Chapel and who worked on the Lord Mayor's Chapel. Alterations were also made to the fabric at the end of the nineteenth century and in the early-twentieth century. The church stands at the centre of an elliptical churchyard and the Horfield tithe map indicates that the churchyard has had that shape since at least 1843.
The church has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/53/1413).

monument_desc The Great House, Redcliff Street. The house stood on the bank of the river Avon at the west side of Bristol Bridge. A vignette in the border of Millerd's 1673 map records structure as a house of three storeys plus attic with a cross-gabled roof. A lantern is shown in the centre of the roof. There were large, apparently mullion, windows of between four- and fifteen-lights in the north face, and some of these appear from the vignette to have been oriel windows. A two-storey range ran north from the west side of the main block, forming the west side of a small courtyard on the north, riverward, side of the house. This had a covered gallery along its north side.
The Great House became a sugar refinery between 1689 and 1715, run by Isaac Hort until c.1700 and then taken over by his son Thomas (Hall 1965, 120n).

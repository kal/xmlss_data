monument_desc Public house, probably once a house. The 'Three Sugar Loaves and Devonshire House'. On the east side of Back Street. Attached. Three storeys and attic. Gabled front; side gable on north elevation; first-floor jettied to front. Entrance to left, 3 sash windows, cellar entrance below windows. Bays to first and second floors, glazed to fronts and sides, transomed. At attic, 3-light window; external collar above window. Lateral stack on south side near west end.
Back Street did a dogleg around north end of the building. Taken down in 1826, replaced by a shallower building (with mansard roof) due to road being widened. At the time of rebuilding a 'font' (possibly a mortar or stoop) was found (Braikenridge drawing, M.2277, no dimensions). 'By' William Bowden in 1826, according to drawing.

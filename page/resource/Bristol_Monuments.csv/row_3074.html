monument_desc The hermitage, Redcliff Hill, located at the south end of the Redcliff Pit burial ground. The hermitage was associated with the hospital of St. John the Baptist (BUAD 1055M) and was in existence by the fourteenth century. It was recorded, in passing, by William Worcestre in 1480 as lying to the west [sic] of the hospital's church (Neale 2000, 248-249) and was located in a garden, known as Redrock Garden (Leech 2001) which became a burial for the Society of Friends in the seventeenth century.
The hermitage is a small chamber cut into the sandstone cliff and appears never to have been the subject of archaeological recording. Taylor, visiting the monument in 1878 (BUAD 2465), noted that it was "entire and unaltered" with the original arch over the entrance. It had two seats cut out of the rock (Maclean 1878-79, 7-8). Of these only one is now evident and the rear part of the hermitage was cut away to create the foundations for the Coliseum public house in Redcliffe Parade East. A seventeenth-century freestone memorial is fixed to the north wall of the monument.
The hermitage is a Scheduled Ancient Monument (AV103).

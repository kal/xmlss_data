monument_desc No.55 Broad Street, located close to the corner of Newmarket Avenue and Broad Street. A building seems to have stood on the site from at least 1295 when William Scoche is recorded as the occupier and in 1384, when it was purchased by John Stanys the property comprised three shops with a cellar beneath. A document of 1437 records the property as a messuage, cellar, solar and garden held by Margaret Asscheley (Leech 1997, 46). By 1543 the property had been acquired by the feoffees of All Saints parish and a lease of that year to Lewes Robyns, skinner, mentions a stable at the end of the plot. In 1598 the feoffees sold the building to the executors of Robert Kitchen. It is not clear what works this entailed but some reconstruction of the building was certainly undertaken. The building appears to have been refronted in the seventeenth century and by the early 1860s was a four-storey, single-bay jettied building with a shopfront at ground floor level and bay sash windows at first and second floors. It had a smaller sash at third floor. It had a pantiled roof with a front gable (Winstone 1972, plates 17-18). The building was demolished in c.1864 to allow construction of The Grand Hotel.

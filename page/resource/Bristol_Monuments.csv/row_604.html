monument_desc Chapel of the Three Kings of Cologne, located on the south corner of the junction of Colston Street and Christmas Steps. The chapel was established in the late-medieval period to serve Foster's Almshouse (BUAD 423M). The chapel escaped confiscation of its property by the Crown in the 1540s because of its relationship with the almshouse (Maclean 1883-84, 248-249).
In the early nineteenth century there was a small flat-roofed building with two-centred arched openings at the top of Christmas Steps against part of the north end wall of the chapel. The south end before restoration consisted of a gable with square bellcote topped by a finial and weathervane. The entrance was by two-leaf door under a four-centred arch with panelled soffits, over which was a porch consisting of a pediment supported on brackets. Above was a six-light mullion and transom window with leaded panes, and, in the apex of the gable, a sundial.
The building was refaced and roofed in 1861 by Foster and Wood as part of the reconstruction of the almshouse. The chapel was restored in 1865 and the internal panelling and pews date from this time. The chapel is constructed of squared, coursed limestone and Pennant rubble, with limestone dressings and a pitched roof. In the west-facing elevation there is a 4-light window with panel tracery. In the north wall there is a single window with intersecting tracery, and the remnant of an arched doorway. The west gable has a doorway with double doors, an ogee hood with crockets and finial, carved tympanum, and carved head stops. Three statue niches above the doorway on carved bases, canted canopies with attached finials and pinnacles containing statues by Ernest Pascoe dating to the mid-twentieth century. On the gable there is a square bellcote with angle buttresses and a weathered ogee cap and finial. Inside are canopied niches either side at the east end, and a Royal Arms above the entrance. C19 half panelling and pews. In 1939 the decorative parapet was replaced by one of a simpler design, the belfry also (Notes by Philip Street).
The building has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/11/75).

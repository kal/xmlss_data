monument_desc Reverend Urijah Rees Thomas Memorial Drinking Fountain, located in a triangular parcel of land between Westbury Road and Stoke Road. The fountain was built in 1904. It is an octagonal structure of red and white granite and red sandstone ashlar, with a tiled, hipped roof surmounted by a square, copper-clad cupola with a clock on each face and a weather vane. Eight polished red granite Roman Doric columns and a central Old Red sandstone column support the roof, and there are four polished red granite basins with lion-head taps on the central column. A polished granite panel on the south side of the fountain carries an inscription commemorating the Reverend Thomas:
"THIS FOUNTAIN / WAS SET UP IN MEMORY OF / THE REV URIJAH REES THOMAS / BY HIS FELLOW CITIZENS OF ALL BELIEFS / IN GRATEFUL RECOGNITION / OF THE CATHOLIC SPIRIT AND GENEROUS ARDOUR / WITH WHICH HE GAVE HIMSELF TO THE PUBLIC SERVICE / AND ESPECIALLY OF HIS WISE AND TENDER CARE / FOR THE CHILDREN OF THE CITY / AND FOR ALL THE FRIENDLESS AND UNHAPPY, / THE DEFENCELESS AND THE POOR. / "THIS COMMANDMENT HAVE WE FROM HIM, THAT HE WHO / LOVETH GOD, LOVE HIS BROTHER ALSO." I JOHN IV 21 / BORN 16TH FEBRUARY 1839 / DIED 8TH MARCH 1901."
The fountain has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/24/1134).

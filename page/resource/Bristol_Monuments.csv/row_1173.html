monument_desc Stable located to the south of The Grange, on the western side of Park Road, Stapleton. The building is a one and a half storey structure constructed of Pennant sandstone rubble, covered with an ochre-coloured render, and with a hipped pantile roof. It was probably constructed in the seventeenth century. The west-facing elevation facing on to Park Road had been replaced, apparently in the early-eighteenth century. The new façade is of two-storey height and is in a classical style. There were three bays with ashlar mouldings forming segmental-headed arches. The central arch contained a doorway and those on either side small windows. At the shoulder of the arches is an impost-band and the arches have keystones. Above these is a plat band and above this there is a smaller segmental-headed arch and on either side a circular moulding. The parapet extends well above the level of the eaves and has a moulded coping.

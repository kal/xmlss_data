monument_desc No.9 Wine Street, located on the northern side of Wine Street. The building was constructed as the western of a pair of houses with No.10 Wine Street (BUAD 1696M) and was a jettied, gabled house of three-storeys and attic, of which the main, south-east facing, elevation and roof, at least, dated to the seventeenth century. The building had a one-window range with the ground floor comprising a shopfront. Above the fenestration consisted of a single canted bay window containing six-over-six pane sashes. A pentice over this extended across the entire width of the elevation. Theere was a similar bay at second-floor level, again with a full-width pentice roof. The attic window in the centre of the gable contained a pair of casements apparently set in moulded openings. There was an exterior collar above lintel level in the gable. A tall brick chimneystack was sited on the west wall. The building was drawn in 1826 as part of a view of this part of the north side of Wine Street by T. L. Rowbotham (BRSMG M2343). At that date there were large signboards fixed to the pentice roofs and the upper part of the shopfront advertising the building as "NOTTINGHAM HOUSE" and the shop as the premises of "GOSS & FOWLER."
Doorways through the party wall had been created between Nos.9 and 10 by the late 1880s. The building survived into the first half of the twentieth century but was subsequently removed and the site redeveloped.

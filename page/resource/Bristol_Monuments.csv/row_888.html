monument_desc The town gate known as St. Giles's, or King Edward's, Gate, at the northern end of Small Street leading to the Quay Head. On the south side the gateway consisted of an ogee-headed arch with a carved head above its apex; the right-hand springing supported by some form of corbel. Above the gate was a two-storey building, the lower part possibly including a roofed gallery.
This tenement was rented out by the Corporation in the early modern period, a landgable rent being paid by John Griffith in c.1650 and a fee farm rent by Thomas Collins in 1741 (Leech 1997, 119). The gate was demolished c.1777.

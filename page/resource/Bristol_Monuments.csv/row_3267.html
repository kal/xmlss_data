monument_desc Memorial to Princess Charlotte Augusta of Wales (1796-1817), Queen Victoria House, Redland Hill. Princess Charlotte was the only child of George, Prince of Wales (later Prince Regent and George IV) by his wife Caroline of Brunswick (Merritt 2002, 20-21). In 1816 she was married to Prince Leopold of Saxe-Coburg-Gotha (1790-1865), later King Leopold I of Belgium. She died from complications during pregnancy on 6 November 1817.
The memorial was erected in the grounds of Vincent Lodge (SMR 21031) after Charlotte's death in 1817 by Jacob Wilcox Ricketts who leased the building from 1806. It is a limestone obelisk comprising a tall shaft with a pyramidal top and a moulded base, set on a square plinth. There are fielded panels on each face and carved wreaths on the sides. A bronze tablet on the front panel carries the inscription:
"This Obelisk was erected by / JACOB WILCOX RICKETTS / to perpetuate the Memory of / HER ROYAL HIGHNESS / The much lamented and beloved / PRINCESS CHARLOTTE / of WALES and SAXE COBURG; Daughter of / HIS ROYAL HIGHNESS THE PRINCE REGENT, / and Consort to his Serene Highness PRINCE LEOPOLD. / This most illustrious Princess, the brightness of whose / Character surpasses all transcript, / was BORN Jan[uar]y. 6th 1796. Was MARRIED May 2nd 1816. AND DIED Novr. 6th 1817. / Thus were the Vine and Branch of true Whiggism and / Britannia's most blooming expectation, by one / irresistible stroke, together cut off."
The memorial has a Grade II listing.

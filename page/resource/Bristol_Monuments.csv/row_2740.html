monument_desc No.43 Broad Street. A medieval house of three and a half storeys plus cellar. Built out over the entrance to Tailors Court. Stone rubble was used for party walls with adjacent properties, also for a cross-wall and the rear ground floor wall. The remainder was of timber-frame construction, including some of the forward party wall with No.44. One roof of medieval timberwork had survived in addition to timbers elsewhere. The rear first floor room had originally possessed an oriel window overlooking what is now Tailors Court, but this had later been removed. The first and second floors retained their rear jetties and accompanying brackets, but the ornately carved and jettied front had been replaced in brick in 1825. A medieval chimney stack survived until the 1979 rebuilding. The rear ground floor wall incorporated two doorways and a window, and on the northern side were two small figures, apparently removed from elsewhere. One doorway, with a two-centred arch, was probably 14th.century in date. The second doorway and the window were more difficult to date, but could also be medieval. Demolition of the single-storey rear extension, formerly the cafeteria kitchen, in April-May, 1993 revealed evidence of a medieval structure, possibly a detached kitchen block, in that corner of Tailors Court.
Drawn by O'Neill in 1821, when known as the 'Guildhall Tavern', kept by Samuel Porch the pugilist. It was refronted in 1825 (Braikenridge Notes, a.5), occupied by Mr.John Willis as a grocer's shop. Some curious old carvings (rosettes) were found in pulling down the entrance doorway. In 1821 the front was of 2 bays, jettied at first-floor, the eaves also jettied. There was the access to Tailors Court on the left, with an entrance in the centre of the remainder, between windows, under the left of which was the cellar entry. To the left of the Court entry was a bracket, and above a frieze of quatrefoil decoration. At the first-floor a full-width oriel window, 14 lights wide, 1 light at the ends; pentice roof above. Two oriels at second-floor, each 4 lights to front, 1 to sides. Two gables to the front, each with single-purlin roofs.

monument_desc The Pithay Chapel, a Baptist chapel. The chapel replaced an earlier meeting house and the Rev. John Tommas preached the last sermon in the old building in 1791. The chapel was a three-storey building with hipped roof; three bays; sash windows; entrance on right-hand through double doors, above which is the plaque. The square rainwater head bearing the date 1700, formerly on the west side of the chapel, was re-erected at "the south top corner of the east wall of the new buildings close by" (Pritchard 1908, 304). The new Bristol Guide for 1794 refers to the chapel as "lately rebuilt". Heath's Guide of 1797 describes the chapel as having "Galleries on three sides supported by small pillars of Mahogany; and two stout pillars of Freestone, which support the cross Arch of the Roof; the whole spacious and beautiful".
The building was visited by John Pritchard in 1907 shortly before its demolition and he described it as "a simple brick erection with a name-stone inset over the entrance". The stone read "BAPTIST MEETING 1653"
His report is accompanied by a photograph of the front. The building was demolished early in 1907, although it had by then been occupied for some time by Messrs. J. S. Fry & Sons.

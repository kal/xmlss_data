monument_desc Linton Farm, Brislington Hill, Brislington. Referred in some documents as Linden Farm and was situated adjacent to the White Hart Inn on its eastern side. Although there is no documentary evidence regarding the property, the house is shown in a drawing made in 1826 by Rowbotham (SMR 20549) depicting a general view of Brislington Hill. The property was noted for supplying milk to Brislington village and to several companies in the city of Bristol in the late-nineteenth or early twentieth centuries. Francis Bishop lived at the farm and ran the dairy business from 1883-1903, and after his death the business was taken over by his son-in-law Thomas Fuller. The farm was owned by the Clayfield-Ireland family and when the estate was broken up in 1925, the Fullers left. The dairy business ended and the house was demolished in 1926. The dairy was in a wing of the farmhouse and was a large, whitewashed room with a stone floor. Cowsheds and pig sties lay to the south of the farm and formed two sides of the farmyard. A large ash tree stood near the entrance gate to the yard.

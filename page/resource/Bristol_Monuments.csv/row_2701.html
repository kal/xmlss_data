monument_desc Warehouse. Part of the complex of buildings known collectively as Back Hall. At one time the entry point for strangers' goods, later in use as the leather market. This part lay to the west, north and east of the main yard, which was entered from Baldwin Street. At the north end of the yard was the street entrance, with large double doors. Above the inner arch was a large sign or notice board, with, immediately above it, a 2-light window. At second-floor level was a clock, within a pedimented frame. The western range lay between the yard and the original line of the north end of Back Street. Four tall arches are illustrated by O'Neill (BRSMG M2234), the northernmost blocked in. Two storeys of windows and openings at various levels, mostly of 2 or 3 lights, lay above the arcade. The roof was not visible. Above the southernmost arch, which had a decorative keystone, were the City Arms, with the initials 'C.B.' and the date 1716, surmounted by a plaque bearing crossed swords, a crown, and the initial 'G.R.'. These last may refer to a rebuilding of the range, as the windows appear earlier in date.
Parts of the buildings were archaeologically excavated in 1958.

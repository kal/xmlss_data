monument_desc Colston's School, located on the north-eastern side of Temple Street. The school had been established and was supported through voluntary contributions before 1711 when Edward Colston established a new school building and schoolhouse (Manchee 1831, 176). The churchwardens and vestrymen were given responsibility for the management of the school and were instructed to meet annually on the Wednesday of Easter week to agree the Master's salary for the coming year, appoint the boys and appoint a clergyman to teach the catechism. The school was also to be inspected four times a year by four visiting clergymen (Manchee 1831, 177). The building was set back slightly from Temple Street, the front area being enclosed by a low boundary wall. The school building was a single storey in height with a hipped pantile roof with two dormers with casement windows. The main, south-west facing elevation had a five-window range, with the main entrance on the northern side beneath a 12-pane window. Colston's arms and and a plaque were fixed to the main elevation above the central window. The plaque bore the legend: "This Charity School was erected & endowed by Edwd.Colston Esqr. Native of this Parish Anno Dom. 1711 For the education in Reading Writing Cyphering and perfecting in the Church Catechism as it is now Established by Law And also for the Cloathing Forty Poor Boys of this Parish for ever."
At the beginning of the 1830s 30 boys were being educated at the school (Manchee 1831, 176).

monument_desc Two enclosures south of Rodbourn Gout. The sites are located adjacent to one another on the south side of Stuppill Rhine (centred on ST 53912 80998). The site was recorded by the 1841 tithe map which records "House, Homestead & Orchard" and "Potato Patch". The farm seems to have disappeared by the end of the nineteenth-century. The enclosures are shown on a 1946 vertical air photograph. One building is currently extant, a stone cow shed.
The earthworks at the site were surveyed by Wessex Archaeology during archaeological work in advance of the construction of the English approaches to the Second Severn Crossing.
The site (Area 1; Site 9) was visited by R. G. Jackson during the course of a desk-based assessment (SMR 20081) of areas of Bristol City Council owned land north of Avonmouth in December 1996. It was noted that the earthworks have been disturbed by ploughing.

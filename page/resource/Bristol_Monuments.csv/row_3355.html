monument_desc Enclosure in a field to the south of the Salt Rhine and to the west of Moorend Farm. The land was part of Rockingham Farm until the mid eighteenth-century when it was acquired by the Southwell Estate. A 1772 plan of the Southwell Estate records the field in which the enclosure is sited as "Old House Ground", although the map shows no building (BRO 26570).
The irregular subrectangular enclosure consists of a ditch and platform, the base of the ditch being 0.7 metres lower than the platform. The enclosure was in good condition in 1946 when it was recorded by the RAF air photographic survey but has since been ploughed. The feature was surveyed in 1997 by Glamorgan-Gwent Archaeological Trust as part of an archaeological evaluation (HER 20226) of fields on the opposite side of the Salt Rhine.

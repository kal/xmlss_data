monument_desc The Demerera House, Quay Head. The building was a three-storey, two-bay building with its entrance off Quay Street. The ground floor was a shopfront with the main entrance off Quay Street, centrally-placed between two bay windows. The upper floors had sash windows, and on the west side of the Quay Head frontage these had four lights while on the east they had two. On this frontage there were bay windows at all levels. The building has a hipped pantile roof from the south side of which a three-flue chimney stack projects. There was a cellar entrance in the pavement on the Quay Head frontage. In the centre of the south (Quay Head) frontage at first floor level was a niche which held a wooden ship's figurehead. This was rescued from the S.S. Demerera which was wrecked on its maiden voyage out of the Floating Harbour in 1850. The figurehead was placed on the building soon after.
The building was demolished in the early 1930s and the figurehead destroyed during attempts to remove it.

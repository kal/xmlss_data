monument_desc Chapel of St. Martin, located in the outer ward of Bristol Castle. The precise location and founding date of the chapel are unclear, though it has been suggested that it may have originated as one of the churches of the Saxon town (Leech 1997, 23). The chapel was noted by William Worcestre in the 1480s who records that while it was dedicated to St. Martin, it was then in the service ("deuocione") of St. John the Baptist. A monk from St. James Priory (BUAD 1024M) was meant to be present daily, but Worcestre comments that a service was only performed on Sunday, Wednesday and Friday (Neale 2000, 240-241). The chapel was apparently still extant in 1540 when a "praty churche" was mentioned by John Leland (Toulmin-Smith 1910, 87).

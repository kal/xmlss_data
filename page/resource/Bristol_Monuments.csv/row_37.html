monument_desc No.39 Old Market Street. The building was constructed in 1688 by the Bristol clay tobacco pipemaker Llewellyn Evans as the eastern of a pair of attached houses. It was originally a house of two storeys and attic with a double-depth plan.
In 1875 the building was recorded in the street directory as being occupied by John Nichols, butcher.
Nos.38 and 39 Old Market Street were converted to a single structure, apparently after 1896 since the Goad fire insurance plan of that year indicates that the property then comprised two shops. The main, south-facing, elevation has a slated ground-floor lean-to canopy on fluted fascia brackets to a flush twentieth century shop front with a glazed door to No.38. A half-hipped pantile roof was added to span the two properties.
The interior of the building was completely remodelled c1985.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/12/1643).

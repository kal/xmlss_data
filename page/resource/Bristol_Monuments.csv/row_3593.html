monument_desc Richmond House, located on the northern side of Lower Clifton Hill. The house was constructed around the end of seventeenth centuy and is a three storey, five-bay rendered building of Brandon Hill Grit in a random rubble construction with limestone dressings. It has a pitched pantile roof with large chimney stacks at either end.
The house was likely to have been constructed as a lodge or summerhouse (ie a merchant's second residence). A lease of 1701 to Whitchurch Phippen calls the house the "Old Castle" and required Phippen to spend £350 on the building within three years. The Freeman family owned the property in eighteenth century and were required to make the house and kitchen available for two days each year for the manorial court. In the 1790s William Sewell ran a boarding school for young gentlemen in the house. Mr J. K. Bragge (one of the honorary secretaries of the Merchant Seamen's Bible Society for the Port of Bristol) also had a boarding school for boys in the building in the 1840s.
Survey of the north-east facing elevation (SMR 21074) recorded three blocked window openings and found that the wall was constructed in Brandon Hill Grit (Pilkington 2002).
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/14/791).

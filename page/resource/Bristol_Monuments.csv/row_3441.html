monument_desc White Cross Court. The building is located on the east side of Wells Road, and is set some 18 metres back from the road. The building is a rendered house constructed of coursed, squared Lias limestone rubble bonded in a soft pale yellow limestone mortar with white lime inclusions. The house consists of a three-storey range with a single depth plan parallel to Wells Road with two-storey ranges at right-angles to the main range on its north and south sides. These were probably constructed as an integral part of the original building since no indication of a change in the character of construction is evident at the junction between any of the ranges. The main, west-facing, elevation is symmetrical and has an open square porch with a moulded cornice and a parapet. A painted stone tablet above the central first-floor window is incised with a garland and the legend "1786 WHITE CROSS COURT". There are chimney stacks in the gable-ends. The fenestration originally comprised sash windows "with fine bars and exposed cases." Internally the original plan of the main range appears to have consisted at each floor of a single room on either side of a central stair and landing.
The house was converted to an accountants office in c.1975 and a maisonette was created within the building. It was converted into a hostel for families in c.1977 and as part of this work windows were inserted in the side elevations of the main range. Works to convert the building into flats was begun in 2004. No historic fixtures or fittings now survive within the building.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/58/1406).

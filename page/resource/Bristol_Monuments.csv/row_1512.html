monument_desc A house located on the eastern side of Regent Street. The house was extant by the mid-eighteenth century and was recorded by the survey of the Manor of Clifton made by Jacob de Wilstar in 1746 for the Society of Merchant Venturers. The plot then comprised "One Tenement, Orchard, Garden and Paddock" which was in the freehold of Francis Freeman and was occupied by a Mr. Knight. The house measured approximately 15 metres from south-west to north-east by 8 metres wide.
The structure was still extant in 1828 but had been extended on the northern end. Also much of the paddock had been developed. The building may have been demolished as part of the works to create Merchant's Road in the late 1860s but had certainly gone by 1883 to make way for the construction of Nos.26-28 Regent Street.

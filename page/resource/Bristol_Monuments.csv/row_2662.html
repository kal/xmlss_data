monument_desc No.20 King Street, located on the south side of King Street. The building was constructed in c.1665. It is a rendered timber frame house with brick stacks to the right, gabled front and rear elevations to the main building and a pantile roof. The building has three storeys, an attic and basement. It has a double-depth plan with central stairs between the front and back rooms. The building also has a single-storey wing linked to a seventeenth-century, two-storey back block. It has an eighteenth-century shop frontage with a seventeenth-century door frame to the left with moulded stops and a Tudor arch, with a studded 9-panel door with decorative panels to the top and a 10+10 pane sash abve a segmental-arched cellar opening. The building was refenestrated in the eighteenth-century and was restored in 1991. Inside, against the side wall is a seventeenth-century stone fire surround with architrave, panels abve and brackets to a cornice. The building is joined with No.19 internally. It now forms part of a public house and restaurant.

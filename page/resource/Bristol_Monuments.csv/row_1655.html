monument_desc Don John's Cross. The cross was probably of medieval date and is recorded by a map of Kingswood Forest in 1610 at the junction of Church Road, Clouds Hill Road and Summerhill Road. This plan suggests that the cross was set on a stepped base and Pooley also reports that the cross was a circular column of freestone, raised on an octagonal base (Pooley 1868, 75). The first edition Ordnance Survey 1:2500 scale plan (Gloucestershire sheet LXXII.14) surveyed in 1880-1881 suggests that the original position of the cross was on the southern side of Church Road, opposite what is now St. George's Park. However, a location nearer the junction would appear more probable. John Latimer, the Bristol City Librarian and historian, reports that the cross was demolished in July 1749 in rioting prompted by the renewal of the Bristol turnpike trustees powers by Parliament in February 1749 (Latimer 1893, 274).
Elements of the cross were removed to the garden of the vicarage to the east of the church (to ST 62490 73570) during the nineteenth century. The remnants of the cross were moved to the front of The Glebes (to ST 62376 73624 approx) during the twentieth century.

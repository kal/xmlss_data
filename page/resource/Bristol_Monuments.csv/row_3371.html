monument_desc Cromwell House, located some 140 metres to the west of St. Michael's Hill. The building was a three-storey house of mid-seventeenth century date, with a cross-gabled roof and chimney stacks at either end of the roof. The building was recorded in a drawing by James Stewart made on 10th April 1752 (BUAD 2757; Bodleian Library, Western MSS, Gough Somerset 2 fo.15) which shows the south-east facing elevation. An undated later Victorian photograph (BUAD 3982; BRO 41881/1) records the north-east and north-west facing elevations and indicates that a two-storey extension had been added to the north-west facing elevation (centred on ST 58255 75455).
The plot was leased to John Hicks, mercer, by the Corporation in 1665 and the lease itself notes that Hicks had "lately erected and built a house three storeys in height" there (BRO 04435(5)). The building seems to have belonged to members of the Hicks family until 1713, when it became the home of William Hopton. The property was leased to John Elbridge in 1727, though he did not occupy it, and this lease was renewed in 1738. The lease was assigned to Henry Woolnough and leased by John Broughton from 1788. Samuel Edwards of Cotham House had a lease from 1791 and Henry Hunt, chocolate manufacturer, from 1823. The building did not therefore form part of the Tyndall estate.
The building was demolished between 1885 and 1903 (Leech, 2000 57-59). Its site was subsequently redeveloped for the H. H. Wills Physics Building of the University of Bristol.

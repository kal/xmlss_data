monument_desc Mason's Arms Public House, No.53 Old Market Street, located on the south side of the street. The building is a timber-framed, single-bay house of two-storeys and attic and was constructed with a detached kitchen to the rear. It was built in the late-seventeenth or early-eighteenth century. The main, north-west, facing elevation on Old Market Street is rendered and at ground-floor level it comprises a shopfront of ninteenth-century date. The main entrance door is on the northern side of the elevation and at the centre is a large window with panelled jambs. A leaded pentice roof divides ground- from first-floor. There is a single sash at the centre of the elevation at first-floor and another, similar window at second-floor. At the apex of the gable there is a small four-light casement window. The building has a cross-gabled pantile roof with a rendered chimney stack on the western side of the roof.
Internally the plan of the building appears to have consisted of a single room at the front and at the rear, with a winder stair between the rooms, on the western side of the building. The stair served all floor of the building, including the cellar. By 1875 the building had become a public house known as The Mason's Arms and was recorded in the street directory as being occupied by William Ward.
The building has been surveyed by Dr. Roger Leech for the Royal Commission on the Historical Monuments of England (BUAD 2068). One timber crossbeam with a stop survives at ground-floor by the main entrance door and the chimneybreast and stack for the detached kitchen is also extant. Another is extant in the rear room at first-floor level. The winder stair survives at first and second floor.
It has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/12/1648).

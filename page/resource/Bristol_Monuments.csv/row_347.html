monument_desc A medieval house excavated during development of the Crown Courts site in Small Street in 1990 (BUAD 313). The house was of Pennant sandstone rubble and had a vaulted cellar. An external covered entrance to the cellar was located on the north-eastern side of the building. The building was owned by the church of St. Werburgh and consisted, in 1533, of vault, cellar, two lofts and a stable. It was leased to Roger Barlow, merchant until 1559 when a new lease was made to Thomas Aldworth, merchant, who held it until after 1600. Ultimately, in 1902, the building was sold to the Bristol Waterworks Company.
The building was almost certainly completely destroyed after partial archaeological excavation in 1990 (BUAD 313) by the construction of the new Crown Courts building in Small Street.

monument_desc No.12 King Street, located on the southern side of King Street. The building was a jettied, gabled house of three storeys, attic and basement. It was constructed in the mid-seventeenth century as part of the development of King Street. The main, north-west facing elevation was two-window range and at ground-floor level there were twin entrances on the eastern side of the elevation. At the centre there was a bay; with a window to the west, with a cellar access beneath. The first-floor was jettied. The fenestration of the first and second floors comprised bays to the east and west, each with six lights, while the area between the bays consisted of an eight-light window. There were decorative panels beneath the windows. At attic level there was a single canted bay. The building had a steeply pitched pantiled roof, with a pendant in the apex of the front gable.

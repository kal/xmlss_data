monument_desc Holy Trinity Church, Stapleton, located on the south-east side of Bell Hill. The church is of medieval origin and the advowson of the rectory was in the gift of Tewkesbury Abbey, although it appears to have been exercised through its cell, St. James Priory. After the dissolution of St. James, the advowson of the rectory was acquired by Henry Brayne as part of his purchase of the St. James property in January 1544 (Gairdner & Brodie 1903, 37).
The church was rebuilt in 1857 to a design by John Norton in a Decorated Gothic Revival style. The building is of squared Pennant rubble with limestone dressings and a slate roof. There is a tower at the west end. The stained glass in the south chancel window was made by the London studio of Charles Eamer Kempe in 1887.
The church has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/33/1938).

monument_desc Free Chapel of Knowle. The chapel was probably constructed in the medieval period, although its origin, form and date of construction are currently (13/02/2002) unknown, as is its precise location. The chapel is consistently stated to be a quarter of a mile from Bedminster and may therefore have been in the area of Lower Knowle, around the area of the junciton of Bedminster Road and St. John's Lane. This, however, remains to be established.
The main evidence for the chapel comes from the documents associated with its sale at Dissolution in the mid-sixteenth century. The survey for its dissolution made in 1548-49 records that the incumbent was then John Bradley and that the plate comprised a single chalice. The chapel did have a set of bells, implying that it also had a tower or campanile (SMR 20690; SMR 20693; Green 1888, 91). It was also endowed with lands in the area. The chapel was granted to London gentleman, Richard Roberts, in December 1549 when it was valued at £73 6s. 8d. (SMR 21112; Woodward 1982, 64) and passed from Roberts to Sir William Sharington, a Wiltshire J. P. who was also master of the Bristol mint, on 11 January 1550 (Woodward 1982, 64n).
The date of demolition of the chapel is unknown.

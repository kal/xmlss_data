monument_desc No.1 Shed, otherwise known as Dublin Shed or A and B Sheds, which stood at the north end of Narrow Quay. The shed was erected in 1862. The building was a long, single storey transit shed with a hipped roof. It was constructed of a framework of girders with wooden panelling between and there were alternate double and single lights set in the roof. The roof was supported on columns, probably of cast iron, which were set in socketed granite blocks in the quayside. Some of these blocks can still be seen in the quayside at the northern end of Narrow Quay along with strips of iron probably positioned to allow sections of the wall on rollers to be opened. The shed was demolished c1937 at the same time that the section of the Floating Harbour south of St. Augustine's Bridge was culveretd to allow construction of the inner circuit road. During a watching brief on the redevelopment of the centre in 1999 further socketed granite blocks probably associated with the shed were exposed along with part of the quay wall (Burchill 1999).

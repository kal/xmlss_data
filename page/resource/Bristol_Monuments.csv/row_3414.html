monument_desc Council House, located on the north-west side of College Green. The building was constructed between 1935 and 1952 to a design in a Neo-Georgian style by the architect Vincent Harris and forms a crescent along the edge of the north-west side of the green. The building has a concrete frame and is of two storeys. It is clad with very wide, thin bricks. It has Portland stone dressings, including full-height porte cocheres at the middle, north-eastern and south-western ends of the building. There is also a large entrance porch at the rear entrance. The building has a leaded hipped roof with a gold unicorn at each end.
Internally the rooms are arranged off a corridor which runs the length of the building. The original fittings survive. There is an horological clock in entrance hall. The Council Room has painted ceilings by John Armstrong on the theme of Bristol and its history. Outside the building, in front of the main entrance, there is a scultpure of a medieval Bristol mariner by Sir Charles Thomas Wheeler R.A. On the parapet of the flanking towers there are sculptures by Jacob Epstein.
The building has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/15/72).

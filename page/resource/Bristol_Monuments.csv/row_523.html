monument_desc Exhibition pavilion located at the north end of Colston Avenue which was erected to house the Bristol Industrial and Fine Art Exhibition of 1893. The pavilion was sited on the then newly-completed culvert over the River Frome. The pavilion was a large temporary timber structure, with its main entrance at the southern end, beneath a wide gable between square turrets. A round-arched porch of five bays wide by one deep. The turrets had pyramidal roofs topped by lanterns. The main hall appears to have been a wide single-span, single-storey structure, with two-storey ranges running down the outside. Internal lighting was electrical, power being supplied by the newly-constructed Central Electric Lighting Station at Temple Back.
The pavilion was removed after the exhibition ended and the site was ultimately redesigned for Magpie Park (BUAD 649M). A drinking fountain (BUAD 66M) on the centre commemorates the exhibition.

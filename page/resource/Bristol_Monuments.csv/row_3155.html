monument_desc Barstaple's Almshouse, or Trinity Hospital North located on the north side of Old Market Street, on the east corner of Redcross Lane. The almshouse was founded in the late-medieval period and was apparently rebuilt in the seventeenth century. The hospital was U-shaped in plan with ranges down both sides and across the northern end of a courtyard running up from the street. Entrance into the courtyard via double gates between a pair of rusticated piers with ball finials. At the southern end of either side range, a 3-bay, 2-storey block with gabled end, a single 2-light window in the end of each storey, with, between storeys, a blank arcade of 6 semicircular arches; stacks at the rears of each block. An entrance to the ground-floor of each range immediately inside the gate. At parapet level, on the south-west corner of the western range, a sundial.
At the northern end of the yard, a C-shaped block, with plinth, cornice and rusticated quoins. The left wing of 1 bay facing south, cross-window at first-floor, 4-window range facing east, entrance second from the south, all other openings with cross-windows; the right-hand range probably the mirror opposite. A single-storey extension at the south end of the left-hand range, 2 cross-windows with a central entrance; parapet. In the connecting 3-window range, at ground-floor outer semicircular-arched doorways with rusticated voussoirs, and a central blank window with semicircular head and projecting keystone and imposts. At first-floor level of the range, a semicircular-headed niche containing a statue of John Barstaple, with flanking sash windows. Pantile roof. Stacks at the rears.
Manchee describes the building as consisting of twenty-four apartments, each with a fireplace and furnished by the residents themselves.The building also had a communal kitchen (Manchee 1821, 79).
The almshouse was rebuilt in the mid nineteenth century, in Pennant Sandstone and limestone dressings, by Foster and Wood and extended again in 1881.

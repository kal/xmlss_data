monument_desc Clifton Rocks Railway. The railway was built between 1891 and 1893. A tunnel was excavated behind the cliff face from Clifton down to Hotwells. There were 4 tracks at a gradient of 1 : 2.5 using four cars on same the hydraulic counter-balancing system used by some other cliff railways. The railway was taken over by Bristol Tramways & Carriage Co. in 1912 and was closed in 1934. Both the top and bottom entrances survive in a dilapidated state. The upper station south-west of St. Vincent's Rock Hotel still exists but is fenced off. The lower station on the Portway still displays the name of the railway but has been obscured by concrete buttresses and is almost unrecognisable in consequence.
The site was reused during the Second World War as an emergency radio studio for the BBC (SMR 10569).

monument_desc Shirehampton camp. The camp was probably established in late 1942 or early 1943 and covered the eastern half of Shirehampton golf course and the site of Portway Community School. The camp appears to have consisted of a a series of timber barracks huts and other storage buildings set on raised piles.
The U. S. Army was racially-segregated through much of the Second World War with African-American troops organised into separate units before late 1944. Most of these, including the units stationed at Shirehampton, were employed in logistical or support roles until manpower shortages during the Battle of the Bulge in December 1944 pushed commanders to start the process of integration. The 517th Port Battalion, Transportation Corps (now 14th Transportation Battalion) was activated at Shirehampton on 4 June 1943. By August 1944 542 Port Company and 543 Port Company (Transportation Corps), United States Army were stationed there and by December 1944 had been replaced by 648 Port Company. The eastern half of the camp was re-incorporated into the golf course after the war and the remaining half of the camp was demolished in the 1950s to enable the construction of Portway School.
Some elements of the camp were recorded during archaeological fieldwork at Grumwell Close in 1993 (SMR 20065) and the Portway School playing fields in 2004 (SMR 22020) including the recording of possible barracks huts (Webster 2005).

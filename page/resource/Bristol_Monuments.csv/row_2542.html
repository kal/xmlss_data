monument_desc Edmonds Trust School, located on the western side of Gloucester Road. The school was established in 1860 by the Rev. H. Richards. The land was given by the Horfield Trustees and the Edmonds Trust gave a grant towards construction costs. The school was consequently known as The Edmonds Trust School and taught both boys and girls. The building was a single-storey structure with two parallel ranges in a Gothic style and was constructed of coursed squared Pennant sandstone rubble with ashlar mouldings. The ranges each had pitched roofs. There was a single-storey porch at the centre of the south-eastern elevation. After 1880 the school accepted only boys as students.
The Ordnance Survey second edition 1:2500 plan of c.1903 records the church building as a Mission Room. In 1905 the school building became the Church of St. Edmund, King and Martyr and a number of alterations to the fabric were subsequently made, including construction of two aisles and the conversion of the single-storey porch into a tower. Internally the building had a timber arch brace roof resting on timber beams supported by hexagonal brick columns. There was a rood screen in front of the high altar and the side aisles had small chapels at their west ends.
The church closed in 1978 and the building was subsequently used as commercial premises.

monument_desc An earthwork feature located north-west of Pale Plantation, 125 metres to the east of Romney Avenue. Dr. H. Taylor observed the feature c.1946 and suggested that the feature was a barrow. However, an excavation by the Bristol Folk House Archaeological Club in 1954-1955 (SMR 21030) recorded sherds of medieval pottery "on or near the original ground surface" (O'Neil & Grinsell 1960, 68), and O'Neil and Grinsell suggest that it is therefore more probable that the feature is a post-medieval garden feature. When O'Neil and Grinsell visited the feature in the late 1950s it measured 60 feet long, 25 feet wide and survived to a height of 4 feet 6 inches (O'Neil & Grinsell 1960, 68). The feature currently measures approximately 26 metres from north-west to south-east and is 13 metres across.

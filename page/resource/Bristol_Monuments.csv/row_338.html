monument_desc The church of St. Augustine's Abbey. The abbey church was apparently the second abbey church, and it has been suggested that it was a larger church than the original (which may have been located on the site of the church of St. Augustine the Less) and was built as part of a repositioning of the abbey in the mid-twelfth century. The church was dedicated in 1170.
Fragments of the church remain, in particular the transepts of the present cathedral are known to rest on the lower part of those of the 1170 church. The plan of the church is unknown but the area of the nave of the cathedral was cleared of old walls when it was rebuilt in the nineteenth-century and the recovery of the full plan of the 1170 church may therefore be difficult.

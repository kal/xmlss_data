monument_desc A Norman first-floor aisled hall with an undercroft which was archaeologically excavated at the site of St. Bartholomew's Hospital in Lewin's Mead in 1974. The hall was built of stone (mainly Brandon Hill Grit although also with limestone, freestone) and was divided by two arcades which ran the whole length of the building. The steps leading to the undercroft were also excavated. Part of one of the arcades survived to a height of roughly 3 metres, although the arches had been rebuilt, as did other elements of the exterior walls. The kitchens and other discrete associated structures were not located.
It was noted by the excavator that the siting of the building respected the cliff to the rear of the property rather than the course of the river Frome at the front, although a slipway (leading to a postulated creek on the north bank of the river) was recorded. The excavator felt that the building was amongst the best preserved of the Norman structures of Bristol. Most of the structural remains recorded during the excavation were retained during the later redevelopment of the site.

monument_desc Bristol Museum and library, located on the east side of the junction of University Road with Queen's Road. The building was constructed between 1867 and 1871 to a design by Foster and Ponton.
Yellow brick with red brick decoration and limestone dressings, pantile hipped roof. Rectangular open plan. Venetian Gothic Revival style. 2 storeys; 7-window range. A symmetrical front has steps up to a ground-floor loggia with an arcade of 2-centre arches on columns, the outer pair of arches now blocked and rendered. First-floor band of shields, below an arcade of alternate large 2-centre arches, glazed, with trefoil heads, and narrow, pointed blind statue niches. Band below a coved cornice and parapet. Left return has 9 ground-floor arches containing triple lancets and round windows above, and 7 first-floor arches. Interior largely rebuilt c.1950. Later attached front area walls and stone railings.
The library moved to Deanery Road, c.1909 and the building then housed the geological and natural history collections of the Bristol Museum until theearly 1940s. Much of the decorative detail including pinnacles and parapet has been lost as a result of damage to the building by bombing during the Second World War. Restored for use as the refectory of Bristol University, became Brown's Restaurant, c.1990.

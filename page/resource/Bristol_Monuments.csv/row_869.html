monument_desc Tailors' Hall, located on the north-western side of Tailors Court. The building was the meeting hall of the Company of Merchant Tailors. The building was constructed in 1740-41.
Rendered limestone ashlar, roof not visible. Single-depth Great Hall and right-hand stair block.
3 storey; 7-window range. A 2-window right-hand service block set forward, Great Hall to the left with raised basement; the right-hand entrance has a fine late C17 shell hood with painted Arms of the guild with flanking winged cupids and a head of John the Baptist, on acanthus brackets, double 6-panel doors. Plain surrounds to square basement windows, with entrances at each end, separated by angled spurs; to the right-hand section, 9/9-pane sashes and a small 6-pane window over the door, and alternate segmental and triangular pediments to tall 20/20-pane sashes to 2-storey windows on the Great Hall. In the re-entrant is a conical lead hopper and downpipe.
In the late-nineteenth century the building was used as a Temperance Hall.
Now connected internally with No.41 Broad Street. Converted c1950.

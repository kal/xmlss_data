monument_desc A smithy on the corner of Fishponds Road and Hockey Lane was first recorded on the Stapleton Tithe Map of 1839. The accompanying apportionment lists the property as house, smith's shop and garden owned and occupied by William Daws.
A photograph of the property taken in 1973 indicates that the smithy was a single storey, rubble stone building with a pitched roof attached to the Farriers Arms (3266M). In 1973 the building was in use as Groce Garage Car Sales.
The building was subsequently demolished when Hockey's Lane was widened after 1973.

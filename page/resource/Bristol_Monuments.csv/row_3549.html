monument_desc Air raid shelter located in the playground of Fairfield School, Montpelier. The shelter was a trench shelter, square in plan, with four galleries (each 2 metres wide and 1.8 metres deep) and was built in 1940. The walls and roof were constructed of interlocking pre-cast reinforced concrete slabs. The shelter was constructed by excavation of a trench through the subsoil which was then lined and capped with the slabs. The whole was braced by a sectional steel frame fixed to plates in a cast concrete floor. Drains ran beneath the floor which contained small square drainage grilles.
The entrance was located on the west side of the shelter and was reached by a flight of steps down from the level of the playground. There were two square escape hatches in the roof and toilet enclosures, each measuring 1 metre square, at each end of the galleries. The shelter was supplied with electricity.
The shelter was stripped of its internal fittings after the Second World War and remained unused within the playground. The roof of the shelter was been damaged by the passage of earthmoving equipment during groundworks associated with the construction of temporary classrooms in the summer of 2002 and a decision was taken to infill it with hard core. A survey was undertaken as a result (SMR 21476; Longman & Stevens 2002; Wills 2003, 272).

monument_desc A boundary stone located on the north side of Lockleaze School. The precise position of the stone is not clear, but it is recorded by the Ordnance Survey third edition County Series 1:2500-scale plan (Gloucestershire Sheet LXXII.05) published in 1916 and this suggests it was sited on the field boundary at the north-west corner of a field named Lockleaze, adjacent to a footpath which followed the east side of the field.
The stone was carved from a single block of Pennant Sandstone with an angled top. It stood 2.03 metres high, although the lowest 0.6 metres was set in the ground and had a rectangular section measuring 0.32 by 0.21 metres. On one side there was a narrow recess 0.67 metres long, 0.1 metres wide and 0.05 metres deep. The function of this feature was not certain, however.
The stone was pulled out of its position, probably when Lockleaze School was built, and is now lying approximately South-east to north-west along the line of ditch metres to the (Andrew Buchan pers. comm.).

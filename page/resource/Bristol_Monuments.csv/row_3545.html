monument_desc Blaise Castle Iron Age hillfort.
An excavation was directed to the east of Blaise Castle by Bartlett in 1918 (SMR 20457) which recorded the foundations of a buttressed building, interpreted as the Chapel of St. Blaise (Bartlett 1919). Several evaluation trenches were dug across the monument in late 1956/early 1957 (SMR 20458) under the direction of Philip Rahtz (Rahtz & Clevedon Brown 1956-59).
By the later eighteenth century Blaise estate, including the hillfort, was owned by the Bristol merchant Thomas Farr, and Farr commisisioned the construction of Blaise Castle, a Gothick sham castle (SMR 780), within the hillfort. When Farr became bankrupt in the late 1770s he sold the Henbury manor to Dr. Denham Skeet, a Bath lawyer. The Castle and its setting were recorded in a number of drawings by Samuel Hieronymus Grimm made in the late 1780s (British Library Add. Mss. 15540 f.112-f.115). In 1789 Skeet sold Blaise to John Scandrett Harford, a Bristol banker. The landscape architect Humphry Repton was commissioned by Harford to landscape the Blaise estate, but is has not been established how much alteration to the hilfort was undertaken as part of these works. The site became a popular destination for day-trips by visitors to Bath at around this time and Blaise Castle is mentioned in Jane Austen's novella Northanger Abbey (written in 1798).
The hillfort is a Scheduled Ancient Monument (AV183).

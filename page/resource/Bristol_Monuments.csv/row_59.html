monument_desc No.10, Small Street. The building was warehouse of nineteenth-century date, but elements of the fabric were of medieval date. The building was of four storeys plus cellars, and five bays in width. In the central bay was the main entrance. The roof comprised parallel gabled roofs supported by 19th.century timber trusses, supported by posts 6.25in. square. There were two barrel-vaulted cellars of medieval and later date. In the ground-floor wall alongside Leonard Lane was a blocked medieval doorway with four-centred head (978A). At first-floor level, in the party wall with No.11, was one jamb of a doorway with a four-centred head and decorated spandrel (979A). On the far side (i.e. at No.11) was an even flatter rear arch, also with decorated spandrel. Between the ground and first floors of the warehouse, and towards the street end, was a large freestone chimney-piece with associated relieving arch of Pennant Sandstone (980A). Towards the rear of the party wall at third-floor level was an old chimney stack, possibly medieval in date (981A). At the south-east end of the rear, or south-west, wall were the remains of a gable, which, at the modern floor level, was 4.70m wide. On the ground-floor were two St. Leonard's parish boundary markers, nos. 12 & 13 (982A), with another, assumed to be no.11, on the Small Street frontage, where it lay alongside another stone, eroded away but presumed to be an old St.Werburgh marker (983A). In the south-east wall at first-floor level was a stone carrying the initials HI and JG (984A).
During demolition of the property a further doorway with four-centred head (985A) was discovered.

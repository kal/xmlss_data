monument_desc Hippodrome, located on the northern side of East Street, Bedminster. The building was constructed by Walter de Freece to seat 3000 as a theatre in 1911 (Anderson 1983, 20) and was a four-storey, eleven-bay brick building with square towers at its south-west and south-east corners. These projected a storey above the level of the parapet and had a pediment on each of the four faces. The main entrance was at the centre of the ground floor and was reached by a flight of five steps. It was converted into a cinema known as Stoll Picture Theatre. This use continued until 1940 when the building was damaged by bombing. The remains of the structure were demolished in 1954 and the site redeveloped (Winstone 1978, pl.78).

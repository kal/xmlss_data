monument_desc The Chapel of St. John the Evangelist, otherwise known as Knape's Chapel. It was a Chantry chapel founded by Thomas Knape, a former mayor of Bristol who died in 1404. Two priests were to be maintained in perpetuity to perform daily service and pray for the souls of Knape and his wife Agnes. In his will Knape directed that he should be buried in the chancel of the chapel (Wadley 1886, 68), suggesting that the building may have been complete, or at least close to completion, at the time of his death. The chapel occupied a plot between Welsh Back and Queen Charlotte Street. The north side was defined by Crow Lane and a plan of 1850 (BRO Plan Book C fo.98a) suggests that there may also have been a lane between Queen Charlotte Street and Welsh Back on the south side of the chapel. The form of the building and its position within the plot is currently unknown.
A survey of the Chantries of Bristol of February 1547/8 (BUAD 2591) found that there was then only one incumbent David Thomas who was then 51 years of age, had "noe other livinge or p[ro]mocion" and received £6 per annum. The property of the chapel brought in an annual income of £21 15 shillings. There was no plate but the Ornamentation was valued at 26 shillings 5 pence (Maclean 1883-84, 238-239). The chapel was probably dissolved in 1548 since a deed of that year refers to the site as the cemetery of St. Nicholas parish (Leech 1997, 168), the parish acquiring the site as an extension churchyard. The chapel building is likely to have been demolished then or soon afterwards.

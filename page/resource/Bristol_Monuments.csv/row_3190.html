monument_desc Nos.18-22 Victoria Street. Shops built in the early 1870s. Trio of buildings with one front. 4-storeys; 6-bay front. Arched ground-floor. Arched openings to first,second and third floors of Nos.18 & 20; square windows set back at first and second floors, round-headed windows at third-floor. Arched openings to first and second floors only for No.22, with square and round-headed windows respectively. Two-light windows to third-floor. Parapets. The roofs to Nos.18-24 were as one, with No.18 hipped to the north, Nos.20 & 22 gabled, and No.24 hipped to the south. No.20 is burnt out, now very derelict. No.22, damaged in the Blitz, was rebuilt behind its facade (but not the third-floor). The twin to No.22 was demolished for the end of the present Counterslip street. Rear elevations of 2 bays. To rear of Nos.18 & 20, tall single-storey structures with clerestory roofs on queen-post roofs, arch-braced collars between the said posts; brick floors. Extensive cellarage to all, stone walls and double brick barrel vaults; brick floors; all linked at one time, but subsequently blocked off from each other. Lift (5-ton capacity) in rear of cellar at No.22.

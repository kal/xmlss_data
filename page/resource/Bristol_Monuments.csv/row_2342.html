monument_desc Hemplow House. The site was purchased in c1680 by Sir John Hawkins from Arthur Sanders and a house was built on the site. Around 1800, Joan Hawkins, widow of John Hawkins, is recorded as selling Hemplow House to a Mr. Prescott upon her purchase of Gotley Lodge. It was subsequently sold to Edward Rollo Clayfield who had also purchased the adjoining estate of Gotley Lodge. In 1828, Edward Ireland Clayfield was recorded as the owner of both Hemplow House and Gotley Lodge. The Brislington Tithe Award of 1846 records Edward Ireland Clayfield as the owner with Daniel Tripp as the tenant. Upon the death of Clayfield's widow in 1865, the property passed to Thomas Priaulx Clayfield Ireland who sold it to Charles Gardiner, a wealthy Bristol clothier. In 1899, the property was sold to James Wise and remained in the Wise family until 1936 when it was purchased by Bristol Corporation. In 1939, the house was requisitioned as a base for the civil defence of Brislington. After the war the house was used as an ambulance and fire station. It was demolished in the late 1960s.
The site of the house and gardens is now occupied by the houses of Hawburn Close and Beeches Grove.

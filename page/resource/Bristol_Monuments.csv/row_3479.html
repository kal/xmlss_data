monument_desc The Convent of the Sisters of Charity. The building was designed by John Dando Sedding and was completed in 1894, after Sedding's death, by Henry Wilson. Its original function was as an industrial school run by the Sisters of Charity (an Anglican order) who were based in a building adjacent to the Church of St. Raphael in Cumberland Road. The building has a central courtyard. It is constructed of Lias rubble with limestone ashlar dressings. The main block has a two-storey, six-window range. The refectory building to the rear of the main group was designed by Henry Wilson and built in 1894. The grounds of the convent originally extended to the south-west of the main block, but the construction of St. Agnes Walk in the 1980s divided a large part of the original grounds from the convent. Within the former grounds on the western side of St. Agnes Walk is a stone shrine with the inscription "VIRGO IMMACULATA ORA PRO NOBIS 1917-1938 GRATIAS". The listing details for the convent note that within the building "many period details survive...".
A chapel to the south-west of the main block was built in 1900 and has a Grade II listing. The limestone rubble boundary wall of c.1891 surrounding the convent also has a Grade II listing.

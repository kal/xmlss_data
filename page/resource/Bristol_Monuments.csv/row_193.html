monument_desc St. Ewen's Churchyard, located on the west side of Broad Street,on the north side of the parish Church of St. Ewen (BUAD 334M). It was enclosed by high walls in the medieval period. At that time it probably measured around 12.7 metres from north-east to south-west and was 5.5 metres wide. By the end of the eighteenth century the area of the churchyard had been reduced to a rectangle of land on the north side of the tower measuring 4 metres (the width of the tower) by 2.4 metres.
The churchyard was not used for interment after the eighteenth century and buildings had been constructed on much of it by 1795. The site was developed for the rear part of Robert Smirke's Council House (BUAD 342M) in the 1820s.

monument_desc Hilhouse's Shipyard, located on the south side of the harbour, opposite Mardyke. The shipyard was establised by Alderman George Hilhouse covered an area between the Floating Harbour and Cumberland Road. It was recorded in a watercolour drawing by T. L. Rowbotham in 1826 (BUAD 1970) and by the Plumley and Ashmead plan of Bristol of 1828. The site included a dry dock [1049A] on the east side, with three slips (BUAD 1050A), then a (?) fitting-out or timber storage dock. Various buildings to the west and south of the slips, single-storey, some with lofts, roofs mainly gabled. Removeable footbridges across the mouths of the dry dock and slips. Later known as the Albion Dockyard, run by Charles Hill & Son, and various escort ships (corvettes and frigates) were built here during the Second World War. The dry dock, slips and some buildings survive, altered. The area of the slips is now (1996) the site of a boatbuilding yard and marina.

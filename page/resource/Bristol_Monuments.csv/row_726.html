monument_desc No.29 High Street, located on the west side of High Street. House, with ground-floor shop. Four storeys; 2-window range; roof not visible. Fully jettied. Two large open windows with central entrance, later two pairs of sashes. One column each end of ground-floor. Three sashes to first-floor. Pair of oriels to second-floor, with intermediate glazing. Two oriels to third-floor. Parapet - a line of washing beyond indicates a probable flat roof. Occupied by Llewellin and Simmons, woollen drapers, in 1810. By that time the shop, kept first by Llewellyn's father then himself, was "almost the only one remaining in Bristol of that description without any windows & open entirely to the Street the Goods being exposed on bulks as they are called" (Braikenridge notes, 44). By August 1825 it was let to Ringer, a snuff concern, after being a woollen draper's for nearly a century.

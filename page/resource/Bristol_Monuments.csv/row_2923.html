monument_desc Nos.4-7 College Green. A terrace of 4 houses, now offices. Left-hand house c.1759, right-hand threesome c.1760, possibly by George Tully; converted 1989. Brick with limestone dressings. Pantile hipped roof to left-hand two, slate mansard to No.6, and hipped at the end. Double-depth plan. Mid Georgian style. Each of 3 storeys, attic and basement. All with raised ground-floors, the left-hand three entered off College Green, the last off Trinity Street. No.4 is 3-window range with right-hand entrance; the next two are both 2-window ranges, arranged off-centre, probably originally with left-hand entrances; No.7 is a two-window range, with a symmetrical 3-window range to the return. Rusticated quoins and strings to the left-hand house, and lesenes to the right-hand three. Attached front area walls and spear-headed railings.
Investigation of the stripped walls in 1989 showed that No.4 had a front wall entirely of brick, with all windows remodelled in the 19th.century. The remainder, also the return to Trinity Street, had rubble walls at basement, ground, first and second floors, with brick for the third-floor. At No.5 the ground-floor had been rebuilt, the windows at first and second floor levels remodelled in the 19th.century. The front ground-floor of No.6 had been altered. Many of the walls, including some containing brick, were bonded in pinky mortars. It is possible that Nos.5, 6 & 7 incorporated the remains of the 'The Masonry', a building known to have previously been on the site.
Nos. 4 & 5 were formerly annexed to the Royal Hotel. All were gutted in 1989, leaving only the external and main party walls standing. The property adjacent to Trinity Street is now known as No.1 of that thoroughfare.

monument_desc Clifton Pool, located on the northern side of Oakfield Place, Clifton. The pool was created as a private swimming pool run on a subscription basis, and opened in 1850. The façade of the building is of three storeys with five bays, the entrance being set in the central bay. The pool was to the rear of this with a gallery on the eastern side. Between 1883 and 1885 the building was reorganised. The eastern two bays of the frontage were converted to The Victoria public house, the gallery building was removed and the pool enlarged into much of the space it had occupied. A small cottage was built where the southern end of the gallery had stood. The bath was shortened and a tepid bath was constructed across the width of the northern end of the site. The width of the pool was reduced in 1934 and the tepid bath was demolished in 1961. The pool itself was refurbished at around the same time.
The building has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/3/10039).

monument_desc Offer's clay tobacco pipe factory, located on the east side of Norfolk Place, Bedminster. The pipe maker Richard Offer moved from Great George Street, St. Philips to George's Barton [modern Norfolk Place] in Bedminster in 1858. He was listed in the street directories there as a pipe maker from 1859 to 1866 and in the 1861 Census he was noted as employing 2 men and 5 women at his premises. Offer was still at Georges Barton according to the 1871 census but had died by 1888. It seems likely that the business was then carried on by Charles Tovey who was listed in the street directories as a pipe maker at No.143 East Street and Nos.1-3 Norfolk Place, Bedminster from 1889 to 1891 (Price et al 1979).

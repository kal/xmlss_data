monument_desc War memorial, located at the centre of the traffic island at the junction of Canford Lane, High Street and Westbury Hill. The memorial was erected c.1920. The memorial is a limestone ashlar obelisk on a square plinth with swag. On each face of the first and second stages are marble plaques recording the names of the fallen. The south-east face of the monument bears the carved inscription from the memorial to the Battle of Kohima in Burma [Myanmar], which is attributed to Major John Etty-Leal and derives from John Maxwell Edmonds (1875-1958): "WHEN YOU / GO HOME / TELL THEM / OF US / AND SAY / FOR YOUR / TOMORROW / WE GAVE / OUR TODAY."
The memorial has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/26/2134).

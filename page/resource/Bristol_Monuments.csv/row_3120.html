monument_desc The Lodge Street Chapel at the south-west corner of the junction of Lodge Street and Trenchard Street.
The Countess of Huntingdon's Connexion built the chapel in 1830 when it left the Salem chapel in St. Augustine's Parade. The structure had five bays and the Trenchard Street frontage contained five tall, three-light windows with four-centred arch heads and hood-moulds above. The windows held stained or painted glass. The central three bays were within a three-sided apse projecting towards Trenchard Street, which had crenellations along the roof line. A string line ran across the full width of the building at the height of the top of the apse. Two crenellated pillars projected from string line at the ends of the facade. In the centre of the pediment was a small single light with a four-centred arch and a hood-mould above. There was a finial at the crown of the pediment.
An organ was built for the chapel by John Smith senior of the Bristol organ builders John Smith and Sons in 1832.
The building became a printing works before its demolition in 1967. A petrol filling station was later built on part of the site. The organ was removed and was reinstalled in the United Reform Church at Rayleigh in Essex.

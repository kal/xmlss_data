monument_desc Prior House, located on the south side of Robin Hood Lane. The building was constructed c.1750 as a house for Richard Going, a Bristol pewterer, and is recorded in the Land Tax assessment for that year as "Mr. Going's new house" (Leech, 2001 9). The house was orientated north south and stood within a rectangular walled garden, being sited against the west wall. As built, it was of two storeys and basement and was constructed of white Lias limestone. The ground-floor plan was of two rooms with a brick-vaulted underground cistern at the south-east corner of the building (Leech, 2001). There was a brick chimneystack in the west wall. The building appears to have been extended between 1828 and the early 1880s. The house survived until 1949 but was subsequently demolished.
The remains of the building were recorded in 2001 by Dr. Roger Leech (BUAD 3811; Leech, 2001).

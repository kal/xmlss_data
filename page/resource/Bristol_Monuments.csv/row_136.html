monument_desc Liverpool, London and Globe Assurance Offices. The building was constructed to a design by W. B. Gingell between 1865 and 1867. Limestone ashlar with dressings. Open banking hall with double-depth plan offices. 3 storeys and attic; 5-window range. The sculpture on the façade is by T. Colley. An elaborate symmetrical front is articulated by paired giant order of columns on pedestals, Composite on ground floor and Corinthian across the upper 2 floors, single to the sides. An attic storey with a pedimented centre. The attic has paired caryatids beneath the entablature, octagonal urns to the ends, a small segmental pediment within the main pediment, half segmental pediments each side.
The building was reconstructed behind the facade in 1977.

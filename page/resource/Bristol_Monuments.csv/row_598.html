monument_desc No.19 Broadmead, located on the north side of Broadmead. The building was a gabled house of two storeys, attic and basement and was of probable seventeenth-century date. The main, south-west facing, elevation occupied a 4 metre wide frontage on Broadmead and the building extended back 8.9 metres from the street. The ground floor was occupied by a shopfront with an entrance on the western side and an adjacent shop window. The first floor and attic storeys were jettied and there was a central canted bay with a single 10 by 10 pane sash window. The attic storey was separated from first-floor by a full-width pentice. In the bay at attic level was a 6 by 6 pane sash. There were lateral chimneystacks on the eastern wall. There may also have originally been a detached kitchen to the rear of the building.
The building was demolished in January 1931 (Notes by P. E. Street) and its site now lies beneath the front part of No.84 Broadmead.

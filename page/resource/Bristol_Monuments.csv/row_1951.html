monument_desc M Shed, Princes Wharf. At the end of the Second World War the Port of Bristol Authority decided to develop a general cargo handling facility at Prince's Wharf. This was completed in 1951 and M Shed was constructed as part of the scheme. The building is a two-storey transit shed with a steel frame and is attached to L Shed (BUAD 1578M). M Shed was used by the Bristol Steam Navigation Company. From 1954 the company operated its Bristol-Dublin trade using M Shed and in 1965 a Scotch derrick crane was installed at the west end of the shed. This was removed in 1969 when BSNC moved its operations from Princes Wharf to Avonmouth. M Shed became a general cargo shed and remained so until the port use ended in the 1970s. The building was converted into an Industrial Museum for the city in 1977.

monument_desc A crossing over Brislington Brook in St Anne's Wood was in existance by the time of the Brislington tithe survey in 1846 (BRO EP/A/32/9). It is not clear from this intial depiction of the crossing whether this was purely a bridge or ford, but by the first edition Ordnance Survey plan (Somerset Sheet VI.08) published in 1886 there does appear to be a bridge and ford in existance. The present bridge is constructed of pennant sandstone with a brick arch. The stone work has been repointed with modern cement and the path over the bridge has been relain with gravel cement. The ford lies immediately to the north of the bridge and partially survives as a series of squared blocks lain on the river bed. Longer blocks create a formalised kerbed edge to the ford on its north side. The original approach to the ford partially survives on the east bank, but has been covered on the west bank. It appears that the ford has been truncated by the construction of the present bridge suggesting that the ford went out of use by the late nineteenth century.

monument_desc A surface air raid shelter of Second World War date which was located on the eastern side of The Seahorse public house, in a small garden in the grounds of the Bristol Royal Infirmary, and was orientated roughly north-east to south-west. The site, part of the site of Nos.4-8 Terrell Street, was requisitioned from the Bristol Royal Infirmary and the Terrill & Foskett Trust on July 1940 for the construction of the air raid shelter. It was released on 30 October 1947 (BRO 37698).
The structure measured 11.4 metres long by 2.3 metres wide (standing 2 metres high) and appears to have been a standard double 50 person capacity shelter with traversed entrances at either end. It was constructed in brick (English Bond) with slightly overhanging flat concrete roof slabs. Small ventilation holes or lights were positioned at approximately 1.5 metre intervals along the longer sides of the shelter. The single width entrance doorway was at the eastern end and the partial demolition of the structure revealed that it was subdivided into (apparently three) modules internally, each with its own entrance doorway.
The development of a new children's hospital on the site led to the demolition of the two eastern bays of the shelter in the autumn of 1997. The remaining western module was demolished in late February 1998.
No plan or elevations of the building, internal or external, were made before demolition although photographs of the structure are held within the archive for an archaeological field evaluation of the site in 1998 (BUAD 3298; Longman, 1998).

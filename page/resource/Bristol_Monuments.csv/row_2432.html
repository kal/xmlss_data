monument_desc Whiteladies Picture House, located on the west side of Whiteladies Road, to the north of the junction with Melrose Place. The building was designed by James Henry LaTrobe and Thomas Weston and was constructed in 1920-1921. It is of Limestone ashlar and render with a square tower over the foyer. The design originally included a restaurant and ballroom. When the cinema opened, the auditorium was 105 feet (32.1 metres) long by 68 feet (20.8 metres) wide and could accommodate 1298 patrons, 1016 at ground level and 282 in the balcony (Staples 2002, 7). The original interior design for the interior included elaborate plasterwork and this survives in many places.
The proscenium arch was removed in the early 1950s. The cinema was converted to three screens in 1978. The balcony became the main screen and the auditorium was split along its long axis to produce two screens. Use of the building as a cinema ceased on 15 December 2001.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building No.901-1/3/10069).

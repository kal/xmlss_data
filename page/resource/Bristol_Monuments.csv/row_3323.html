monument_desc H.M.S. Flying Fox, located on the south side of Winterstoke Road. The building was constructed in 1940 as a Territorial Army Centre for the Gloucestershire Regiment and the building remained largely unaltered after its construction. It is a three-storey brick structure designed in a Neo-Georgian style. The main, south-facing, elevation has a central entrance porch with pilasters topped by the head of a sphinx (in reference to the badge of the Gloucestershire Regiment) extending up to mid second-floor level set on either side. Between the first and and second floor windows is the date "1940".

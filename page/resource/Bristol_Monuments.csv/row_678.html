monument_desc College Square, previously known as Lower College Green. Millerd's 1673 map shows the area already laid out, with some buildings on each of the four sides. The Green itself has a medieval origin as the lower cloister of St. Augustine's Abbey.
In the mid nineteenth century (BUAD 3790; BRSMG M4342) the space at the centre of College Square was a square grassed area, enclosed by iron railings and with a circular planting bed in the middle. There was a large tree towards the south-west corner of the space and other smaller trees elsewhere within the space. There was also a lamp post immediately outside the railings at the south-west corner.

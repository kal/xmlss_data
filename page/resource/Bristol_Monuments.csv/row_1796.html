monument_desc A roughly circular earthwork on Kingsweston Down. The earthwork is roughly 60 metres in diameter bounded by a slight bank which is more prominent on the south side - with a shallow outer ditch. Further west is a cross-ridge dyke which may be an outwork to the hillfort (SMR 781). Excavation by Rahtz (1956) across the east side of the circular earthwork revealed a rock cut ditch 2 feet deep and 2-3 feet wide. This can be tentatively identified as a palisade ditch with an exterior berm, enclosing farm buildings or huts. Pottery from the fort and circle show primary occupation in a late phase of the Iron Age "A" period, but 13 sling stones in the secondary fill of the circle's ditch suggest Iron Age "B" occupation. The finds from the excavation are held by Bristol Museum and Art Gallery.

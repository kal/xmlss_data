monument_desc The Mansion House, located on the northern side of Fort Lane [modern Royal Fort Road]. The house stood some 4.5 metres back from the road with a small, walled front garden and was accessed from a gateway on the lane flanked by two large gateposts with finials.
The building was a two-storey, five-bay house dating to the first half of the eighteenth century. It was extant by 1747 (BUAD 2766; Leech 2000, 46) and, given the location of the house within the plot for Elbridge's Charity School (BUAD 933M), is likely to have been built as the dwelling for the master of the school under the will of John Elbridge. The house was aligned from north-east to south-west, parallel with Fort Lane, and the main frontage measured 35 feet (10.7 metres) long and the building was 25 feet (7.6 metres) deep. The fenestration of the main elevation comprised sash windows set within openings with ashlar dressings which had segmental-arched pediments above. The central ground-floor bay contained the main doorway which was set in a segmental-arched opening which also had ashlar dressings. The upper part of the side, south-west facing, elevation appears to have been blank. The pitched roof was tiled and had tall chimney stacks at either end. The building also seems to have had a single-storey rear extension.
The building survived until c.1905 when it was removed to make way for the construction of new wards for the Bristol Children's Hospital. The site of the house now lies almost entirely beneath the Faculty Building.

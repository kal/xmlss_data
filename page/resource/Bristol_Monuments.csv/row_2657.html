monument_desc A round-nave church built by the Knight's Templar, probably in the mid twelfth century. The history of the church is unknown but it was presumably demolished to make way for the construction of the later parish church.
The monument was first observed during works to the floor within Temple Church in the 1870s and was archaeologically excavated in 1960 by Andrew Saunders (BUAD 485). Saunders's excavation established that the church was fragmentary, but enough remained for a reconstruction of the plan of the church which consisted of a round nave enclosed within an ambulatory, while Saunders indicated it had an apsidal chancel. It probably formed part of one of the larger preceptories in southern England but the plan of its precinct is unknown. The location of archive for the excavation is unknown and the extent of attrition caused to the archaeological deposits associated with the church is therefore unknown.

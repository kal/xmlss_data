monument_desc Holy Trinity Church, located on the north side of Hotwell Road. The church was constructed in 1829 to a design by C. R. Cockerell and consecrated in 1830. The site was purchased for £800 and construction cost £10000, with the funds necessary being raised though public subscription. The building is of Bath stone ashlar with a slate roof. The original interior was by T. Burrough. The church could accommodate 1654 worshippers. The seating plan is held by Bristol Record Office (BRO EP/A/45/2 pp315-317). The church was damaged by bombing in 1941, being gutted, and the interior was completely rebuilt in 1957.
The building has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/14/1461).

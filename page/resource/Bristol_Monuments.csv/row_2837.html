monument_desc The conduit of St. Mark's Hospital, also known as Gaunt's Pipe. The conduit was constructed in the thirteenth century to supply water to St. Mark's Hospital and to St. Augustine's Abbey. A charter of c1205-35 made by Roger, Lord of Clifton granted the springs and watercourses arising in the croft, now the Gorse Lane area, to the Abbey of St. Augustine. The property containing a spring close to present Constitution Hill was granted to Henry de Gaunt master of St. Mark's Hospital at College Green in c.1235-45, thereby giving the right to the water to the Hospital of St. Mark and St. Augustine's Abbey. The conduit was also recorded by the 1373 perambulation of the new County of Bristol (Harding 1930, 154-155).
The conduit collected water from several springs on the hill below Bellevue and elsewhere above Jacobs Wells Road into a series of underground chambers. These include the possible "bet tohorah" (BUAD 949M), which is a Scheduled Ancient Monument, at the foot of Constitution Hill (ST 57689 72864), a late-medieval chamber, accessed via a moveable slab at the junction of Gorse Lane and Jacob's Wells Road (ST 5770 7293), and a further medieval chamber on the south side of the junction of Constitution Hill and Jacob's Well Road (ST 57692 72843)(BUAD 1191M). A plan by William Halfpenny of 1742 shows the chambers along Jacobs Wells Road connected by conduits and these combined to a single watercourse beneath the centre of the road south-east of the junction of Constitution Hill and Jacobs Wells Road. According to a plan of mid-nineteenth century date in the Corporation Plan Books the conduit then ran down Jacobs Wells Road and along St. George's Road to a conduit head at Unity Street. There was a feather close to the junction of York Place and St. George's Road to serve St. Augustine's Abbey. This ran south-east to the St Augustine's conduit head in the lower cloister, now College Square, and the water was then presumably carried to the north-east to serve the refectory of the abbey. The course of this conduit may have been diverted away from an original route along Frog Lane when the Park Street viaduct was built.

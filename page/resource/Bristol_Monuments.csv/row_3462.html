monument_desc Gazebo located in the north-west corner of the garden of Southmead Manor Gardens (SMR 20100). The structure was built in the late eighteenth-century. It is a two-storey structure with a two-window range and stands against the south-west and north-west boundary walls. It is constructed of coursed carboniferous limestone with occasional Dolomitic Conglomerate and has dressings probably of Dundry stone. There is a chimney breast and fireplace inside the building as well as a red brick bench along the east wall. The exterior of this wall was rendered with stucco, and traces of this survive. The structure was derelict, the interior strewn with rubble and the walls overgrown by foliage when it was visited in February 1995, in the course research for a desk-based assessment of the site (Boore 1995).
The gazebo is listed Grade II (Listed Building Number 901-1/53/1823).

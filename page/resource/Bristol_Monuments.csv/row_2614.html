monument_desc Welsh Particular Bapist Chapel, located on the southern side of Upper Maudlin Street. The plot was sold to the trustees of the chapel in 1839 and the chapel was built, of Pennant sandstone rubble in 1840-1841. It was recorded by the 1880s 1st edition Ordnance Survey map. The chapel was given new furniture in 1901 and continued in use until its closure in c.1970. It was demolished in 1978 to allow construction of an underpass to the Bristol Royal Infirmary beneath Upper Maudlin Street (Dawson, 1982 43). The entire plan of the building was recorded during an archaeological excavation in 1999 directed by R. Jackson (BUAD 3399).

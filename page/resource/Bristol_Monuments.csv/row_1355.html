monument_desc An earthwork on Purdown. The earthwork was first noted by Dr H. Taylor according to Tratman who describes the features as consisting of "half a circle with an outer ditch and an avenue" (Tratman 1946, 167). O'Neil and Grinsell observed the feature in the 1950s, reporting that it measured 340 feet from north-west to south-east, was 50 feet wide and survived to a height of 2 feet 6 inches. Grinsell and O'Neil argued that it was likely to be "some form of post-medieval landscape gardening" (O'Neil & Grinsell 1960, 68).
The monument is 80 feet in diameter, banded by a bank and outer ditch, adjoined by an enclosure or avenue about 115 yards long by 35 feet wide, enclosed by parallel banks and with traces of an outer ditch on the north-east side. The Ordnance Survey map of 1921 shows both the semi circular and the long enclosure, both planted with trees. The monument was surveyed by Dr. Mark Corney in December 2003 (Corney 2003). The survey demonstrated that it was of post-medieval date and was likely to have been created as a feature for the landscape garden of Heath House as Grinesll and O'Neil had suggested.
The monument was a Scheduled Ancient Monument (SAM No. AV 26) but was descheduled on 10 December 2004.

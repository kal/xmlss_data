monument_desc St. Leonard's Church. The church stood over the town gate at the junction of Corn Street and St. Stephen's Street. Barrett describes the interior (Barrett, 1789 506). A flight of thirteen steps led to the only door of the church, at the west end of the north aisle, which was 35 feet long. The south aisle was 55 feet in length and 30 feet to the ceiling, the width of both aisles being 30 feet. Above the Corn Street gate stood "a plain freestone tower, 65 feet high from the ground, and 18 feet in front from north to south and from east to west 10 feet, having four small freestone pinnacles at the top, surrounded with freestone battlements". There were two bells, one large and one small. Against the east window was an altar. The church had a small crypt (crowde?).

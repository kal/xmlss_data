monument_desc No.60, Wine Street, located on the south side of Wine Street. The building was a jettied house of three storeys and attic with a cross-gabled roof which was of seventeenth century date. The main, north-facing, elevation had a two-window range with the entrance door at the centre of the elevation. The ground floor contained a shop while there were two bays at both first- and second-floors. There was a single attic window in the gable end.
The building was drawn by James Stewart in 1746 and was then occupied by a Mr. Stretton.

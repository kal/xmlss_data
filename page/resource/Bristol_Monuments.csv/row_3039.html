monument_desc Moravian Church, located on the south-east side of Upper Maudlin Street. The Moravian Society in Bristol was formed in 1748, the original chapel being located in Avon Street, St. Philips. It was relocated to Maudlin Lane, the land being bought in 1756; the chapel opened the next year. Various alterations were made, with substantial rebuilding in 1896 to provide a street-level chapel with hall beneath. This consisted of rendered walls with a brick superstructure and tiled roof. The original building had rusticated quoins and tall segmental-arched windows, with four bays on each side. There was an Upper chapel of 16.7 metres by 9.18 metres, with, at the south end, a re-used gallery of c.1757 with bowed centre supported by two Tuscan columns. The organ was built in 1827 by John Smith senior of John Smith and Sons, Bristol organ builders. The pulpit was added in 1854.
The chapel stood on the north side of the burial ground and had a large arched gateway leading to it. The four sides of the burial ground were lined by buildings associated with the brethren. The minister's house stood to the east of the church. On Lower Maudlin Street were the Old Sister's Houses, while on the south side fronting Blackfriars was the day and Sunday school. West of this building was the Old Brethren's House while the west side of the burial ground was open until a stable block was built (by the late 1880s). The site, including the burial ground and other buildings, was sold for redevelopment, 1971 and the chapel was removed before Easter 1973. The site was subject to archaeological excavation in 1973 and 1976.

monument_desc The Lord Rodney Public House situated on the north side of Two Mile Hill Road at the junction with Brockhurst Road. The buildling consists of a two sorey double depth plan with pan tiled pitched roofs. The side walls are constructed of rubble pennant stone, the south elevation is currently (o6/05/2011) rendered. The earliest depiction of a structure on the site is on the William Maule 1803 map of St George and an Inn called the Lord Rodney was in existence by the time of the St George tithe apportionment of 1842. Numerous alterations have been carried out to the building, which is predominantly 19th-century but may have a late 18th-century core.

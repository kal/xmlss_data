monument_desc Eastville Park. A large late nineteenth-century municipal park. The main area of land for the park was bought by the Corporation from Sir Greville Smyth in February 1889, although further land purchases took place from 1893 to 1963 (Hillary Taylor Landscape Associates, 2008, 27). The design for the park was carried out by Frederick Ashmead, the Sanitary Authority Surveyor (ibid, 28). Between 1889 and 1892 paths were laid out and a bandstand erected.

There is a large lake on the north side of the park (centred on ST 61597 75451). This was created in 1908-9 and was excavated by unemployed men contracted by the Distress Committee's Labour Bureau (Hillary Taylor Landscape Associates, 2008, 30).

During the Second World War a barrage balloon was tethered in the park necessitating the removal of the bandstand and a searchlight cluster (3228M) was created close to the site of the existing play area.

The park is of some importance as the largest of Bristol's nineteenth-century municipal parks (Avon Gardens Trust 1991, 18; Lambert 2000, 40-44).

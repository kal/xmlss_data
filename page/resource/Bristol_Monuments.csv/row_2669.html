monument_desc Brandon Hill Fort, located at the summit of Brandon Hill. The fort was constructed in 1642 as part of the line of defences protecting the northern side of Bristol during the civil war of 1642-1646. The Royalist engineer Sir Bernard de Gomme noted in 1643 that the fort was "some 18 foote square, & as many high; its Graff or mote but shallow & narrowe, by reason of the rockynesse of the grownd" (Firth 1925, 184). The survey of cannon captured by the Royalist armies in July 1643 recorded three minions and one Robinet in the fort (BUAD 4094; Roy 1975, 261). The fort may have been reconstructed as part of the Royalist refortification of the defences in 1643-1644. It is constructed following the principles of trace italienne and is of Brandon Hill Grit. Overall it appears originally to have measured approximately 45 metres by 30 metres.
The fort, along with the other surviving civil war defences on Brandon Hill, is a Scheduled Ancient Monument (AV 118).

monument_desc The hospital of St Lawrence. According to William Worcester the hospital of St. Lawrence stood 1200 paces (approximately 650 metres) from Lawford's Gate (Neale 2000, 109). The exact position of the site is not known but plots on the east and west sides of the junction of Lawrence Hill and Barrow Lane are possible, although the triangular plot at the junction of Lawrence Hill and Easton Road has also been suggested.
The hospital was founded by John, the Earl of Mortain who, as king, confirmed the foundation in 1208. The Crown retained the right to appoint the master until the early-fifteenth century. Responsibility for the hospital was given to the Dean and Chapter of Westbury College, Westbury-on-Trym, in 1465. The College paid a salary of £2 for a priest to celebrate mass in the chapel and 16s. to support four almoners. The form of the hospital is uncertain. William Worcestre mentions "the church, and the house and hospital attached to the said church" (Neale 2000, 109), but gives few other details.
The hospital was surrendered as part of the property of Westbury College on 18 February 1544 (Graham 1907, 119). The site and all of the hospital's possessions were acquired by Sir Ralph Sadleir in March 1544 as part of his purchase of the lands of Westbury College (Gairdner & Brodie 1903, 175).

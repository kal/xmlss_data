monument_desc The Society of Friends meeting house, located in an alley off the west side of Temple Street. The meeting house was constructed in 1667 to serve the Rosemary Street meeting. It was converted to use as a cholera hospital in 1832 but this use ended c.1834. The building became a Wesleyan Methodist chapel in the later 1830s and in 1840 was bought by Bristol's Jewish community. It was reconstructed as a synagogue, with the addition of a cupola and a colonnade. A further renovation took place in 1857 (Samuel 1997, 70-71). The building was sold in 1868 to enable the construction of Victoria Street and subsequently demolished. Its site is now largely beneath Victoria Street.

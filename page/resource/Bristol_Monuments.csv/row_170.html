monument_desc Redcliffe Poor House, located on the north side of Commercial Road. The almshouse was built in 1804 by the Bristol docks company as part of the construction of the New Cut "under the powers of their act, and at their own expense" (Manchee 1831, 57-58) and replaced the original poor house on the west side of Redcliff Hill (BUAD 983M). The almshouse appears to have been managed by the vestry of St. Mary Redcliffe and supported using parish funds.
The building was still extant during the First World War but was subsequently demolished.

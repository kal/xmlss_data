monument_desc No.19a Orchard Street, also known as Orchard Villa and Orchard Chambers. The building is located at the north-eastern end of Orchard Street and is a two-storey house with a cellar of early nineteenth-century date. The building stands in an elevated position with its own railed yard reached by steps from Orchard Street to the front. The main, north-west facing, elevation is rendered and has a central entrance surrounded by an ashlar moulding with a single bay on either side, reflecting a simple plan with a room on either side of the entrance hall. There is a full-height bay window to the south of the doorway with an ashlar base and sills. It contains a central three-by-four pane sash and narrow two-by-four pane sashes in the sides. The window to the north of the door is also a three-by-four pane sash. Ground is divided from first-floor by an ashlar string course and there was a parapet above an ashlar cornice. Internally several early nineteenth-century features survive including chimneypieces in most of the ground- and first-floor rooms, although the main ground-floor room was redecorated around the end of the nineteenth century and the cornice and chimneypiece are of that date. The newel posts of the stair, which is located at the northern corner of the building, also appears to date to the early nineteenth century. Extensions have been added to the rear of the building by knocking through the rear wall of the building. At the south corner of the building there was a strongroom constructed of brick with a steel door made by Gardiners and possibly dating to the same time as the decoration of the main ground-floor room. Access to the cellar is by steps from a door adjacent to the stair. The cellar walls are of stone rubble with a brick vault in the eastern part.
In 1875 the building known as Orchard Villa and was recorded in the street directory as being occupied by Henry M. Morrish.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/15/154). Photographs of the building are held in the SMR collection of Ground-based photographs (nos.1612-1634 inclusive).

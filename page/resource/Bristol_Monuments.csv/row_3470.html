monument_desc House on the south side of Waterloo Road. The building lies on the eastern side of the junction of Waterloo Road with a lane from the south-west. It was probably constructed in the early eighteenth century and is recorded by Jean Rocque's 1742 map which shows it as a rectangular structure, measuring approximately 9.5 metres from south-east to north-west by 5 metres wide. There was a rectangular formal garden on its eastern side. The structure had been extended on its eastern side by 1828, having been enlarged by one metre along the entire length of its eastern side. An extension, forming a south-west to north-east range 11.5 metres long on the Waterloo Road frontage, was built into the area of the garden. The Goad fire insurance plan of 1896 records that earliest element of the house was of three storeys in height while the extension was of two storeys. The building survived into the late 1960s but was subsequently demolished and the site redeveloped.

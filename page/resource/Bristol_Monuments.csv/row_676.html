monument_desc No.124 Temple Street. The building was an attached house of three storeys and attic with a gabled, jettied, front. The main entrance was to right, with a winged porch, each side with a pair of trefoil-headed lancet openings beneath a square head, and projecting forward to provide support for the jetty above. Two sash windows with shutters, behind a wooden railing with pyramid-capped posts; bracket at left end. First-floor with much timber-framing evident, all except left-hand panel glazed, and with right return glazed below bracket to second-floor; another bracket at left end. Bay projecting beyond second-floor jetty, transomed, 8 lights to front, 2 to side; some glazing beside bay; bracket at right end. Attic flush with second-floor, but with blocked window. There were two purlins on the south side of roof. The porch is not unlike that of Spicer's Hall. The date of the building is unknown although the brackets of the portch suggest that it may have been of fifteenth-century date, and was refenestrated later.

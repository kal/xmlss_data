monument_desc The Metropole Cinema on the north side of Ashley Road. The cinema opened in 1913. It was extensively remodelled by Hyatt and Neale, Bristol to a design by W.H. Watkins for George Allen of the Eastville Hippodrome Company in 1938 (Ewins 1984, 29) and reopened as the Metropole de Luxe with 1460 seats (Anderson 1983, 36). The Metropole de Luxe had shops either side of the brick tower entrance and made extensive use of coloured lights as the Bristol Evening Post of 7th October 1935 reported, "Another notable piece of equipment is the electrical arrangement for diffusing lights of all the colours of the rainbow. Alluring light effects are obtainable by the infinite variety of colours which the equipment can present. They are particularly charming in the contrast or harmony with the decorative scheme of the cinema itself," (Ewins 1984, 22).
The building was converted into a bingo hall in 1968 and then became a cinema again showing Indian films (Ewins 1984, 29).
The building was demolished in 1990 and is now the site of Ashley Court flats.

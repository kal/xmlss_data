monument_desc Statue of Charles Wesley, located on the south side of The Horsefair in a courtyard between The Horsefair and the New Room (BUAD 256M). The work is a standing figure of Samuel Wesley on a square ashlar pedestal of Hornton stone and was donated by Sir Arthur Sunderland, a Newcastle shipping magnate and then the owner of Aston Martin. It was sculpted by F. Brook Hitch in 1939. Carved into the north face of the plinth is the legend "CHARLES WESLEY / 1707 - 1787 / O LET ME COMMEND / MY SAVIOUR TO YOU" taken from Charles Wesley's hymn Thy Faithfulness, Lord, Each Moment We Find. The statue was unveiled by Sutherland's daughter on 3 June 1939 (Merritt 2002, 45).
The statue has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/40/484).

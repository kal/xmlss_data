monument_desc The 'Crown' or 'Old Crown' tavern, on Broad Weir, demolished in 1906. It was reported that the workmen found "portions of a carved chimney-piece in pulling down the principal room", but that all ancient decorations "had long since gone". Jettied front. Carved 17th.century post on ground-floor at west end. Bracket to second-floor jetty at west end; east end bracket gone by 1901. Large lamp below centre of second-floor jetty. Gabled roof running east-west, with stacks at ends; pair of gables with decorative bargeboards on front elevation, slightly extending beyond line of second-floor. Horizontal sliding sashes to both windows on first and second floors. Advertised as having a 'bowling saloon' on sign. Its replacement was removed in 1957.

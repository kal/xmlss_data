monument_desc No.21 Castle Street, located on the north side of Castle Street. The building was a jettied, gabled house of three storeys and attic with a 1-window range. It was almost certainly constructed as part of the redevelopment of the site of Bristol Castle after its demolition in the late 1650s.
By the nineteenth century the ground floor was a shop. Shop windows with a central, flush, entrance, possibly with a 2-leaf door. One canted bay each to first and second floors, sashes. A single attic window with a pivotting pane. Fretted bargeboards. Lateral stack(s) on east side.
The building was destroyed by bombing on 24 November 1940.

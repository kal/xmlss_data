monument_desc A pond located on the west side of West Town Grove. The pond was in existence by the time of the Brislington tithe survey of 1846 and the form of the field to the south suggests that the pond may originally have been considerably larger. The pond was still extant in the early 1880s and the first edition Ordnance Survey plan (Gloucestershire Sheet LXXVI.06) records it as a fishpond. At that date the feature measured 65 metres north-south by 42 metres east-west.
Dennison and Iles interpreted the features as a fishponds with a likely medieval origin (Dennison and Iles 1985, 43). However, cartographic evidence suggests that the pond itself formed part of a larger system of water management linked to Brislington Brook and records a building (SMR 4249) at the northern end of the pond in the 1840s. The function of this building is not clear. However, an alternative interpretation is that the pond may have been a mill pond.

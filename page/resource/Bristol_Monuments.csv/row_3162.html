monument_desc No.16, Victoria Street. Early 1870s. Brick and terracotta dressings, brick stacks and slate mansard roof. Double-depth plan. Two storeys and attic; one-window range. 20th.century shop front, with haulingway entrance on right; haulingway ran much of the plot depth. Deep cornice and parapet; a large 2-light window in a semicircular arch and a tympanum with a lion head and COURAGE above it; semicircular-arched dormer, 2 lights and a round light above. Rear elevation of 3 bays. Behind the main building, a link to a 2-storey brick building, then a single-storey gabled block. Lift (1-ton capacity) to cellar. Part of a group of Victorian commercial buildings developed after Victoria Street was cut. Latterly a bar, but earlier a wine merchant's premises.

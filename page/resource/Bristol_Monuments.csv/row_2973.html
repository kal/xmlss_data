monument_desc Brandy Cask Hotel, No.79 Broad Quay, located on the north side of the junction of Broad Quay and Alderskey Lane. The site was created by the infilling of Aldsworth's Dock in the mid-seventeenth century and a building is recorded on the site by Millerd's 1673 map of Bristol.
As built, the structure was a double-gabled house of two storeys plus attic with a pantile roof. The west-facing, Broad Quay, elevation measured 11.4 metres from north to south while the south-facing, Alderskey Lane, elevation measured 9.2 metres east-west.
The building was apparently refronted in the late-eighteenth or early-nineteenth century (Winstone 1960, pl.63) and the roof altered to a hip. In the west-facing elevation it had an entrance door at the south end with an adjacent shop window, while to the north of this were two sash windows, one against the party wall with No.77 and between them a plain door opening. Adjacent to the north of the door was an arched cellar access with a cellar drop in the footway in front of the building. A string course separated ground and first floor and the first-floor fenestration comprised sashes, the two on the north side of the elevation being slightly larger than the rest. At the south corner of the building there was a large ornate lamp supported on brackets and projecting over the footway. There were four six over six pane sash windows at second floor and these were separated from the third floor by a string course. At third floor level the fenestration consisted of four three over three pane sashes. The dentils beneath the eaves continued from No.77, suggesting that the two buildings were refronted at the same time.
The street directory for 1882 records the building as a public house, The Bell, and the licensee was then James Blake. The name had been changed by the 1980s to the Brandy Cask Hotel.
The building was apparently demolished soon after the closure of Alderskey Lane in 1900 (Pritchard 1904, 128-129) and the site was subsequently redeveloped for the Co-operative Wholesale building.

monument_desc Bristol Grammar School. Incorporating some of the buildings of St. Bartholomew's Hospital, reputedly founded 1240 by Sir John le Warre. Bristol Grammar School here 1532-1767, Queen Elizabeth Hospital School 1767-1847.
By the 1820s the school comprised 4 main buildings. Entering through the archway beneath No.18, Christmas Street, there was a passage leading to the playground in a courtyard at the rear, against Johnny Ball Lane. On the left of the passage, immediately behind No.17 Christmas Street, was a 3-storey range of possible C17 date, with a number of mullion and transom windows in the south-west side, overlooking a narrow yard cut into the hillside; there were 4 trefoil-headed windows, possibly re-used medieval ones. Continuing the line of this range, and reaching towards the north-west corner of the site, was one of the hospital blocks, 2-storey, largely refenestrated, but including in its north-east elevation a first-floor window with Y-tracery. At right-angles to these ranges was a 3 storey 5-window late C17 range with a cornice and parapet, a central elliptical-arched doorway, cross windows with label moulds, the right-hand range separated by a pilaster strip, with a right-hand open elliptical arch, 2-centred arched first-floor window with Y tracery, and second-floor quatrefoil window. There was a 1-window right-hand return between the 2 ranges previously described, the ground-floor with an arched doorway and 2 small windows. At the north-east end of this range was a further building, running northwards to the corner of Johnny Ball Lane, built in the 1810s, a 3-storeys and 4-window range, an open arcade of four-centred arches facing the playground, cross-windows above.
The range on the south-east side of the playground largely survives; that to its north was demolished in c.1981. The south-west elevation of the range behind No.17 Christmas Street has also been preserved in the modern office development. The medieval range was rebuilt in the C19, and parts also survive.

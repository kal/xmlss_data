monument_desc A house of probable seventeenth century date which stood a few metres to the west of the transept of Bristol Cathedral, fronting on to the south side of College Green. The house was of two storeys plus attic with an entrance is on the west side of the facade. Each of the two bays had sash windows. The cross-gable roof was pantiled and there was a chimney stack on the end of the northern gable on College Green and another on the north side of the west gable. Attached to the west side of the building was a smaller, two-storey cross-gabled ?gazebo, also with a pantile roof.
The house is recorded in a painting of 1791 by J. M. W. Turner was extant still extant in 1828 but had been demolished by the time of Ashmead's map of the city of 1855.
The southern part of the building extends beneath the north aisle of the Victorian nave of Bristol Cathedral, but the northern part lies in the grassed area on the north side of the Cathedral. Although the area has been landscaped and disturbed by services, survival of the sub-surface deposits may be expected to be good.

monument_desc The inner circuit town wall, defending the medieval town of Bristol. The circuit was constructed in the Norman period and documentary evidence indicates that it was in existence by the mid-1140s. The wall comprised a tall rubble wall of Brandon Hill grit which was approximately 2 metres thick and there appear to have been a number of semi-circular bastions or interval towers, although only two have so far been identified. Inside the wall were narrow intra-mural lanes - St. Nicholas Street, Leonard Lane, Bell Lane and Tower Lane - which followed its line. A series of town gates, St. Nicholas Gate (BUAD 283M), St. Leonard's Gate (BUAD 1207M), St. Giles's Gate (BUAD 930M), Blind Gate (BUAD 389M), Pithay Gate (BUAD 1209M) and Old Gate (BUAD 34M), controlled movement on the roads leading into the town. There were also several wall churches - St. Nicholas, St. Leonard's, St. Giles, St. Lawrence, and St. John - which were presumably contemporary with the wall. These were either above or were attached to the gates. Of the wall churches, St. Nicholas Church (BUAD 147M) and St. John's Church (BUAD 145M) are extant.
The wall has been archaeologically investigated on several occasions. The antiquarian Samuel Seyer noted elements of the wall in a number of loactions in the 1820s and a long section in Tower Lane was observed by John Pritchard during the construction of the Fry's Factory in Pithay and reported in the Transactions of the Bristol and Gloucestershire Archaeological Society between 1900 an 1910. A standing section of the wall to the rear of Nos.41-43 Baldwin Street was surveyed by Philip Rahtz for the Ministry of Public Building and Works in 1957 (BUAD 484) and an area excavation (BUAD 1503) was simultaneously undertaken in front of it (Rahtz 1960). A further section of the wall was excavated (BUAD 523) to the north-east at No.65 Baldwin Street by Roger Price for the city of Bristol Museum and Art Gallery in 1974 (Thomas 1979, 15-27).
The extant section of the wall to the rear of Nos.41-43 Baldwin Street is a Scheduled Ancient Monument (AV 117). St. Nicholas Church has a Grade II* Listing (Listed Building number 901-1/16/662) and St. John's Church has a Grade I listing (Listed Building number 901-1/11/536).

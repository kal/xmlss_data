monument_desc Church Hill House, Church Hill, Brislington. Situated at the end of Church Hill Has also been called St. Luke's Vicarage. The house was built in the Palladian style by Charles Vaughan, and in 1704, his widow sold the property to Jeremiah Paerce. By 1751 the property had been purchased by John Newman, who had also purchased Hill Cottage (SMR 20575. By 1828, it was part of the Clayfield-Ireland estate. The Brislington Tithe Survey of 1846 (BRO EP/A/32/9) records Isaac Cooke as the owner, trustee under the will of James Ireland. In the early nineteenth century, the house was a boarding school known as Church Hill House Academy. It remained a school until 1856 when Major Robert Bush became the occupier. In 1896, James Clayfield-Ireland presented the house to the parish as a vicarage. It remained the vicarage until 1981 when the house was sold to be converted into flats and a new vicarage was built. Pevsner describes the house as the vicarage and writies "a specially pretty early Georgian house rendered, five bays, two storeys, slightly projecting centre bay with pediment, and oval window in the pediment. The middle window on the first floor is of the venetian type" (Pevsner 1958, 465). This Venetian window belonged to a first floor room but was moved during alterations to the property in the 1930s. Other alterations included the moving of the staircase.

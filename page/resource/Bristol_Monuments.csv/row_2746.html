monument_desc The town gate known as St. John's Gate, which forms the west end of the Church of St. John Baptist in Broad Street. The tower of the church stands over the gate. The gate stands as part of the earliest circuit of the town wall and is the only survivor of the gates of the town. The structure was renovated in the nineteenth-century when two smaller arches were inserted on either side of the main gateway.
The north elevation of the gateway has a tall moulded arch of fourteenth-century date with a crenellated parapet. On either side are two arches, the east one under the end of the nave, the west beneath a parapeted wall.
The south side of the central gateway has flanking niches with restored hoods and painted statues of Brennus and Bellinus, the mythical founders of Bristol, each holding an orb and sceptre; There are shields and a Royal Arms on the parapet above.
The main gateway has a ribbed fan vault with bell hole, the side gateways a ridge rib and drips each side.
The gateway has a Grade I listing and is listed together with the Church of St. John the Baptist.

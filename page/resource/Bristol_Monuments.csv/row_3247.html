monument_desc Grand Hotel, located on the northern side of Broad Street. The hotel was constructed between 1865 and 1869 on the site of the White Hart inn (BUAD 628M) and the White Lion Inn (BUAD 629M) and Nos.54 (BUAD 630M) and 55 (BUAD 631M) Broad Street. It was designed in an Italianate Renaissance style by the architectural practice of Foster and Wood and the building is of five-storeys and attic with a double basement. It is faced with limestone ashlar
As designed the Broad Street frontage at ground-floor was occupied by shops with rectangular rooflights, and the area behind accommodated the service functions of the hotel with substantial brick vaulted arches supporting the upper floors. Carriage access was from the south-eastern return to stables at the rear, which included a carriage turntable.
The public entrance was at the centre of the façade and visitors walked up a flight of steps to the first-floor lobby. On the north-western side of the stair at the front of the building was a conference hall and an office on its south-east side. The lobby extended along the centre of the building and there was a large sale room at its northern end. On the north-west sideof the lobby was a bar and restaurant, and on its south-east side a club room with a large bay overlooking the Christ Church churchyard on the south side of the building and another bar.
The entrance was relocated slightly to the north-west in the early twentieth century and the interior was also extensively altered later in the century. While the front element of the building dates to the 1860s, some of the fabric towards the rear may be reused from earlier structures. A fourteenth-century well survives in the basement towards the rear of the building.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/11/537).

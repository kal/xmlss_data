monument_desc Bishopsworth Lodge or Bishport Lodge. Buildings on the site are first recorded by a survey of the parish of Bedminster in 1828 (BRO 32875/AC/PL/107/1). The date of the building of the lodge is unknown but the property is named as Bishport Lodge by 1832. The Lodge was located at the north side of the plot with an outbuilding to the south-west in 1841, according to the Bedminster tithe map, and one to the west by the late 1940s. A small pond lay to the east of the earliest outbuilding in the late 1940s. Air photographic evidence indicates that the Lodge had been demolished to make way for the surrounding housing development by c.1963 (BRO Picture Box 1/35 (1 & 2)). The site was subsequently landscaped to form the grounds of Elm Hays Elderly Persons Home.
A watching brief (SMR 20282) during redevelopment of the site for housing found little evidence for the Lodge and its outbuildings other than part of the foundation of a limestone rubble wall 0.6 metres wide and bonded in a white lime mortar. This was likely to have been part of the outbuilding indicated by the Bedminster tithe map. It was apparent that post-war building and landscaping had seriously affected the survival of archaeological stratification on the site and little of the lodge may be expected to survive (Brett 1995; Hume 1998).

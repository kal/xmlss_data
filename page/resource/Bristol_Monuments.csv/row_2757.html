monument_desc Ashton Gate Brewery, located on the south side North Street, Ashton Gate. The brewery was founded in the late 1820s and was recorded by a street directory of 1829 as "Baynton, Thomas, Charles & John, Brewers, Somerset Brewery, near Ashton gate, Bedminster." Thomas Baynton alone was listed as the operator from 1834. By the mid-nineteenth century the brewery consisted of a large main building on North Street with a narrower range of buildings to the rear. Immediately to the souh-west of this range there was a trapezoidal pond measuring approximately 35 metres long by 18 metres wide.
The brewery was sold to a new registered company, the "Ashton Gate Brewery Co. Ltd." in 1865 and a number of new buildings were constructed. The company changed its name to "Hardwick & Co." between 1868 and 1883 but subsequently reverted to Ashton Gate Brewery. More expansion projects were undertaken including the construction of a new fermenting house in 1903 by George Adlams and Sons, local brewery engineers. In 1909 plans were produced for a new single-storey cellar brick cellar which was built alongside the fermenting house. This had a queen post roof with a lantern running along the centre. The Ashton Gate Brewery was purchased by George's and Co. in 1932 and closed down.

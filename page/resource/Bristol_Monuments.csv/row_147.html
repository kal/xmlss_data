monument_desc No.27 St. Michael's Hill, located on the western side of St. Michael's Hill. The building is an early seventeenth-century jettied, gabled house of two storeys and attic. The ground floor has a single central sash window with the main entrance door on the northern side of the elevation. The first floor is jettied and at the centre of the elevation there is a projecting bay containing a sash. There is also a projecting bay in the centre of the attic gable.
The house was demised to Philip Bowcher the younger by his cousin Humphrey Andrewes in 1637 and during the 1660s it was apparently occupied by Richard Cox. In 1695 the house was occupied by Martha Jones, widow, and by a sequence of other individuals through the eighteenth century (Leech 2000, 74-75).

monument_desc The chapel of St. Brandon, on Brandon Hill. The date of foundation of the chapel is not known but it was in existence by c.1193 when the Bishop of Worcester granted it to the Priory of St. James and other churches (Hall Warren 1907, 203). A hermitage was attached to the chapel, apparently by 1351 when Lucy de Newchirche asked to retreat there (Hall Warren 1907, 203). In 1480 William Worcestre visited the chapel in 1480 and recorded that::
"Capelle longitudo sancti Brandane continet .8. Virgas cum dimidio
latitudo eius .5. virgas, continet
Circitus muri capelle sancti Brandani continet .180. gressus" (Neale 2000, 182-183).
Leland visited the site in 1542 and described it as "now defacyd" and the building was apparently removed by the Corporation for the construction of a windmill in 1565 (Hall Warren 1907, 203). Part of the structure may have been found during excavation of foundations for Cabot Tower in 1898 (BUAD 2457) and certainly human remains representing at least three individuals were discovered, including a grave aligned east-west and lined with masonry (Fryer 1989). Most of the chapel seems likely to have been destroyed by later building.

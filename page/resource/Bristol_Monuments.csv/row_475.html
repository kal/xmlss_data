monument_desc The Water Lane pottery, located to the east of Temple Church, on the eastern side of Petticoat Lane. The pottery was founded around 1683 by Edward Ward and was handed through the Ward family until it was leased to Thomas Cantle in 1746. It was sold in March 1751 with Cantle as tenant and he remained in occupation until 1756. The pottery was then taken over by William Taylor until it seems to have been amalgamated with the next door Temple Back pottery by Richard Frank. As part of the Temple Back Pottery [BUAD 86M] it continued to work until 1886.

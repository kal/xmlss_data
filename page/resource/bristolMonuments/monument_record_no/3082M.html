monument_desc Zion Chapel, a Primitive Methodist chapel, located on the south-east side of Fishponds Road. The site was purchased for £150 and the chapel was designed by Rev. T. T. Shields. The foundation stone was laid by Mark Whitwell on 1 September 1874 and the building was constructed by the Bristol firm of Eastabrook & Sons. The opening ceremony was held on 28 March 1875.
The chapel was a two-storey structure constructed of Pennant sandstone blocks with freestone ashlar facings in a romanesque style. A Sunday school had been built to the rear of the chapel by 1904.
On 16 March 1941 a 50 kilogramme bomb landed in Wharf Road, killing eight people, destroying three houses and severely damaging both the chapel and Sunday school. The latter was demolished and the chapel re-roofed with asbestos sheets. Its public function ended and the building was sold to a local haberdashery firm, Deighton Brothers, in the early 1950s. The company shared the buildign with Ranarf Press and J. M; Smith Ltd., linen converters, by 1956. By 1972 Hilti, an art transfer company, occupied the building and it was subsequently acquired by the Governmental Community Industries scheme before being sold to Tollgate Hire Ltd. The chapel building was demolished in 1996.

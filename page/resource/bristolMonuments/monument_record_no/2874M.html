monument_desc Field House, located some 400 metres to the south-west of Heath House. The house was in existence by the late eighteenth century when it appears to have been recorded by a plan of the Heath House estate. At the time of the Stapleton tithe survey of 1839 (BRO EP/A/32/35) the building was owned by William Hillier Bailey and occupied by James Lorymer. The building survived into the twentieth century but had been demolished by 1946 (CPE/UK/2157 Frame 5132) and the site was subsequently developed for housing. The site of the building now lies mainly in the rear garden of No.25 Rousham Road.

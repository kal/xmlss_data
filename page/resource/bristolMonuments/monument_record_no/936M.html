monument_desc The Great Ditch of the town of Bristol. The ditch probably dates to the early post-Conquest or perhaps even late pre-Conquest period. A large ditch protecting the Old Market area of the town ran from the River Frome firstly east-south-east, then south-south-east through and beyond Lawford's Gate. It then turned to the south-west. It is not clear where the ditch terminated, but it should have extended to either the River Avon or the ditch of the King's Orchard. The ditch was recorded as the "magni fossati ville Bristollie" in the 1373 perambulation of the new County of Bristol (Harding 1930, 160; 163).
Jean Rocque's 1742 map shows a section of a wide ditch, about 150 feet (45 metres) in length, running south-eastwards from the southern bank of the Frome, immediately inside the 1373 County boundary (BUAD 2990; ST 59580 73363). The line of the ditch appears to have been fossilised in a continuous line of gardens to the rear of properties on the south-west side of New Street. It appears again on the west side of what is now Midland Road, between Trinity Hospital and the corner of Unity Street, as a 160 foot (49 metre) stretch of garden. The line lies on the north-east side of St. Matthias Park, crossing beneath it at its south-eastern end, then running on the west side of Lawford Street and Midland Road, before turning to run along the northern side of Unity Street.
Part of the ditch may have been located during a watching brief carried out by John Bryant in 1986 (BUAD 381) at the rear of No.49 Old Market Street (BUAD 901A), and possibly also earlier, in 1982, on Unity Street near the corner with Midland Road (BUAD 200A).

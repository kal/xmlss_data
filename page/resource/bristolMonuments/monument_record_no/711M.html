monument_desc Penitentiary or prison, located on the south side of Upper Maudlin Street. A seventeenth-century house of L-shaped plan with a later addition to the south-east. Garden, or south-east, elevations. Main house of 3-window range with a right-hand return of 1-window. 3 storeys and attics. 2 gables to main elevation, 1 to return, and 1 to south-east end of same. Entrance to right end of main wall, 2 wide sashes to left. Similar sashes to first-floor, but narrower example above entrance. 3 normal-size sashes to second-floor. 1 shorter sash to each gable. Right-hand return with possible ground-floor casement window, otherwise wider sashes to the next 2 floors, normal sash to attic. Extension block along part of north-east side of garden, 2 storeys and 3-window range, rubble foundations below a plinth, rendered main walls, left-hand entrance below segmental arch, small round-arched window above, 2 full-length round-arched sash windows with gothic tracery. At first-floor, 3 sashes. Roof not recorded.
Pump and cistern/sink at south corner of the seventeenth-century return. Level area in the angle between the two ranges, then the garden slopes down to a lower area. A south-west garden wall of rubble construction, with ashlar gateposts with ball finials near to its north-west end.
Partly later the site for the church of St. James-the-Less, consecrated in 1867.

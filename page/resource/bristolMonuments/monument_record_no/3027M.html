monument_desc Obelisk at Bowness Gardens, Southmead. In June 2002 Michael Fairfax was commissioned by the Bristol City Archaeologist, R. H. Jones, to produce an artwork to mark the site of the Bronze Age round barrow on the northern side of Bowness Gardens. Fairfax designed an obelisk, a pyramid of brushed stainless steel which tapered to a rounded point. The piece was 2.0 metres tall, 0.64 metres wide and 0.25 metres thick. The upper 0.25 metres of the obelisk is mirror-polished and beneath, on the south-east face, there is a circle comprising 23 mirror-polished lines which radiate from an indented central hole 30 millimetres in diameter. A text written by Fairfax laser-cut into the surface runs downwards from the circle and furls into a spiral towards the base. The text reads "At Badocks wood ghostly windmill sails turn and like a rewound film spin through history to remote times when this was a burial place for a bronze age warrior. In that past landscape wolves prowled and nervy deer grazed while wild hog rooted among trees."
The obelisk was installed on 19 February 2003, the commissioning and installation process costing £8000 in all. It was unveiled by Peter Hammond, the one of the Councillors for the Southmead Ward, on 20 February 2003.

monument_desc Brick Yard Pool, a clay pit located to the north-east of Avon Street. The clay pit apparently dates to the late-seventeenth or early-eighteenth century. It is recorded by Jean Rocque's 1742 map, where it is shown as about 200 metres in length. Its extent was defined by the western side of a lane (modern Barton Road), from the junction with a lane leading to Avon Street (the section later named Kilboar Street) as far north as the line of the later Louisa Street. The pit presumably provided the clay for the brickyard in Old Bread Street (BUAD 978M).

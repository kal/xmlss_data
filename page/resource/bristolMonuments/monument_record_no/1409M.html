monument_desc No.6 Denmark Street, located on the south side of Denmark Street. The building is a rendered house of three storeys, attic and basement with an M-shaped roof. It was constructed in the early 1720s as part of a terrace and No.6 itself was first recorded in the Land Tax assessment of 1728 under the name of a Captain Dunn (presumably a sailor), but was then unoccupied. A plan of c1743 recorded the property as being 20 feet [6.1 metres] wide and 40 feet [12.2 metres] deep (BRO 04479(1)).
The building is constructed of Brandon Hill grit bonded in a brown mortar.
The fireplace in the east wall is bonded in a red-orange mortar with white lime flecks. The fireplace is supported by two corbels in the east wall of the cellar. The building was substantially altered and the main elavation reconstructed in the nineteenth century. The main, north-facing, elevation is in an early nineteenth-century style and is of two bays with a small attic dormer window behind the parapet. At ground-floor the façade has a shopfront apparently of late-Victorian or Edwardian date and the main entrance is on its west side. A narrow pentice divides ground from first floor and the west side of the elevation is marked by a pilaster. The fenestration at first- and second-floor levels comprises sashes. The pitched roof is tiled and there is a rendered chimney stack at the east end of the roof.
The ground floor rooms have been knocked into one during the nineteenth or twentieth century. There are two rooms at first and second floor level. In both the northern, front, and the southern, rear, rooms late eighteenth- or early nineteenth-century chimneypieces survive in the east wall. Cornices which appeared to be contemporary also survived in the first-floor front room.
Access to the cellar is by a wooden stair on the west side of the building and its walls appeared to be constructed of stone. The front, north, wall appears to have been cut back in the centre to create a new and larger cellar entrance in the nineteenth century.
The building is listed as part of the terrace Nos.1-7 Denmark Street and has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/15/88). Photographs of the building are held in the SMR collection of Ground-based photographs (nos.1566-1596 inclusive).

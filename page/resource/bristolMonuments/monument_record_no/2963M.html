monument_desc War memorial located on the southern side of Stoke Hill. The memorial is a stone cross supported on a pillar with a square base. This is set on a stepped octagonal plinth. On the north-west face of the column there is a sword carved in relief. The north-west face of the base bears the inscription "IN GRATEFUL MEMORY/OF/STOKE BISHOP MEN WHO GAVE/THEIR LIVES IN THE GREAT WAR/1914-1918/"HE THAT DOETH THE WILL/OF GOD ABIDETH FOR EVER"[a quotation from the oratorio The Prodigal Son by Sir Arthur Sullivan]/"WHO STANDS IF FREEDOM FALL/WHO DIES IF ENGLAND LIVE" [a quotation from the poem "For All We Have and Are" by Rudyard Kipling]." The same face of the upper step of the plinth has the inscription "ALSO IN GRATEFUL MEMORY OF/THOSE WHO GAVE THEIR LIVES/IN THE SECOND WORLD WAR/1939-45." The names of the fallen are inscribed on the other faces of the plinth. A stone was added to the north-east face of the plinth to commemorate a soldier killed in Palestine in 1947 and Lieutenant Terence Edward Waters of The West Yorkshire Regiment (The Prince of Wales's Own), attached to The Gloucestershire Regiment, who died in Korea in 1951 and was posthumously awarded the George Cross.
[Public Monument and Sculpture Association reference number BR274]

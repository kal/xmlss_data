monument_desc Southmead Manor House, Southmead. The manor of Southmead was owned by the Nunnery of St. Mary Magdalene, located at the foot of St. Michael's Hill, in Bristol. The manor was possibly part of the property endowed on the monastery by its founder Eva Fitzhardinge in 1173. The manor house was extant by the early fourteenth-century. At dissolution in 1536 the manor was acquired by the Haynes family and then passed through several hands (Boore 1995, 5). It was acquired by Joshua James, a Bristol distiller, who made "great improvements" to the building according to Samuel Rudder in his history of Gloucestershire (Rudder 1779, 803). The Westbury tithe map of 1841 shows the manor house as an extensive range of buildings surrounded by outbuildings and with a large pond (possibly a former millpond) to the south-east.
The manor house and estate was bought by Dr. Stanley Badock in the late nineteenth-century. Badock laid out the gardens (SMR 20100). Southmead Road was created c.1903, dividing the Manor House from its outbuildings and the site of the pond; these now lie beneath, and on the south side of, the road. The manor house was sold again a few years later and rebuilt in 1912, although it is reported that several features were retained during the work (Boore 1995, 6). A sale document of 1926 refers to lawns, kitchen gardens, orchards, outbuildings, and a 70 foot long swimming pool (Boore 1995, 9). Boore reports that a "wet" cellar or possible ice-house lies beneath the entrance to the building, with access below the nursery doorway. The property was owned by the Courtney family from the late 1930s until the 1980s. The plot was then broken up and the gardens divorced from the main house. The present building is in use as a children's nursery (February 1995). It appears from the modern Ordnance Survey 1:1250 Landline plan that the northern buildings of the (?eighteenth-century) manor house were sited to the north of the present building, the site of No.149 Southmead Road.

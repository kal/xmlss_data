monument_desc Heath Farm, located on the northern side of Ironmould Lane. The farm is a two-storey gabled building constructed of stone rubble and dates to the seventeenth century. The original form appears to have been a single-depth plan with a room on either side of a central entrance and a projecting rear stair tower. The stone of the stair tower is bonded in an orange-red mortar. The internal organisation of the house has been substantially altered but major structural timbers with roughly-hewn stops survive. Some of the original roof timbers are also extant. A rear north-south range was added to the western side of the building in the late-eighteenth or the nineteenth century. Openings were made through the rear wall of the seventeenth-century building into this range and a lean-to extension on the east side of the stair tower.
The farmhouse has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/49/464).

monument_desc Blaise Manor, originally known as Henburyhill House, located on the east side of Henbury Road. The building dates to the late-eighteenth or early-nineteenth century and is a roughly square, three-storey structure with a double-depth plan and a hipped slate roof. The building is rendered with limestone ashlar dressings. The main, north-facing, elevation has a four-window range with a central four-storey stair tower with rusticated pilasters. The main entrance door is set in the ground floor of the stair tower, beneath a porch has a pair of square columns and entablature with a shallow pediment. On either side there are projecting single-storey D-shaped bay windows.
The north-, west- and south-facing elevations are all of three storeys with four-window ranges and a plat band beneath the first-floor windows, cornice and parapet. There are two-storey ranges on the eastern side of the building. The fenestration throughout consists of six-over-six pane sash windows.
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/21/2071).
